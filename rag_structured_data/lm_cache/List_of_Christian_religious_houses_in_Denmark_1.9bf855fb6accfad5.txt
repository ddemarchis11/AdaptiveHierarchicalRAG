Flensburg Friary housed Franciscan friars from 1263 until 1528, while Husum Friary’s Franciscans served 1494-1528. Guldholm Abbey began as a Benedictine monks’ house in 1190/91, but after the community transferred from St. Michael’s Abbey, Schleswig, it became Cistercian and moved to Ryd Abbey in 1210. Ryd or Rüde Abbey near Glücksburg then continued as Cistercian monks from 1210 to 1536. St. Michael’s itself, Schleswig’s Benedictine double monastery founded c. 1030, saw its monks removed for discipline c. 1190 and sent to establish Guldholm, leaving only Benedictine nuns until c. 1200. St. John’s Priory, also in Schleswig, sustained Benedictine nuns from c. 1200 to 1536. Mohrkirchen Hospital sheltered Hospital Brothers of St. Anthony from 1391 to 1535. Schleswig’s Dominican Friary served from 1239 to 1529, and its Franciscan Friary from 1232 to 1530.
