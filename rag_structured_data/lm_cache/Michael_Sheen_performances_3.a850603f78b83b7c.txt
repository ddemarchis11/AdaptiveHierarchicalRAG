Between 1993 and 1999 Michael Sheen voiced Claudio for the BBC’s 1993 Much Ado About Nothing, then narrated Naxos AudioBooks’ Crime and Punishment in 1994, the same year he appeared in BBC Radio 4’s Strangers on a Train and spoke Great Poets of the Romantic Age for Naxos. He continued as Naxos narrator for 1995’s The Idiot, The Picture of Dorian Gray and Dangerous Liaisons, playing Valmont in the latter, while on BBC Radio 4 he voiced Frank in Alaska and Jack in The Importance of Being Earnest. In 1996 he read The Old Testament and The Great Poets – John Keats for Naxos, followed by 1997 narrations of Poets of the Great War, Lady Windermere’s Fan as Lord Darlington, Romeo and Juliet as both Romeo and director, and The Great Poets – Samuel Taylor Coleridge, plus BBC Radio 4 roles as Gorse in The West Pier and narrator of A White Merc With Fins. He returned to Naxos in 1998 to narrate Walton’s Henry V and to BBC Radio 3 that year to play Paris in Troy, closing the decade by narrating the 1999 Naxos poetry anthology A Lovers Gift: From Him to Her.
