Saddle Peak rises 732 m (2,402 ft) on North Andaman Island, making it the loftiest summit in the Andaman & Nicobar Islands. Chandigarh’s highest ground is an unnamed 383 m (1,257 ft) point within its Capital Area, while neighbouring Dadra and Nagar Haveli tops out at 424 m (1,391 ft) on an unnamed hill near Khanvel. Diu Island’s unnamed highpoint reaches only 30 m (98 ft), matching the 30 m (98 ft) elevation of Puducherry’s Les Montagnes Rouges in the Red Hills. Delhi’s Ridge culminates at 319 m (1,047 ft) inside Fort Tughlaqabad. Far to the north, Nun Peak towers at 7,135 m (23,409 ft) in Jammu & Kashmir’s Western Himalayas, yet Ladakh’s Saltoro Kangri in the Karakoram Range surpasses it at 7,742 m (25,400 ft). Lakshadweep’s low coral terrain peaks at a mere 15 m (49 ft) on Agatti Island.
