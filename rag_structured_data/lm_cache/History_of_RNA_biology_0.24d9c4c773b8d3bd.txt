Sidney Altman and Thomas Cech shared the 1989 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for RNA work, while David Baltimore, Françoise Barré-Sinoussi, Elizabeth Blackburn, Sydney Brenner, Andrew Fire, Carol Greider, Robert Holley, François Jacob, H. Gobind Khorana, Craig Mello, Jacques Monod, Luc Montagnier and Marshall Nirenberg earned Nobel Prizes in Physiology or Medicine across 1962–2009. Renato Dulbecco joined Baltimore as a 1975 laureate, Walter Gilbert received the 1980 chemistry award, Aaron Klug the 1982 chemistry prize, and Roger Kornberg the 2006 chemistry honor. Francis Crick, born 1916 and deceased 2004, gained his 1962 Nobel early in the RNA chronicle.
