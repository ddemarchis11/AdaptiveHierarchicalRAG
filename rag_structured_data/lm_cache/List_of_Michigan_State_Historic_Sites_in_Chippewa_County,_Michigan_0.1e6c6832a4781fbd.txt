Allen House at 126 E. Water Street in Sault Ste. Marie became a Michigan State Historic Site on October 1, 1971, the same day the Old Union Carbide dock at the foot of Johnstone Street was honored as the Louis W. Hill site. Bishop Frederick Baraga’s house at 501 E. Water Street and the John Johnston House at 415 East Waters Street both received their historic status on February 19, 1958. The Bay City House Hotel, 501 East Portage, was listed March 22, 1983, while the Carnegie Library at 315 Armory Place joined the register January 8, 1981. Central Methodist Church, 111 East Spruce Street, gained recognition June 17, 1993, and the Church of Our Saviour, Friend of Children on North Shore Road of Sugar Island’s Payment Settlement was honored January 19, 1978. Elmwood, 435 E. Water Street, dates to September 25, 1956, and the Federal Building, 209 East Portage Avenue, to December 14, 1976. Birch Lodge on 21830 S. Birch Lodge Drive in Trout Lake was listed November 20, 1987. Fort Brady, bounded by C.O.E. Service Plaza, Portage, Brady, and Bingham streets, and Fort Colyer on Drummond Island’s Whitney Bay, trace to August 23 and November 27, 1956. Fort Repentigny and Governor Cass and the Indians informational designations at Park Place and River Street and Brady Field both date to February 18, 1956. Johnston Homestead on Neebish Island near Barbeau was listed April 4, 1975. Lake Superior State College at West Easterday and Meridian roads, Larke Road at Three Mile Road, Legends of Hiawatha at Tahquamenon Falls State Park in Whitefish Township, and the Emerson Informational Site along M-123 near Paradise followed in 1975, 1982, 1959, and 1979.
