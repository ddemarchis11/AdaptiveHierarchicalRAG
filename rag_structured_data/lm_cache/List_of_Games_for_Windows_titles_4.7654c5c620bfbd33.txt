Kalypso Media released 3D Hunting 2010 on 20 April 2010, while Funcom shipped Age of Conan: Rise of the Godslayer on 11 May 2010; neither title supports LIVE or Games on Demand. Rebellion Developments’ Aliens vs. Predator launched on 16 February 2010 under Sega, likewise eschewing both services, whereas Obsidian Entertainment’s Alpha Protocol, published by Sega on 1 June 2010, is available only via Games on Demand. Viva Media’s Alter Ego arrived on 3 August 2010 without either feature, but Rocksteady Studios’ Batman: Arkham Asylum Game of the Year Edition, issued by Eidos Interactive on 11 May 2010, carries both LIVE and Games on Demand badges. 2K Marin’s BioShock 2, released by 2K Games on 9 February 2010, supports both services; its Russian edition, published by 1C on 18 June 2010, keeps LIVE but drops Games on Demand. Zombie Studios’ Blacklight: Tango Down, UTV Ignition’s 14 July 2010 release, and Arc System Works’ BlazBlue: Calamity Trigger, launched on 2 September 2010, both embrace LIVE and Games on Demand. Singapore-MIT GAMBIT Game Lab’s CarneyVale: Showtime, Synetic’s Crash Time 4: The Syndicate, Airtight’s Dark Void, Capcom Vancouver’s Dead Rising 2, Giants Software’s Demolition Company, Akella’s Disciples III: Renaissance, Stardock’s Elemental: War of Magic, Creative Assembly’s Empire & Napoleon Total War GOTY, Codemasters’ F1 2010, and Obsidian’s Fallout: New Vegas round out 2010’s Games for Windows roster with varying LIVE and Games on Demand support.
