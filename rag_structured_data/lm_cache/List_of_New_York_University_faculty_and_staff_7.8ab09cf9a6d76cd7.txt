NYU’s professorial ranks include former Caltech provost Steven E. Koonin, ex-Cornell president Jeffrey S. Lehman, Swarthmore’s onetime president Alfred Bloom, Rice’s seventh president David Leebron, and Princeton’s sitting twentieth president Christopher L. Eisgruber. Treasury Secretary Jack Lew, Pulitzer poet Yusef Komunyakaa, Fields medalist Martin Hairer, Turing laureate Frances E. Allen, and Abel winner Louis Nirenberg all teach there. Anthropologist Thomas A. Abercrombie holds a 2004-2005 Guggenheim, while emeritus computer scientist Martin Davis co-invented the Davis-Putnam and DPLL algorithms and framed Post-Turing machines. International-law scholar Philip Alston, the UN Special Rapporteur on Extrajudicial Executions, occupies NYU’s John Norton Pomeroy chair, and illusionist David Copperfield has offered a class on magic. Olympic luger Cammy Myler, 1985-1998 national-team veteran of four Winter Games, is on faculty; finance professor Edward Altman, hired in 1977, devised the Altman Z-Score. Poet-in-residence Yehuda Amichai collected the 1969 Brenner, 1976 Bialik, and 1982 Israel prizes. Dramatist Awam Amkpa directs NYU’s Africana studies, bioethicist Jacob M. Appel serves as visiting faculty, and Roger S. Bagnall, director of NYU’s Institute for the Study of the Ancient World, visits as professor.
