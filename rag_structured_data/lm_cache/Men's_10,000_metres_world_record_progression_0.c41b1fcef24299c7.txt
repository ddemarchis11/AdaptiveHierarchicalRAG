Gertrud Schmidt opened 1966 with 39:25.0, only to be overtaken weeks later by compatriot Hannelore Middeke’s 39:10.0. Ann O’Brien trimmed the Irish mark to 38:06.4 on 26 March 1967 in Gormanstown. Paola Pigni sliced over two minutes off in Milan on 9 May 1970, stopping the clock at 35:30.5. Kathy Gibbons lowered the U.S. record to 34:51.0 in Phoenix on 12 June 1971, before Julie Brown edged back to 35:00.4 in Los Angeles on 29 March 1975. Christa Vahlensieck then pushed the West German best to 34:01.4 in Wolfsburg on 20 August 1975. Loa Olafsson first brought Denmark into the spotlight with 33:34.2 in Hvidovre on 19 March 1977, only for Peg Neppel to take the U.S. record to 33:15.09 in Los Angeles on 9 June 1977. Natalia Mărăşescu raised the Romanian standard to 32:43.2 at Băile Felix on 22 January 1978, yet Olafsson reclaimed the Danish mark at 32:45.40 in Copenhagen on 6 April 1978. Olga Krentser closed the pre-IAAF era at 32:30.80 in Moscow on 7 August 1981.
