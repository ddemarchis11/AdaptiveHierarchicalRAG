Maccabi Los Angeles and Bethlehem Steel each own five U.S. Open Cup titles, Maccabi’s last coming in 1981 and Bethlehem’s in 1926, with Maccabi finishing second in 1982 and Bethlehem in 1918. Chicago Fire, Greek American AA, Philadelphia Tridents, Seattle Sounders FC, Fall River Marksmen and Sporting Kansas City follow with four wins apiece: Chicago last lifted the trophy in 2006 and were runners-up in 2011, Greek American AA in 1967 and 1989, Philadelphia Tridents in 1966 and 1964, Seattle in 2014 and 2012, while Fall River and Sporting Kansas City have never lost a final, their fourth triumphs coming in 1931 and 2017 respectively. Stix, Baer and Fuller collected three titles, the last in 1935, and were beaten in the 1937 final. New York Pancyprian-Freedoms also have three wins, their most recent in 1982, without ever finishing second. D.C. United’s three victories culminated in 2013 after losing the 2009 final. Brooklyn Italians earned back-to-back wins, their second in 1991 after being runners-up in 1990. Brooklyn Hispano secured two titles, the last in 1944, without ever finishing second. Los Angeles Galaxy and FC Dallas each have two wins, Galaxy’s in 2005 after losing the 2006 final, Dallas in 2016 after falling in 2007.
