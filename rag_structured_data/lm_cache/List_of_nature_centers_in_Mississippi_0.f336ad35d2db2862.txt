The Box Environmental Education Center in Brooksville, Noxubee County, sits on the 48,000-acre Sam D. Hamilton Noxubee National Wildlife Refuge and is run by the Starkville School District. Clinton’s 33-acre Clinton Community Nature Center serves Hinds County from the Southwest region. Over in Jackson County’s Southeast corner, the 18,400-acre Grand Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve near Moss Point hosts the Grand Bay Coastal Resources Center Interpretative Area, while the same city also offers the Pascagoula River Audubon Center managed by the National Audubon Society and the City-run Scranton Nature Center inside IG Levy Park. Jackson itself is home to the Mississippi Museum of Natural Science, a Southwest-region natural-history museum with aquarium displays and 2.5 miles of trails tucked within LeFleur’s Bluff State Park. Columbus hosts the Plymouth Bluff Center, an outdoor-education facility run by Mississippi University for Women with its own museum on the Lowndes County landscape of East Central Mississippi. North of them all, the Strawberry Plains Audubon Center spreads across 2,500 Holly Springs acres under National Audubon Society care and showcases a restored 1850s mansion.
