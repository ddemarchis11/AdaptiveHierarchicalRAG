Baikonur in Kazakhstan led 2008’s launch tally with 19 missions, 18 fully successful and one partial failure, while Russia’s Plesetsk added six flawless flights and Dombarovsky and Kapustin Yar each contributed one success. The United States split its 11 launches between Cape Canaveral (3), Kennedy (4) and Vandenberg (4), every one rated a success. France’s Kourou site sent up six rockets, all successful, and the Ocean Odyssey platform, flagged International, logged five successes. China conducted 11 launches across Jiuquan (3), Taiyuan (4) and Xichang (4) without a single failure. Japan’s Tanegashima flew once, successfully, and India’s Satish Dhawan matched that perfect record with three launches. Iran’s sole attempt from Semnan ended in failure, while Kwajalein Atoll in the Marshall Islands saw one failure among its four launches.
