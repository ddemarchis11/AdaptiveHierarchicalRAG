William Atwater, called in 1520, rose to become Bishop of Lincoln, while Stephen Gardiner, called in 1555, served as Bishop of Winchester. Richard Bancroft, called in 1588, and John Whitgift, called in 1592, both became Archbishops of Canterbury, as did Lancelot Andrewes, called in 1589, who first served as Bishop of Winchester. William Laud and Joseph Hall, both called in 1615, became Archbishop of Canterbury and Bishop of Exeter respectively. Thomas Morton, called in 1622, became Bishop of Lichfield, while James Ussher, called in 1623, served as Archbishop of Armagh and Primate of All Ireland. William Juxon, called in 1635, also became Archbishop of Canterbury, followed by Gilbert Sheldon, called in 1663, who likewise attained that position.
