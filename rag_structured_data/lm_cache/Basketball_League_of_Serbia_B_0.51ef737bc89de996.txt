Beovuk 72 and Radnički Beograd both call Belgrade home yet split the 2,000-seat SC Šumice, while Zemun Fitofarmacija stakes out the capital’s 5,000-capacity Pinki Hall. In Niš, Fair Play plays at Dušan Radović School Hall and Konstantin commands the 4,000-seat Čair Sports Center. Mladost SP occupies the 500-seat Vuk Karadžić School Hall in Smederevska Palanka, Pirot hosts matches in its 835-seat Pirot Kej Hall, and Proleter Naftagas fills Zrenjanin’s 3,000-seat Crystal Hall. Radnički Kragujevac packs Jezero Hall’s 3,750 seats, Sloga takes Kraljevo Sports Hall with 3,350, and Spartak brings 3,000 fans to Subotica’s SC Dudova Šuma. Sveti Đorđe of Žitište plays in Nikola Tesla School Hall, Zdravlje commands Leskovac’s 3,600-seat SRC Dubočica, and Železničar rules Čačak’s 4,000-seat Borac Hall.
