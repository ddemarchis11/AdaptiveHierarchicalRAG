Chatham Dockyard and its Defences in Kent, England, a 17th- to 19th-century naval complex, was listed in 2012 under UNESCO criteria ii and iv with reference 5670. Creswell Crags, the Derbyshire gorge where humans lived 60–15 thousand years ago, gained status the same year under criterion iii as entry 5671. Darwin’s Landscape Laboratory in Greater London, the 1842–1882 Down House environs where Charles Darwin refined evolution theory, was inscribed as 5672 under criteria iii and vi. Across the South Atlantic, the Island of St Helena, a natural site without specified date, entered the list as 5675 under criterion x. Mousa, Old Scatness and Jarlshof—the Zenith of Iron Age Shetland—preserve 4,000-year-old Scottish settlements and were added as 5677 under criteria iii and iv. Gwynedd’s Slate Industry of North Wales, active from the 18th to 20th centuries, became entry 5678 under criteria ii and v. Flow Country, the peat-rich Caithness and Sutherland wilderness, was listed as 5679 under natural criteria ix and x. The Twin Monastery of Wearmouth Jarrow, founded 672/3 and 681 in Tyne and Wear, joined as 5681 under criteria ii, iii, iv and vi. The Turks and Caicos Islands, a Caribbean natural site, were inscribed as 5682 under criterion x. Finally, Bath, Somerset’s part of the transnational Great Spas of Europe, spanning the 1st to 20th centuries, was added in 2014 as 5935 under criteria ii, iii, iv and vi.
