Algeria’s first-ever submission, Costa-Gavras’s French-language political thriller Z, won the 42nd Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film, making Algeria the first African country both nominated and victorious. France entered Éric Rohmer’s French conversation piece My Night at Maud’s and secured a nomination, while Sweden, the USSR and Yugoslavia also reached the five-film shortlist. Among the also-rans, Austria sent Georg Lhotsky’s German drama Moss on the Stones, Belgium offered Emile Degelin’s Dutch-language Palaver, and Brazil forwarded Glauber Rocha’s Portuguese O Dragão da Maldade contra o Santo Guerreiro, released abroad as Antonio das Mortes. Czechoslovakia’s Czech entry The Cremator came from director Juraj Herz, Denmark submitted the Danish Ballad of Carl-Henning by Lene and Sven Grønlykke, and West Germany proposed Peter Fleischmann’s Bavarian hunting tale Jagdszenen aus Niederbayern. Vasilis Georgiadis represented Greece with the Greek Girls in the Sun, Tang Shu Shuen flew the flag for Hong Kong with the Chinese The Arch, and Sándor Sára brought Hungary’s Hungarian The Upthrown Stone. India’s Tamil Deiva Magan was directed by A. C. Tirulokchandar, Israel entered Gilberto Tofano’s Hebrew Siege (Matzor), and Federico Fellini offered Italy the Italian Fellini Satyricon. Shōhei Imamura’s Japanese 神々の深き欲望 became Kuragejima, Legends From a Southern Island for Japan, Choi Ha-won’s Korean 독 짓는 늙은이 was retitled The Old Craftsman of Jar for South Korea, Harry Kümel shot the Dutch Monsieur Hawarden in the Netherlands, Armando Robles Godoy made Peru’s Spanish La muralla verde (The Green Wall), Andrzej Wajda delivered Poland’s Polish Wszystko na sprzedaz (Everything for Sale), and Gheorghe Vitanidis directed Romania’s Romanian Răutăciosul adolescent (A Woman for a Season); none of these advanced to the final five.
