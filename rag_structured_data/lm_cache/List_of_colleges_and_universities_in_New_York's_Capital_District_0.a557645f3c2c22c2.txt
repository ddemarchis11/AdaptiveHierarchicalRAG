SUNY Adirondack, founded in 1961, serves Queensbury as part of the State University of New York System, while Columbia-Greene Community College, established five years later, is based in Hudson under the same system. Albany hosts a cluster of specialized institutions: Albany College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences, dating to 1881, shares the University Heights joint campus with Albany Law School, the nation’s oldest independent law school, founded in 1851, and Albany Medical College, tracing its origins to 1839, all three operating within Union University. The College of Saint Rose, a private college established in 1920, and Maria College, a four-year Catholic college founded in 1958, also call Albany home, as do Excelsior College, a private institution since 2001 after two decades as SUNY Regents College, and Mildred Elley, dating to 1917. Troy houses Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, the English-speaking world’s oldest technological university, founded in 1824, alongside Russell Sage College, established in 1916, and Hudson Valley Community College, dating to 1953, with the latter also serving Northbush. The Sage Colleges network includes Sage College of Albany, founded in 1957 on the University Heights campus, and Sage Graduate School, established in 1949, operating in both Troy and Albany. Empire State College, created in 1971, maintains administrative offices in Saratoga Springs and learning centers in Latham, Queensbury, Schenectady, and Johnstown. Schenectady County Community College, founded in 1967, and Fulton-Montgomery Community College, dating to 1963, are SUNY community colleges located in Schenectady and Johnstown respectively. Bryant & Stratton College, a proprietary institution since 1854, and Siena College, a Catholic college established in 1937, are both situated in Colonie, while the Graduate College of Union University, the newest entry, opened in Schenectady in 2003.
