In 2007 the United States beat the Czech Republic for gold in Düsseldorf, Germany, while Germany itself took bronze. Great Britain stunned the United States 5-4 in the 2008 final in Philadelphia, with Canada earning bronze. The Czechs then began a four-year reign: in 2009 they defeated Canada in Varese, Italy, leaving the United States with bronze; in 2010 they edged the United States again in Düsseldorf while France claimed bronze; in 2011 they repeated the same medal order in Roccaraso, Italy; and in 2012 they overcame host Colombia in Bucaramanga, the United States settling for bronze. Anaheim 2013 saw the Czechs beat the United States once more, Canada third. France broke through in 2014, downing Canada in Toulouse with the Czechs third. The Czech Republic returned to the top in 2015 in Rosario, Argentina, ahead of France and Italy, then in 2016 defeated Italy on home ice in Asiago/Roana, Switzerland taking bronze. France regained the title in 2017 in Nanjing, China, over Spain with Italy third. The United States reclaimed gold in 2018 in Asiago/Roana, Italy, ahead of Italy and Spain. In 2019 the Czechs again edged the United States in Barcelona, Spain, with Spain third. Cartagena, Colombia, was slated to host the 2020 championship.
