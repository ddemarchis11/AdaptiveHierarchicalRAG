Wadgaon Road, a census town in Maharashtra, counted 40,884 residents in 2011, while nearby Wai, a municipal council, held 36,025 and Wani, also a municipal council, reached 58,840; Warora, another Maharashtrian municipal council, totaled 46,532, dwarfing Warud’s 6,386. Across Gujarat, Wadhwan municipality housed 75,755 people and Wankaner municipality 43,881. Karnataka’s Wadi, governed by a town municipal council, had 37,988 inhabitants. In Telangana, Wanaparthy municipality recorded 60,949. Madhya Pradesh’s Wara Seoni municipality contained 25,103, whereas Uttar Pradesh’s Warhapur nagar panchayat held 23,456 and Bihar’s Warisaliganj nagar panchayat 34,056. Farther northeast, Nagaland’s Wokha town committee comprised 35,004. Maharashtra’s Washim municipal council, largest among these, reached 78,387.
