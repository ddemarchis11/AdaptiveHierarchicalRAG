The Southeastern Conference dominated the 2011 NFL Draft with 38 selections, followed closely by the Atlantic Coast Conference with 35 players chosen. The Pac-10 Conference contributed 31 players, while the Big 12 and Big Ten Conferences saw 30 and 29 of their athletes drafted, respectively. The Big East Conference had 22 players selected. The Western Athletic Conference and Mountain West Conference produced 16 and 10 draftees, respectively. Conference USA contributed 7 players, while both the Southern Conference and Sun Belt Conference each saw 5 of their athletes drafted. The Big Sky Conference had 4 selections, and both the Mid-American Conference and Mid-Eastern Athletic Conference each contributed 3 players. Several conferences, including the Colonial Athletic Association, Lone Star Conference (Division II), Missouri Valley Football Conference, Pennsylvania State Athletic Conference (Division II), and Southland Conference, each had 2 players selected. The Ivy League rounded out the selections with 1 player drafted.
