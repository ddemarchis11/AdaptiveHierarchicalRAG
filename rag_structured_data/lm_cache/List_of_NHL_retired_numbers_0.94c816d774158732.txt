Ace Bailey’s number 6 was retired by the Toronto Maple Leafs on February 14, 1934, the league’s first such honour, while Bill Barilko’s 5 joined the rafters on October 17, 1992, and both Syl Apps and George Armstrong shared the October 15, 2016 retirement of number 10. Detroit Red Wings legend Sid Abel’s 12 was lifted on April 29, 1995, and Daniel Alfredsson’s 11 was put out of circulation by the Ottawa Senators on December 29, 2016. Glenn Anderson’s 9 was retired by the Edmonton Oilers on January 18, 2009, the same digit the New York Rangers withdrew for Andy Bathgate on February 22, 2009. Philadelphia Flyers stalwarts Barry Ashbee and Bill Barber saw 4 and 7 retired on October 13, 1977 and October 11, 1990 respectively. Montreal Canadiens immortalized Jean Beliveau’s 4 on October 9, 1971 and Emile Bouchard’s 3 on December 4, 2009. Ray Bourque uniquely had 77 retired twice within two months: by the Boston Bruins on October 4, 2001 and by the Colorado Avalanche on November 24, 2001. Mike Bossy’s 22 was raised by the New York Islanders on March 3, 1992, Rob Blake’s 4 by the Los Angeles Kings on January 17, 2015, Michel Briere’s 21 by the Pittsburgh Penguins on January 5, 2001, and Rod Brind’Amour’s 17 by the Carolina Hurricanes on February 18, 2011. Toronto further honoured Johnny Bower and Turk Broda by retiring number 1 for both on October 15, 2016.
