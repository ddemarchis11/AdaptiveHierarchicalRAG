Black Mambas play in Harare at the 5,000-seat Morris Depot Police Camp, while fellow Harare sides Black Rhinos, Dynamos and Harare City all share the 35,000-capacity Rufaro Stadium; CAPS United, also from the capital, occupy the giant 60,000-seat National Sports Stadium. Bulawayo’s Chicken Inn use the 8,000-seat Luveve Stadium, whereas city rivals Highlanders command the 32,000-seat Barbourfields and promoted How Mine stage matches at White City Stadium, which holds 20,000. FC Platinum and Shabanie Mine, both based in Zvishavane, host at Mandava Stadium (3,000) and Maglas Stadium (5,000) respectively. Mutare’s Buffaloes welcome fans to 10,000-seat Sakubva Stadium, Hwange’s Hwange play at the 15,000-capacity Colliery Stadium, Monomotapa United utilise Harare’s 10,000-seat Gwanzura Stadium, Motor Action’s home is the tiny 1,000-seat Motor Action Sports Club, Triangle United F.C. operate from Chiredzi’s 3,000-seat Gibbo Stadium, and Triple B draw support in Beitbridge at the 4,000-capacity Dulibadzimu Stadium.
