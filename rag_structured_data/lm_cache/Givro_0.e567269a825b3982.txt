Almanic Corporation, later Givro, opened its catalogue on 26 May 1990 with E.V.O.: The Theory of Evolution for Enix on the Japanese PC-98. A year and a half later Treco brought Fighting Masters to Mega Drive audiences in Japan and North America on 6 December 1991. Banpresto followed on 28 August 1992 with the Super Famicom exclusive CB Chara Wars. Enix returned on 19 December 1992 to publish the Super NES evolutionary sequel E.V.O.: Search for Eden in both Japan and North America. Sega took worldwide distribution of Mazin Saga: Mutant Fighter on 25 February 1993, releasing the Mega Drive title across Japan, North America and Europe. Dynamic Kikaku shipped the Super NES side-scrolling shooter Shien's Revenge on 8 April 1994, again for Japan and North America.
