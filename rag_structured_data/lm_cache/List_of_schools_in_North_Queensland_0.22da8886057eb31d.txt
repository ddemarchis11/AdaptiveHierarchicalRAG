Abergowrie State School has served the Hinchinbrook community since 1953, while Airville State School in Burdekin dates back to 1890. Aitkenvale State School opened in Townsville in 1924, and Annandale State School joined the Townsville suburbs in 1998. Alligator Creek State School and Andergrove State School, both in Mackay, trace their origins to 1896 and 1939 respectively. Ayr hosts two Burdekin schools: Ayr State School from 1886 and Ayr East State School from 1952. Barkly Highway State School has operated in Mount Isa since 1959. Beaconsfield State School in Mackay welcomed students from 1999, while Belgian Gardens State School in Townsville has a longer history dating to 1887. Bloomsbury State School in Mackay opened in 1927, followed by Bluewater State School in Townsville in 1957. Bohlevale State School in Townsville's Bohle suburb began on 20 November 1911. Bowen State School in Whitsunday is among the oldest, established in 1865. Brandon State School and Burketown State School both opened in 1888, serving Burdekin and Burke communities respectively. Bucasia State School in Mackay started in 1985. Bwgcolman Community School on Palm Island opened in 1964, while Cameron Downs State School via Hughenden in Flinders began in 1967.
