Campbell Cemetery, tucked off Muddy Lane southeast of Imboden, entered the National Register on 25 May 2005. Cedar Grove School #81, five miles north of Pocahontas on Highway 115 in Brockett, followed on 21 January 2004. The Hillyard Cabin, standing on the west side of Old Burr Road northeast of Warm Springs, was listed 16 August 1994, while the Looney-French House at 1325 Deer Run Trail in Dalton joined the register on 23 September 2004. Marr’s Creek Bridge on South Bettis Street, Pocahontas, was honored 24 January 2008. Old Davidsonville Historic Site, northeast of Black Rock on the Black River, dates to 18 January 1974, and the Old Randolph County Courthouse at Broadway and Vance in Pocahontas to 24 April 1973. Old Union School on 504 Old Union Road, Birdell, was listed 12 November 1993, and the Old US 67 segment from Biggers to Datto along Biggers-Reyno Road, 1st Street, and County Road 111 on 24 September 2004. The Pocahontas Commercial Historic District, roughly bounded by Rice, Thomasville, Jordan, and McDonald streets, entered 12 June 2009; Pocahontas Federal Savings and Loan at 201 West Broadway on 24 January 2019; the Pocahontas Post Office at 109 Van Bibber Street on 16 May 2002; and the current Randolph County Courthouse at the southwest corner of Broadway and North Marr on 22 August 1996. Ravenden Springs School on Highway 90 was listed 14 January 2004, the Rice-Upshaw House two miles south of Dalton on Highway 93 on 6 October 2004, St. Mary’s AME Church-Pocahontas Colored School at 1708 Archer Street on 5 August 2002, and Yadkin Church on the west side of Upper James Creek Road north of its junction with Lower James Creek Road in Ravenden Springs on 30 May 2019.
