Allen Iverson became the first overall pick of the 1996 NBA Draft when Philadelphia selected him, while that same year Detroit took Jerome Williams 26th overall and Houston chose Othella Harrington 30th. Two years earlier, Charlotte had drafted Alonzo Mourning second overall in 1992, and Denver selected Dikembe Mutombo fourth overall in 1991. Reggie Williams was the fourth pick in 1987, going to the Los Angeles Clippers. The 1986 draft saw four Hoyas selected: David Wingate 44th to Philadelphia, Michael Jackson 47th to the New York Knicks, Michael Graham 76th to Seattle, and Ralph Dalton 142nd to Cleveland. In 2013, Otto Porter Jr. was chosen third overall by Washington, while Greg Monroe went seventh overall to Detroit in 2010. Roy Hibbert was the 17th pick in 2008, selected by Indiana, and that same year Sacramento chose Patrick Ewing Jr. 42nd overall. Jeff Green was the fifth overall pick in 2007, heading to Boston, while Mike Sweetney was selected ninth overall by the New York Knicks in 2003. DaJuan Summers was the 35th overall pick in 2009, also going to Detroit, and Ruben Boumtje-Boumtje was chosen 50th overall by Portland in 2001. Jahidi White was selected 43rd overall by Washington in 1998, and Don Reid was the 58th overall pick in 1995, going to Detroit.
