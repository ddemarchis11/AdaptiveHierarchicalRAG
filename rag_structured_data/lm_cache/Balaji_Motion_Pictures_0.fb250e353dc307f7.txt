Balaji Motion Pictures opened its 2001 account with David Dhawan’s comedy Kyo Kii... Main Jhuth Nahin Bolta starring Govinda, Sushmita Sen and Rambha, then turned to horror in 2003 with Anurag Basu and Anil Vimal Kumar’s Kucch To Hai featuring Tusshar Kapoor, Esha Deol, Anita Hassanandani and Yash Tonk. Santram Verma’s 2004 chiller Krishna Cottage cast Sohail Khan, Isha Koppikar and Anita Hassanandani Reddy, while 2005 brought Sangeeth Sivan’s comedy-thriller Kyaa Kool Hai Hum with Tusshar Kapoor, Riteish Deshmukh, Isha Koppikar and Neha Dhupia, and Partho Mitra’s romance Koi Aap Sa headlined by Aftab Shivdasani, Anita Hassanandani, Dipannita Sharma and Himanshu Malik. Apoorva Lakhia’s 2007 crime saga Shootout at Lokhandwala crowded Amitabh Bachchan, Sanjay Dutt, Sunil Shetty, Vivek Oberoi, Arbaaz Khan, Amrita Singh, Tusshar Kapoor and Rohit Roy onto the screen, and Lakhia returned in 2008 with the crime thriller Mission Istanbul starring Vivek Oberoi, Shriya Saran and Zayed Khan. Also in 2008, Sachin Yardi’s comedy C Kkompany united Tusshar Kapoor, Anupam Kher, Rajpal Yadav and Raima Sen, while Saurabh Kabra’s social drama EMI – Liya Hai Toh Chukana Parega featured Arjun Rampal, Sanjay Dutt, Urmila Matondkar, Malaika Arora Khan, Aashish Chaudhary and Neha Oberoi. Dibakar Banerjee’s 2010 found-footage experiment Love Sex aur Dhokha introduced Anshuman Jha, Nushrat Bharucha, Sandeep Bose and Rajkummar Rao, the same year Milan Luthria’s gangster epic Once Upon a Time in Mumbaai paired Ajay Devgn and Emraan Hashmi with Kangana Ranaut and Prachi Desai. 2011 delivered Kiran Yadnyopavit’s Marathi drama Taryanche Bait with Vinay Apte, Sachin Khedekar, Ashwini Giri and Ashmita Joglekar; Raj Nidimoru and Krishna D.K.’s Hindi drama Shor in the City starring Sendhil Ramamurthy, Tusshar Kapoor and Nikhil Dwivedi; Pawan Kripalani’s horror-thriller Ragini MMS led by Rajkummar Rao and Kainaz Motivala; and Milan Luthria’s biographical hit The Dirty Picture spotlighting Vidya Balan alongside Emraan Hashmi, Tusshar Kapoor and Naseeruddin Shah. Sachin Yardi’s 2012 comedy sequel Kyaa Super Kool Hai Hum reunited Tusshar Kapoor and Riteish Deshmukh with Neha Sharma, while 2013 offered Kannan Iyer’s supernatural thriller Ek Thi Daayan with Emraan Hashmi, Konkana Sen Sharma, Huma Qureshi and Kalki Koechlin; Sanjay Gupta’s gangster crime follow-up Shootout at Wadala front-lined Anil Kapoor, John Abraham, Kangana Ranaut, Manoj Bajpayee and Tusshar Kapoor; Vikramaditya Motwane’s period romance Lootera paired Ranveer Singh and Sonakshi Sinha; and Milan Luthria closed the list with the crime-romance sequel Once Upon a Time In Mumbaai Dobara starring Akshay Kumar, Imran Khan and Sonakshi Sinha.
