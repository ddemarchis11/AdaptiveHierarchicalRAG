On April 1, 2010, 92-year-old John Forsythe—remembered as Blake Carrington on Dynasty and the unseen voice of Charlie on Charlie’s Angels—died. Eight days later, Meinhardt Raabe, 94, the Munchkin who triumphantly announces the witch’s death in The Wizard of Oz, passed away. The next day, April 10, brought the loss of 70-year-old Dixie Carter, whose Southern charm animated Designing Women, Diff’rent Strokes, Out of the Blue, The Edge of Night and Family Law. April 12 claimed Peter Haskell, 75, star of Bracken’s World and Ryan’s Hope. Civil-rights figure Benjamin Hooks, 85, the first African-American commissioner of the FCC and former NAACP executive director, died on April 15. Voice-over legend Allen Swift, 86, whose talents shaped The Howdy Doody Show, Mighty Mouse and Underdog, left on April 18. Screenwriter Myles Wilder, 77, who penned episodes of McHale’s Navy and The Dukes of Hazzard, died April 20. The month’s final farewell came April 27 with Dorothy Provine, 75, actress-comedian-dancer-singer who lit up The Roaring 20s, The Alaskans and Man Without a Gun.
