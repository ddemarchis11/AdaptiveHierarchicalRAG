Athens 1896 staged its tennis on clay at both the Athens Lawn Tennis Club and the Neo Phaliron Velodrome, the latter holding 7,000 spectators, while Paris 1900 used the clay courts of Cercle de Puteaux. Francis Field in St. Louis 1904, also clay, hosted the tournament, and London 1908 switched to grass at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club. Stockholm 1912 chose clay at the Östermalm Athletic Grounds, Antwerp 1920 returned to grass at Beerschot Tennis Club, and Paris 1924 again favoured clay at Stade de Tennis de Colombes. After a long hiatus, Seoul 1988 built a 15,000-seat Olympic Tennis Center with hard courts, followed by Barcelona 1992’s clay Vall d’Hebron complex seating 8,000. Atlanta 1996 erected the 27,500-capacity Stone Mountain Tennis Center on hard courts, Sydney 2000’s NSW Tennis Centre held 10,000 on hard courts, and Athens 2004’s Olympic Tennis Centre seated 8,600 on the same surface. Beijing 2008’s Olympic Green Tennis Center accommodated 17,400, London 2012 returned to the All England Club’s grass with 30,000 seats, Rio 2016’s Olympic Tennis Centre held 18,250 on hard courts, and Tokyo 2020’s Ariake Tennis Forest Park closed the list with 18,000 hard-court seats.
