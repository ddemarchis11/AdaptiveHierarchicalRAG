Ion Antonescu, Romanian prime minister from 1940 to 1944, was executed by firing squad on 1 June 1946, while his fellow Bulgarians Ivan Bagryanov, Dobri Bozhilov and Bogdan Filov, who successively led Bulgaria during the war, were all executed in early 1945. Turkey’s Celal Bayar, president from 1950 to 1960 after earlier serving as prime minister, saw his death sentence commuted and was freed in 1964, whereas Adnan Menderes, Turkish premier from 1950 to 1960, was hanged on 17 September 1961. Pakistan’s Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, president then prime minister during the 1970s, was hanged on 4 April 1979, and Central African Emperor Jean-Bédel Bokassa, after ruling as president then emperor, had his sentence commuted and walked free in 1993. Romania’s Nicolae Ceauşescu, president from 1967 to 1989, was shot by firing squad on Christmas Day 1989. Charles I, king of England Scotland and Ireland from 1625 to 1649, was beheaded on 30 January 1649, and France’s Louis XVI, king from 1774 to 1792, was guillotined on 21 January 1793. Collaborationist China’s Chen Gongbo and Liang Hongzhi were both executed by firing squad in 1946, Iran’s premier Amir-Abbas Hoveida was shot on 7 April 1979, Equatorial Guinea’s Francisco Macías Nguema was executed in 1979, Congo-Brazzaville’s Alphonse Massemba-Débat was executed in 1977, and South Korea’s Chun Doo-hwan, president from 1980 to 1988, had his sentence commuted and was released in 1997.
