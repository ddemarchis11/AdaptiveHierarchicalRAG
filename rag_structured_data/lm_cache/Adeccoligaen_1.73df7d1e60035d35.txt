Aalesund, third last year, play at the 10,778-seat Color Line Stadion in Ålesund, while HamKam, who finished ninth, host matches at Briskeby Arena in Hamar with room for 7,800. Grimstad’s Jerv ended thirteenth and use the 3,300-capacity Levermyr Stadion, and Oslo’s KFUM, promoted via the 2. divisjon group 2 play-offs, now compete at the 1,500-seat KFUM Arena. Kongsvinger, eighth last season, play at Gjemselund Stadion holding 5,824, and Nest-Sotra, sixth, welcome opponents to Ågotnes Stadion on Sotra with only 1,200 seats. Raufoss earned direct promotion as group 1 winners and stage games at 2,500-capacity Nammo Stadion, whereas Sandefjord, relegated from Eliteserien in sixteenth, bring top-flight experience to Komplett Arena’s 6,582 seats. Sandnes Ulf, tenth, occupy Sandnes Stadion with 4,969 places, and Oslo’s Skeid, champions of 2. divisjon group 2, now face senior opposition at tiny 264-seat Nordre Åsen. Sogndal, fourth, play at 5,622-seat Fosshaugane Campus, Start, relegated in fifteenth, command Kristiansand’s 14,563-seat Sør Arena, Strømmen, eleventh, use 1,850-seat Strømmen Stadion, Tromsdalen, seventh, share Tromsø’s 1,695-capacity Alfheim Stadion, and Ullensaker/Kisa, fifth, host at 3,500-seat Jessheim Stadion.
