St. Patrick’s Church, raised in 1915 on Higgins Hill in Bisbee, Arizona, earned National Register recognition in 1995 for its Late Gothic Revival design. Flagstaff’s Our Lady of Guadalupe, completed in 1926, was listed in 1986, while Globe’s 1916 Holy Angels gained NRHP status in 1983. Kingman’s 1906 St. Mary’s, Mission/Spanish Revival in style, and Miami’s 1917 Our Lady of the Blessed Sacrament, listed in 2008, anchor their respective towns. Pearce honors the 2004-listed Our Lady of Victory, and Phoenix shelters four treasures: the 1966 Modern Cathedral of Saints Simon and Jude, the 1993 Immaculate Heart of Mary, the 2012-listed Sacred Heart, and the 1902 Mission Revival/Spanish Colonial Revival St. Mary’s Basilica, NRHP-listed in 1978. Prescott’s 1894 Late Gothic Revival Sacred Heart, rectory included, joined the Register in 1978, while Tempe’s Romanesque 1903 St. Mary’s-Our Lady of Mount Carmel did the same. Tucson claims four: the 1881 Gothic Revival Sacred Heart, 1968 Mexican Baroque Cathedral of St. Augustine, 1918 Mission/Spanish Revival Santa Cruz, and 1797 Mission/Spanish Colonial Mission San Xavier del Bac, listed in 1966. Finally, Window Rock’s St. Michael’s Mission, NRHP-listed in 1975, stands north of town off Route 264.
