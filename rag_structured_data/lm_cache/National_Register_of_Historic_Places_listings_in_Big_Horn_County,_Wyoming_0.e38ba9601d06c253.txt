Greybull’s American Legion Hall, Post 32, entered the National Register on 27 June 2014 at 130 N. 5th St., while the Carey Block at 602 Greybull Ave. followed on 18 December 2009. Greybull also safeguards the Bear Creek Ranch Medicine Wheel, listed 4 May 1987 but kept at an undisclosed location, and the Lower Shell School House, added 7 February 1985 beside U.S. Route 14. Basin’s Republican-Rustler Printing Building, 409 W. C St., was honored 19 July 1976, and Cowley’s Big Horn Academy Historic District, 25 and 35 E. 1st S., joined the register 26 March 1992. Lovell claims the 29 October 1975 Bad Pass Trail east along the Bighorn River inside Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area, the 22 February 1985 EJZ Bridge over the Shoshone River on County Road CN9-111, the 2009 Hyart Theater at 251 E. Main St., and the 15 July 1992 M L Ranch off Alternate U.S. 14 near Bighorn Lake. Shell’s Black Mountain Archeological District, encompassing the summit and southeast slopes, was listed 2 July 1987, the same year the EJE Bridge over Shell Creek on County Road CN9-57 was entered on 22 February 1985; the Hanson Site, recorded 15 December 1978, remains address-restricted. Hyattville contributes the 22 February 1985 EJP County Line Bridge on Road CN9-60, the 5 July 1973 Medicine Lodge Creek Site at 4800 County Road 52, and the 12 July 1990 Paint Rock Canyon Archeological Landscape District, the latter two with restricted or omitted coordinates. Bridger Immigrant Road–Dry Creek Crossing, 26 miles east of Cody on U.S. 14, was listed 17 January 1975, while Medicine Wheel–Medicine Mountain, near the summit of Medicine Mountain, became a National Historic Landmark on 16 April 1969. Rairden Bridge south of County Road CN9-30 was added 22 February 1985 near Manderson, and Schunk Lodge, roughly a mile north of Red Grade Road in Bighorn National Forest, entered the register on 4 January 2018 in the Big Horn vicinity.
