Bon Jovi’s 1986 hard-rock and glam-metal landmark Slippery When Wet and Mariah Carey’s 1993 pop-and-R&B blockbuster Music Box have each sold 28 million copies. Madonna’s 1986 dance-pop classic True Blue, Linkin Park’s 2000 nu-metal/rap-metal Hybrid Theory, Eminem’s 2002 hip-hop statement The Eminem Show and Norah Jones’ 2002 jazz debut Come Away with Me all stand at 27 million. Eric Clapton’s 1992 acoustic rock and blues set Unplugged has moved 26 million, while Bob Marley & The Wailers’ 1984 reggae anthology Legend, Carole King’s 1971 pop masterpiece Tapestry, Phil Collins’ 1985 pop-rock release No Jacket Required, Queen’s 1981 rock compilation Greatest Hits, Simon & Garfunkel’s 1970 folk-rock Bridge over Troubled Water, U2’s 1987 rock epic The Joshua Tree, Prince and The Revolution’s 1984 pop-rock/new-wave/R&B soundtrack Purple Rain, Britney Spears’ 1999 pop debut ...Baby One More Time and George Michael’s 1987 pop/R&B/funk/soul album Faith have all reached 25 million. Elton John’s 1974 pop collection Greatest Hits, the Backstreet Boys’ 1997 self-titled/Backstreet’s Back and their 1999 Millennium, plus the Spice Girls’ 1996 pop debut Spice, close the list with 24 million and 23 million respectively.
