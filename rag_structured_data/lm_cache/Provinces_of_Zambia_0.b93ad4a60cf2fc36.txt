Central Province stretches across 110,450 km² from its seat in Kabwe and holds 1,743,999 people at 15.8 per km², divided into 12 districts. On the mineral-rich Copperbelt, Ndola governs 31,328 km² that teem with 2,642,132 inhabitants at 81.2 per km² across 11 districts. Eastern Province, headquartered in Chipata, covers 51,476 km² and counts 1,961,269 residents at 38.1 per km² within 14 districts. Mansa presides over Luapula’s 50,567 km², home to 1,215,294 people at 24 per km² split into 12 districts. Lusaka Province, smallest yet most crowded, unites its capital city and 3,186,336 citizens across 23,490 km² at 135.6 per km² in just 7 districts. Muchinga, run from Chinsali, spreads across 87,806 km² with 1,011,655 people at 11.5 per km² in 10 districts. Solwezi directs sparsely peopled North-Western Province: 125,826 km² hold 902,631 residents at 7.2 per km² in 11 districts. Northern Province, centred on Kasama, occupies 77,650 km² and shelters 1,430,543 inhabitants at 18.4 per km² within 12 districts. Southern Province, governed from Choma, covers 68,410 km² and houses 1,852,361 people at 27.1 per km² across 13 districts. Western Province, largest of all, extends 126,386 km² from Mongu and contains 1,041,500 residents at 8.2 per km² in 16 districts.
