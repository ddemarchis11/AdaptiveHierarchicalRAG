The Presbyterian Church of Atlanta, New York, erected in 1895 and listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2010, stands at 2 Main Street in Queen Anne and Gothic Revival styles. Batavia’s First Presbyterian Church, built in 1854 and NRHP-listed in 2004, occupies the corner of East Main and Liberty Streets in Gothic Revival form. Bedford Presbyterian Church, dating to 1872 and listed in 1973, sits at 44 Village Green within the Bedford Village Historic District, also Gothic Revival. Schenectady’s First Presbyterian Church, built in 1809 and NRHP-listed in 2000, stands at 115 Union Street in New England style and belongs to the Presbyterian Church in America. Batchellerville’s Greek Revival church, built in 1867 and listed in 2000, lies on County Route 7. Central Presbyterian Church in Manhattan, founded in 1821 and housed since 1922 at 593 Park Avenue, is a Gothic Revival structure originally built as Park Avenue Baptist Church by John D. Rockefeller, Jr., and is now part of the Evangelical Presbyterian Church. Brockport’s 1852 Greek Revival First Presbyterian Church, NRHP-listed in 1999, stands at 35 State Street. Setauket Presbyterian Church and Burial Ground, built in 1812 and listed in 1996, is located at 5 Caroline Avenue in Brookhaven in Federal style. Cuyler Presbyterian Church, designed by Edward Sargent in Greek Revival, was built in 1892 at 358-360 Pacific Street, Brooklyn, and NRHP-listed in 2001. Buffalo’s First Presbyterian Church, founded in 1812, erected its current building in 1891. Lafayette Avenue Presbyterian Church, built in 1894 and NRHP-listed in 2009, stands at 875 Elmwood Avenue in Romanesque Revival style. Chester’s First Presbyterian Church, built in 1854 for $10,000 and NRHP-listed in 1998, is at 106-108 Main Street in Greek Revival style. Cochecton Presbyterian Church, built in 1903 and NRHP-listed in 1992, sits on County Road 114 east of the Delaware River Bridge in Akron Plan Late 19th and Early 20th Century American Movements style. Silliman Memorial Presbyterian Church, designed by Fuller & Wheeler in 1897 and NRHP-listed in 1979, stands at Mohawk and Seneca Streets in Cohoes in Romanesque and Richardsonian Romanesque styles. Canterbury Presbyterian Church, built in 1826 and NRHP-listed in 1996, is in Federal style. Cortland’s First Presbyterian Church Complex, built in 1889 and NRHP-listed in 2002, stands at 23 Church Street in Late Victorian and Late 19th and 20th Century Revivals styles. Hopewell Presbyterian Church, built in 1831 and NRHP-listed in 1998, is located at the junction of NY 302 and NY 17 in Crawford in Gothic Revival style. Delhi’s First Presbyterian Church, built in 1882 and NRHP-listed in 2006, stands on Clinton Street in Late Victorian style. South Presbyterian Church, built between 1868 and 1869 and NRHP-listed in 2000, is in Dobbs Ferry in Gothic Revival style. Reformed Presbyterian Church Parsonage, built circa 1829 and NRHP-listed in 1984, stands on Duanesburg Churches Road in Federal Vernacular style.
