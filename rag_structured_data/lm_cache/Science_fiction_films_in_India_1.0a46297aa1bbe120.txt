Kaadu, the 1952 Tamil-English co-production about woolly mammoths causing animal havoc, became India’s first science-fiction film. Shantilal Soni’s 1964 Hindi Mr.X in Bombay starred Kishore Kumar, Kum Kum and Madan Puri. Satyajit Ray’s cancelled 1965 Bengali-English project The Alien, drawn from his 1962 Sandesh tale Bankubabur Bandhu, would have shown friendly extraterrestrial Mr. Ang visiting a 1960s Bengali village. Mahesh’s 1967 Malayalam Karutha Rathrikal, starring Madhu and Gemini Ganesan with music by MS Baburaj and lyrics by ONV Kurup, re-imagined Stevenson’s Jekyll-Hyde via a split-personality drug invented by a vengeful doctor. Naresh Kumar Saxena, in the 1971 Hindi Elaan starring Vinod Mehra, Rekha and Vinod Khanna, gains invisibility through an atomic ring and battles underworld bosses. M. G. Ramachandran directed and starred in the 1973 Tamil blockbuster Ulagam Sutrum Valiban alongside M. N. Nambiar, Manjula and Thai actress Metta Roongrat. Kamal Haasan produced and headlined the 1983 Tamil Vikram, India’s first showcase of ICBMs and advanced computers. Raj N. Sippy’s 1985 Hindi superhero film Shiva Ka Insaaf starred Jackie Shroff, Poonam Dhillon and Gulshan Grover. Sasi Kumar’s 1987 Malayalam Jaithra Yaathra featured Thilakan as a scientist whose invisibility-locket empowers Nizhalgal Ravi against Babu Antony. Shekhar Kapur’s 1987 Hindi-Urdu superhero hit Mr. India, scripted by Salim-Javed, paired Anil Kapoor and Sridevi against Amrish Puri. In the 1989 Tamil Nalaya Manithan a 21st-century doctor’s resurrection drug creates an immortal zombie; its 1990 sequel Adhisaya Manithan continues the undead rampage. Ketan Desai’s 1989 Hindi Toofan cast Amitabh Bachchan in a double role as crossbow-wielding superhero Toofan and magician twin Shyam. Aditya 369, the 1991 Telugu debut of the genre, centred on a time-machine and diamond heist. Laal Paree, also 1991 Hindi, starred Aditya Pancholi and Sohani in a mermaid romance inspired by Splash. Dinesh Babu’s 2002 Kannada Hollywood showcased Upendra in a triple role including android US 47 alongside Australian actress Felicity Mason. Abhijit Chaudhuri’s 2003 Bengali Patalghar followed criminals hunting an ancient sleeping machine, while the same year’s Hindi comedy Funn2shh sent travellers to the 10th century. Rakesh Roshan’s 2003 Hindi blockbuster Koi… Mil Gaya presented Hrithik Roshan’s Rohit Mehra, a mentally-challenged youth who gains superhuman powers from a visiting alien.
