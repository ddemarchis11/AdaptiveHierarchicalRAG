Christian Gytkjær left Lyngby for Nordsjælland on a free transfer on 2 February 2010, while Mathias Nielsen made the same cost-free switch from HB Køge to Nordsjælland on 10 April. Canadian midfielder Atiba Hutchinson departed Copenhagen for PSV Eindhoven without a fee on 22 April, and Swedish defender Benjamin Kibebe moved from Nordsjælland to Swiss club Luzern on 5 May for an undisclosed sum. Kasper Jensen joined Midtjylland from German side Paderborn on a free the next day, and on 19 May Jesper Jensen went from SønderjyskE to Vejle under undisclosed terms. Jesper Mikkelsen returned from French club Troyes to sign with Silkeborg for free on 20 May, and on 25 May both Mads Pedersen and Mark Leth Pedersen left Brøndby—Mads to Danish third-tier FCV on undisclosed terms and Mark Leth also to FCV but without a fee. Peter Ankersen completed a free move from Esbjerg to Vejle on 26 May. Henrik Ipsen followed the same free path from SønderjyskE to Vejle on 2 June 2010, while Czech Martin Raška left Midtjylland for Slovakian side Trnava on 3 June 2009 and Icelandic winger Arnór Smárason arrived at Esbjerg from Dutch club Heerenveen on 7 June 2009, both without transfer fees. When the window officially opened on 1 July 2010, six players returned from loan spells: Swedish goalkeeper Abbas Hassan to Elfsborg from AaB, Danish striker Morten Nordstrand to Copenhagen from Groningen, Danish midfielder Emil Ousager to OB from Næsby, Icelandic forward Gunnar Thorvaldsson to Esbjerg from Reading, Swedish forward Ken Fagerberg to Midtjylland from Örgryte, and Icelandic midfielder Stefan Gislason to Brøndby from Viking, while Danish defender Pierre Kanstrup also returned to Brøndby from Lyngby.
