Microsoft leads the world’s public companies with a market capitalization of 264,003 million USD, anchored in the software industry and headquartered in the United States. Close behind, General Electric, a U.S.–based conglomerate, is valued at 259,647 million USD, while Exxon Mobil, the oil-and-gas giant also based in the United States, holds 241,037 million USD. Retail titan Wal-Mart follows at 234,399 million USD, still within U.S. borders. Pfizer, specializing in health care, claims 195,948 million USD, and Citigroup, in banking, reaches 183,887 million USD. Johnson & Johnson, another health-care leader, is appraised at 170,417 million USD. Breaking the U.S. streak, Royal Dutch Shell of the Netherlands, an oil-and-gas major, is valued at 149,034 million USD, while the United Kingdom’s BP, also in oil and gas, stands at 144,381 million USD. IBM, dual-rooted in computer software and hardware and based in the United States, closes the top ten at 139,272 million USD.
