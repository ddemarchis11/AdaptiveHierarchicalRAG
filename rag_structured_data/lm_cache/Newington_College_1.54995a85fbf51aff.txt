Rev. James Egan Moulton, educated at Kingswood School and founding headmaster of Tupou College in Tonga, led Newington from its 1863 opening. George Metcalfe, Melbourne and Sydney graduate who had founded Druitt Town Public School and run Goulburn High, succeeded him 1867-69. Trinity Dublin and Melbourne alumnus Michael Howe, previously founding head of Galt Grammar School and head of Toronto’s Jarvis Collegiate, served 1868-77. Joseph Coates, Huddersfield College alumnus and founding head of Sydney Boys’ High, held office 1877-83. Cambridge classicist William Williams, former professor at the University of Tasmania, guided the college 1884-92. Arthur Lucas, Kingswood and Balliol graduate, ex-head of Sydney Grammar and Tasmanian mathematics professor, led 1893-98. Edward Cornwall, Oxford classicist and former acting head of Ipswich Grammar, served briefly in 1899. Rev. Charles Prescott, Worcester College alumnus who founded Wesleyan Ladies’ College, held the post 1900-31. Philip Le Couteur, Melbourne Queen’s and Oxford graduate who had headed Methodist Ladies’ College and Perth’s Hale School, served 1931-48. Mervyn Austin, Melbourne Grammar and Oxford classicist and former UWA professor, led 1950-51. Lawrence Pyke, Wesley and Oxford-educated dean of Melbourne graduate studies, headed the school 1952-60. Otago and Columbia mathematician Ernest Duncan, ex-Rutgers professor, served 1962. Rev. Douglas Trathen, Sydney graduate and former Wolaroi head, led 1963-70. Scots College and Sydney alumnus Tony Rae, ex-Albury Grammar head, served 1972-93. Michael Smee, King’s School and Sydney graduate, former Pulteney Grammar head, led 1993-2003. David Scott, with three WA universities, previously head of Kingswood College and Brisbane’s Anglican Church Grammar, served 2003-09. David Mulford, ex-Radford and Blue Mountains Grammar principal, led 2009-18. Since 2019 James Ruse and Sydney graduate Michael Parker, former Oxley head and Cranbrook deputy, has governed the college.
