fluid Operations eCloudManager, launched on 1 March 2009 under a proprietary license and built in Java and Groovy, is the only platform listed that cannot be consumed as a service yet can be installed locally. AppScale followed six days later, offering Apache-licensed code written in Python, Ruby and Go, and both service and local deployment options. Cloud Foundry arrived on 12 April 2011 with Ruby, C, Java and Go under the Apache license, while Cloud.com/CloudStack debuted on 4 May 2010 in Java and C, both supporting service and local use. Eucalyptus, dating to 29 May 2008, carries dual proprietary and GPL v3 licensing in Java and C, and Flexiant Limited, the earliest from 15 January 2007, remains proprietary in Java and C, both enabling service and local operation. Nimbus, released 9 January 2009, and OpenNebula, from March 2008, use Apache licenses—Nimbus in Java and Python, OpenNebula in C++, C, Ruby, Java, shell, lex and yacc—each service-ready and locally installable. OpenQRM, also March 2008, opts for GPL licensing with C++, PHP and shell, while OpenShift, launched 4 May 2011, uses Apache-licensed Go; both provide service and local modes. OpenStack, 21 October 2010, contributes Apache-licensed Python, OnApp, 1 July 2010, offers proprietary Java, Ruby and C++, and Jelastic, 27 January 2011, multi-licensed under GPL, Apache and BSD, blends Java, JavaScript, Perl and shell, all service and local capable. oVirt, newest on 9 August 2012, Apache-licensed in Java and Python, supports local installation while its service status is unspecified.
