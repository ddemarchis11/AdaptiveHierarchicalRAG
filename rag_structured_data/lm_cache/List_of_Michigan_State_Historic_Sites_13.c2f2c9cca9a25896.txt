The Bradley House, standing at the corner of Cook Road and Main Street in Midland, earned its Michigan State Historic Site status on April 23, 1971, and is also listed on the National Register. The Chemical Industry Informational Designation, located on the 700 block of West Main Street, was recognized much earlier, on September 17, 1957. Out in the Midland vicinity, the Homer Township Cemetery at 1136 East Prairie Road, bordered by Pine River and Prairie roads, was listed on September 19, 1991. Downtown at 505 Main Street, the John and Almira Kelly House joined the register on September 24, 1984. The Midland County Courthouse, situated at 301 West Main Street, was designated on May 21, 1985, and carries National Register recognition. Nearby at 812 West Main Street, the James T. Pardee House was listed on March 19, 1987, also holding National Register status. Spanning Currie Parkway over the Tittabawassee River, the Upper Bridge was honored on August 18, 1988. Finally, the Alden Dow House and Studio at 315 Post Street was listed on June 29, 1989.
