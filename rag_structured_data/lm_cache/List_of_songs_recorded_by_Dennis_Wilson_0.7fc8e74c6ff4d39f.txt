Dennis Wilson cut “Album Tag Song” on 22–23 March 1977, a 3:45 self-penned track that surfaced only on the 2008 re-issue of Pacific Ocean Blue, co-produced with John Hanlon and Gregg Jakobson. Carli Muñoz supplied both “All Alone” (June 1978, 3:44) and “It’s Not Too Late” (May–July 1978, 4:32), each produced with Wilson, while Muñoz and Rags Baker’s “Constant Companion” (June 8 & July 5 1978, 3:22) shares the same credits. Wilson and Jakobson co-wrote nearly everything else: “Are You Real?” (1978, 3:38), “Cocktails” (1977, 3:00), “Common” (April 3 & 13 1978, 3:34), “He’s a Bum” (July–August 1977, 2:50), “Holy Man” (February 12 1975, 4:24), “I Love You” (October 15 & November 1 1978, 2:02), “Love Remember Me” (March 3 & early August 1977, 4:04), “Love Surrounds Me” (1978-79 sessions, 3:40), and “Only with You” (September 1976–77, 3:57). “Dreamer” (March 18 1977, 4:22), “End of the Show” (March 29 & April 19 1977, 2:55), “Farewell My Friend” (April 21 1976, 2:26), “Friday Night” (3:09) and “Moonshine” (2:27) all appear on the original 1977 Pacific Ocean Blue, produced by Wilson and Jakobson. “Lady,” recorded December 26 1969–January 26 1970 (2:24), was released only as a 1970 single, producer uncredited, while “Mexico” (May 1 1978, 5:13) completes the re-issue set.
