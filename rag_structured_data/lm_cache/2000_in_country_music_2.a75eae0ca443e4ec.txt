Neal McCoy’s 24-7-365 reached No. 28 on the U.S. chart under Giant, while Ronnie Milsap’s 40 # 1 Hits peaked at 19 on Virgin Nashville. Amanda Stott’s self-titled Warner release, Johnny Cash’s American III: Solitary Man hit 11 on Lost Highway, and Jeff Foxworthy’s Big Funny climbed to 15 on DreamWorks Nashville. Ricky Skaggs honored Bill Monroe with Big Mon: The Songs of Bill Monroe at 42 on his own Skaggs Family label, BR5-49’s Coast to Coast reached 46 on Arista Nashville, and Buddy & Julie Miller’s duo album landed at 45 on Hightone. Chris LeDoux’s Cowboy rode to 17 on Capitol Nashville, the Various Artists compilation A Country Superstar Christmas III hit 22 on Hip-O, and Elvis Presley’s posthumous The Elvis Presley Collection - Country reached 19 on RCA. Steve Wariner’s Faith in You peaked at 31 on Capitol Nashville, Jennifer Day’s The Fun of Your Love reached 36 on BNA, and Neko Case issued Furnace Room Lullaby on Mint without a listed chart position. Roy D. Mercer’s Greatest Fits: The Best of How Big a Boy Are Ya? hit 26 on Virgin Nashville, BlackHawk’s Greatest Hits reached 18 on Arista Nashville, Bryan White’s Greatest Hits peaked at 25 on Asylum, John Berry’s Greatest Hits reached 43 on Capitol Nashville, Stompin’ Tom Connors released Greatest Hits/Souvenirs on EMI, and Chet Atkins’s triple-package Guitar Genius/Relaxin’ with Chet/Nashville Gold closed the list at 42 on RCA Nashville.
