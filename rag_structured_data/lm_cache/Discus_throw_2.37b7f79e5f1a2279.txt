Jay Silvester opened 1968 with a 68.40 m heave in Reno, then reclaimed the spotlight in 1971 by reaching 70.38 m at Lancaster. Between those American highlights, Sweden’s Rickard Bruch dominated 1969-74, recording 68.06 m in Malmo, 67.14 m the next year in the same city, 68.40 m in Stockholm 1972, 67.58 m in Skellefteå 1973, and 68.16 m in Helsingborg 1974. John Powell interrupted the Swedish run with 69.08 m at Long Beach 1975, before Mac Wilkins soared to 70.86 m in San Jose 1976 and followed with 69.20 m at Westwood 1977. Wolfgang Schmidt pushed the standard to 71.16 m in Berlin 1978, yet Wilkins answered in 1979-80, throwing 70.66 m at Walnut and 70.98 m in Erfurt. Powell returned to win 1981 with 69.98 m in Modesto, while Luis Delís reached 70.58 m at Salinas 1982. Yuriy Dumchev raised the bar to 71.86 m in Moskva 1983, and Powell matched 71.26 m in San Jose 1984. Imrich Bugár equalled that 71.26 m in San Jose 1985, before Jürgen Schult shattered the progression, hurling 74.08 m at Neubrandenburg 1986 and closing the sequence with 69.52 m there in 1987.
