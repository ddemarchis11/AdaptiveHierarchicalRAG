Agde Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Stephen, stands in Agde under Montpellier’s jurisdiction but lost its cathedral status when the bishopric was suppressed in 1801. Agen Cathedral honours Saint Caprasius in Agen itself, serves as the diocese’s cathedral, and is listed as a World Heritage Site. Aire Cathedral, dedicated to Saint John the Baptist, rises in Aire-sur-l’Adour for the combined diocese of Aire et Dax. Aix Cathedral, the Holy Saviour church in Aix-en-Provence, functions as cathedral and minor basilica. Ajaccio Cathedral, dedicated to the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary, is the cathedral of Ajaccio. Albi Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Cecilia in Albi, holds both cathedral rank and minor-basilica status. Alès Cathedral, once the Saint John the Baptist seat in Alès under Nîmes, was reduced to a former cathedral in 1801. Alet Cathedral, now ruined, was the Blessed Virgin Mary cathedral at Alet-les-Bains until its 1801 suppression. Aleth Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Peter in Saint-Servan, was replaced by Saint-Malo in 1146 and destroyed by 1255. Amiens Cathedral, Notre-Dame in Amiens, is a cathedral, minor basilica and World Heritage Site. Angers Cathedral honours Saint Maurice in Angers; Angoulême Cathedral honours Saint Peter in Angoulême; Annecy Cathedral honours Saint Peter in Annecy. Antibes Cathedral, dedicated to Notre-Dame de la Platéa, lost its cathedral standing in 1801. Apt Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Ann, was suppressed the same year. Arles’ former cathedral, now Church of St Trophime, honours Saint Trophimus and lost archiepiscopal status in 1822. Arras Cathedral, dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary and Saint Vedast, is a cathedral and minor basilica. Auch Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Mary, is a cathedral, minor basilica and World Heritage Site. Autun Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Lazarus, is a 12th-century cathedral and minor basilica, while the Old Autun Cathedral, dedicated to Saint Nazarius, served from the 5th to the 12th century.
