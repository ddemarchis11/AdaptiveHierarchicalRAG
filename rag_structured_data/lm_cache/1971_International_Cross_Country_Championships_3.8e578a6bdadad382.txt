England’s sextet of Dave Bedford, Trevor Wright, Peter Standing, Tony Simmons, Frank Briscoe and Andy Holden dominated the 1971 International Cross Country Championships with a commanding 56-point victory. Belgium followed 120 points adrift as Gaston Roelants, René Goris, Willy Polleunis, Karel Lismont, Eddy van Butsele and Paul Thijs totaled 176. France took third on 186 points from Noel Tijou, Lucien Rault, Antoine Borowski, Michel Bernard, Jean-Yves Le Flohic and Jean-Pierre Dufresne. New Zealand’s Eddie Gray, Rod Dixon, Bryan Rose, Rex Maddaford, John Sheddan and Martin Simmons amassed 194 for fourth, while Spain’s Javier Álvarez, Mariano Haro, Juan Hidalgo, Julio Gude, Ramon Tasende and Fernando Aguilar scored 198 for fifth. Morocco’s Lahcen El Hachmi, Moha Aït Bassou, Miloud Chenna, Haddou Jaddour, Ben Assou El Ghazi and Moumoh Haddou earned 215, just ahead of Scotland’s Ian Stewart, Dick Wedlock, Norman Morrison, Jim Alder, Alistair Blamire and Lachie Stewart on 216. Finland, Wales, Poland, Ireland, Switzerland, Italy, Algeria and Northern Ireland completed the field with totals of 229, 293, 362, 392, 462, 521, 557 and 636 respectively.
