William A. Jones III, Class of 1945, rose to colonel in the United States Air Force and earned the Medal of Honor for rescuing a downed pilot. Andre Lucas, Class of 1954, became a lieutenant colonel decorated with the Medal of Honor for holding off a vastly superior force for 23 days. Roger Donlon, who left the Academy in 1959 for personal reasons, later served as a captain and eventually colonel, receiving the Medal of Honor for repelling a much larger enemy force. Humbert Roque Versace, Class of 1959, earned the Medal of Honor as a captain for defying Viet Cong indoctrination while a prisoner of war, an ordeal detailed by fellow POW Nick Rowe in Five Years to Freedom. James A. Gardner, who did not graduate with the Class of 1965, nevertheless received the Medal of Honor posthumously as a first lieutenant for leading his platoon to relieve an embattled company. Frank S. Reasoner, Class of 1962, cross-commissioned into the Marine Corps as a first lieutenant and earned the Medal of Honor for leading a reconnaissance patrol against overwhelming odds while trying to save a wounded comrade. Robert F. Foley, Class of 1963, received the Medal of Honor as a captain for rallying his unit on 11 November 1966, personally destroying three enemy strongpoints; he later served as West Point Commandant of Cadets, president of Marion Military Institute, and director of the Army Emergency Relief Program. Paul William Bucha, Class of 1965, earned the Medal of Honor as a captain for leading his unit against superior forces in Bình Dương Province, Vietnam, and later advised Barack Obama on foreign policy during the 2008 presidential campaign.
