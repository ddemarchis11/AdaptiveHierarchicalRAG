The Caldwell Home Place at 160 Curry Street in LaBelle entered the National Register on February 13, 2003. Clewiston’s educational heritage is anchored by the Clewiston Historic Schools, listed September 26, 1997, occupying 325 East Circle Drive and 475 East Osceola Avenue. Nearby, the Clewiston Inn, fronting U.S. Route 27 west of its junction with State Road 832, was honored on February 21, 1991, while the Dixie Crystal Theatre at 100 East Sugarland Highway followed on September 25, 1998. Downtown LaBelle’s historic core, the 300 block of North Bridge Street, gained district status March 25, 1999. Clewiston residential landmarks include the Capt. F. Deane Duff House at 151 West Del Monte Avenue, listed January 30, 1998; the Executive House next door at 125 West Del Monte Avenue, listed February 5, 1998; and the Scharnberg House at 325 East Del Monte Avenue, listed April 26, 1999. The First Clewiston Post Office, 111-113 Bond Street, was added March 7, 2017. LaBelle commercial heritage is represented by the Forrey Building and Annex, 264-282 Bridge Street, listed July 28, 1995. The Capt. Francis A. Hendry House, 512 Fraser Street, LaBelle, and the Old Hendry County Courthouse at the junction of Bridge Street and Hickpochee Avenue, LaBelle, were both listed February 5, 1998, and November 8, 1990, respectively.
