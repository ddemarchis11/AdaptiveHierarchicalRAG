Rosi Mittermaier dominated Alpine skiing for West Germany, winning gold in the women’s downhill and slalom and adding silver in the giant slalom. Wolfgang Zimmerer and Manfred Schumann teamed up for silver in the two-man bobsleigh, then joined Peter Utzschneider and Bodo Bittner to take bronze in the four-man event. Josef Fendt claimed silver in the men’s individual luge, while Hans Brandner and Balthasar Schwarm did likewise in the men’s doubles. Urban Hettich earned silver in the Nordic combined individual contest. The West German men’s national ice hockey squad—featuring Erich Weißhaupt, Anton Kehle, Rudolf Thanner, Josef Volk, Udo Kiessling, Stefan Metz, Klaus Auhuber, Ignaz Berndaner, Rainer Phillip, Lorenz Funk, Wolfgang Boos, Ernst Köpf, Ferenc Vozar, Walter Koberle, Erich Kühnhackl, Alois Schloder, Martin Hinterstocker and Franz Reindl—took bronze in the men’s competition, and Elisabeth Demleitner added another bronze in the women’s individual luge.
