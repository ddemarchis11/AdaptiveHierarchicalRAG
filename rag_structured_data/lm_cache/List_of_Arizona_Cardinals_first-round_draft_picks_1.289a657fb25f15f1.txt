In 1960 the Cardinals used the second overall pick on Notre Dame quarterback George Izo, then followed with Auburn tackle Ken Rice at eighth overall in 1961. They doubled up in 1962, grabbing Northwestern defensive tackle Fate Echols sixth and Kentucky center Irv Goode twelfth, and did the same in 1963 when LSU safety Jerry Stovall went second and Purdue defensive end Don Brumm thirteenth. Louisville defensive tackle Ken Kortas was the ninth selection in 1964, Alabama quarterback Joe Namath the twelfth in 1965, Oklahoma linebacker Carl McAdams the eighth in 1966, and Washington wide receiver Dave Williams the sixteenth in 1967. Utah State running back MacArthur Lane arrived thirteenth in 1968, Missouri defensive back Roger Wehrli nineteenth in 1969, Texas A&M running back Larry Stegent eighth in 1970, and Utah cornerback Norm Thompson seventeenth in 1971. Oregon hybrid back Ahmad Rashad was the fourth pick in 1972, Purdue defensive tackle Dave Butz fifth in 1973, Colorado tight end J. V. Cain seventh in 1974, Texas A&M defensive back Tim Gray twenty-first in 1975, Arizona defensive tackle Mike Dawson twenty-second in 1976, and Missouri quarterback Steve Pisarkiewicz nineteenth in 1977.
