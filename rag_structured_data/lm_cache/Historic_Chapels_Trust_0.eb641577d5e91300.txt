Farfield Friends Meeting House, erected in Addingham, West Yorkshire in 1689 on an earlier burial ground, is a modest Quaker meeting house whose forecourt still holds five rare chest tombs, and it is listed Grade II*. Walpole Old Chapel, Suffolk, likewise built in 1689 from a converted farmhouse after the Act of Toleration, served Independents, then Congregationalists, then Primitive Methodists, and is also Grade II*. Cote Baptist Chapel, Bampton, Oxfordshire, built 1703-04 for Baptists from beyond the Thames, was enlarged in the 1750s, restored in the 1850s and 1990s, and is now hired for weddings and concerts, Grade II*. Salem Chapel, East Budleigh, Devon, begun in 1719 for Presbyterians, later housed Congregationalists and Assemblies of God, and is still used for occasional services and events, Grade II*. Coanwood Friends Meeting House, isolated near Haltwhistle, Northumberland, built 1720, retains its original benches and typical Quaker gravestones commemorating the founding Wigham family, Grade II*. Grittleton Strict Baptist Chapel, Wiltshire, opened 1721, keeps its galleries, box pews, child’s pew and pulpit, Grade II*. St George’s German Lutheran Church, Alie Street, London, 1762-63, served Lutherans until 1996 and now hosts concerts and the Trust’s offices, Grade II*. St Benet’s Chapel, Netherton, Merseyside, 1793, hides behind a presbytery and is being restored to pre-Vatican II appearance, Grade II*. Bethesda Methodist Chapel, Hanley, Staffordshire, 1819, once the “Cathedral of the Potteries,” closed 1985 and is undergoing £2.5 million repairs, Grade II*. Biddlestone Chapel, Northumberland, c. 1820, built as a private Roman Catholic chapel for the Selby family on a medieval pele tower, survives alone in remote countryside, Grade II*. Dissenters’ Chapel, Kensal Green Cemetery, London, 1832, the first Nonconformist chapel in a public cemetery, had its wings rebuilt after 1970s decay, Grade II*. Thorndon Park Chapel, Essex, c. 1850, William Wardell’s Decorated-style chantry and mausoleum for the Petre family, features angel-roof and carved reredos, Grade II*. Wainsgate Baptist Church, Hebden Bridge, West Yorkshire, 1859-60, elevated above the town, closed 2001 and now hosts concerts, Grade II*. Todmorden Unitarian Church, West Yorkshire, 1865-69, John Gibson’s 196-ft spired Gothic monument by the Fielden mill-owners, restored for £1 million, Grade I. Westgate Methodist Chapel, Bishop Auckland, County Durham, 1871, Primitive Methodist, closed 2007 with all Victorian fittings intact, Grade II*. Umberslade Baptist Church, Hockley Heath, West Midlands, 1877, George Ingall’s blue-lias Decorated church for G. F. Muntz, repaired for £500,000, Grade II. Penrose Methodist Chapel, St Ervan, Cornwall, 1861, single-storey rectangle with box pews and musicians’ benches, Grade II*. Longworth Roman Catholic Chapel, Bartestree, Herefordshire, rebuilt 1869-70 from a medieval manor chapel, probably by E. W. Pugin, Grade II*. Wallasey Memorial Unitarian Church, Merseyside, 1899, Arts and Crafts brick church by Waring & Rathbone with Bromsgrove Guild Art-Nouveau fittings, Grade II*. Shrine of Our Lady of Lourdes, Blackpool, Lancashire, 1955-57, Portland-stone thanksgiving for wartime safety, now becoming a community centre, Grade II*.
