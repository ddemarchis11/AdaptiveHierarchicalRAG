Indiana’s Hoosiers play on Bill Armstrong Stadium in Bloomington, a 6,500-seat venue opened in 1981, while Minnesota’s Golden Gophers occupy the 1,000-capacity Elizabeth Lyle Robbie Stadium in Falcon Heights since 1999. Michigan State’s Spartans moved into the 2,500-seat DeMartin Soccer Complex in Lansing in 2008, and Penn State’s Nittany Lions have competed at 5,000-seat Jeffrey Field in State College since 1966. Ohio State’s Buckeyes honor Jesse Owens in the 10,000-seat Jesse Owens Memorial Stadium, which debuted in Columbus in 2001, and Wisconsin’s Badgers have used Madison’s 1,611-seat McClimon Soccer Complex since 1959. Northwestern’s Wildcats relocated to the 20,000-seat SeatGeek Stadium in Bridgeview, Illinois, in 2006, making it the largest soccer-specific home among these programs. Michigan’s Wolverines opened the 2,200-seat U-M Soccer Stadium in Ann Arbor in 2010, Rutgers’ Scarlet Knights have played at 5,000-seat Yurcak Field in Piscataway since 1994, and Maryland’s Terrapins have occupied 7,000-seat Ludwig Field in College Park since 1995.
