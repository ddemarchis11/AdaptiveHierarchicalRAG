Alnwick Castle in Alnwick is a multi-themed attraction offering castle tours, a Napoleonic Wars military museum, the Fusiliers Museum of Northumberland, British and Irish archaeology, and 19th-century vehicles. Armstrong’s Household and Farming Museum in North Charlton displays antique household and farming items in room settings, including a toy-filled nursery, World War II memorabilia, a milking parlour, washing room, agricultural tools, and local needlework. Aydon Castle in Aydon, run by English Heritage, is a 13th-century manor house castle. Bailiffgate Museum in Alnwick covers local history, culture, fishing, agriculture, railways, mining, and art. Bamburgh Castle in Bamburgh, still inhabited since Norman times, houses a museum on engines, technology, and the industrialist William Armstrong, 1st Baron Armstrong. Bellingham Heritage Centre in Bellingham explores local history, the Border Counties Railway, the Border Reivers, mining, farming, a recreated smithy, and rural-life photographs. Belsay Castle in Belsay, managed by English Heritage, is a 14th-century medieval castle, while Belsay Hall in Belsay is a 19th-century unfurnished country mansion with gardens and park, also under English Heritage. Berwick Gymnasium Gallery in Berwick-upon-Tweed, within Berwick Barracks, hosts contemporary art installations by Berwick Visual Arts. Berwick Museum & Art Gallery in Berwick-upon-Tweed, likewise in Berwick Barracks, presents fine art, Asian porcelain, historic glass, medieval Berwick and castle stories, local and social history, trades, mining, and fishing. Blyth Battery in Blyth, a military site, preserves World War I and II coastal-defence buildings and displays military and local history. Cherryburn in Mickley, National Trust property and birthplace of artist-ornithologist Thomas Bewick, exhibits his life and engravings. Chillingham Castle in Chillingham is a medieval castle with state rooms. Chesters Roman Fort in Walwick, English Heritage-run, is a preserved Roman fort on Hadrian’s Wall with an artifact museum. Corbridge Roman Site in Corbridge, also English Heritage, is a preserved Roman fort south of Hadrian’s Wall with artifacts. Cragside in Rothbury, National Trust site, is the 19th-century technologically innovative country home of Lord Armstrong. The Fusiliers Museum of Northumberland is located inside Alnwick Castle. George Stephenson’s Birthplace in Wylam, a National Trust 18th-century stone cottage, commemorates the rail pioneer with railway exhibits. The Grace Darling Museum in Bamburgh, operated by the RNLI, tells the story of Victorian heroine Grace Darling, who with her father rescued 13 people from the SS Forfarshire in 1838. Heatherslaw Mill in Etal is a 19th-century water-driven corn mill.
