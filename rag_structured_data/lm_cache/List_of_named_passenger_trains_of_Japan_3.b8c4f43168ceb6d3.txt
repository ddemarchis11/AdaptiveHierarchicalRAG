Akatsuki linked Kyoto to Nagasaki from 1965 until JR-West withdrew it in March 2008, while JR East’s Akebono ran nightly between Ueno and Aomori from 1970 to January 2015. JR-West’s Asakaze connected Tokyo with Shimonoseki for nearly half a century, ceasing in March 2005, and the same operator’s Twilight Express carried sleeper passengers from Ōsaka to Sapporo between 1989 and March 2015. JR East’s Cassiopeia offered luxury overnight travel from Ueno to Sapporo from 1999 to March 2016, and jointly with JR Hokkaido the Hokutosei plied the same route from 1988 until August 2015. JR Kyushu’s Dream Nichirin served Hakata to Minami-Miyazaki and Miyazaki Airport from 1993 to 2011, while its Fuji linked Tokyo with Ōita from 1964 to March 2009 and the Hayabusa ran from Tokyo to Kumamoto between 1958 and March 2009. The Naha connected Kyoto to Kumamoto from 1968 to March 2008, and the Sakura spanned Tokyo to Nagasaki from 1959 to March 2005. JR East’s Hokuriku ran between Ueno and Kanazawa from 1947 to March 2010, and its Izumo reached Izumoshi via Tottori from 1972 to March 2006. JR Hokkaido’s Marimo linked Sapporo and Kushiro from 2001 to 2008, the Rishiri joined Sapporo with Wakkanai from 2000 to 2007, and the Okhotsk ran daytime service from Sapporo to Abashiri from 1992 to March 2006. JR-West and JR East jointly operated the Nihonkai between Ōsaka and Aomori from 1968 to March 2012, while JR-West’s Suisei connected Kyoto to Minami-Miyazaki from 1968 to 2005. Since 1998 JR Central and JR-West have jointly run the Sunrise Izumo from Tokyo to Izumoshi via Okayama and the Sunrise Seto from Tokyo to Takamatsu, both still in service.
