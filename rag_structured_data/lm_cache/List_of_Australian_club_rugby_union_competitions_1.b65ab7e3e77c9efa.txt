Bond University, founded on the Gold Coast in 1996, entered the top flight as the Breakers in 1997. Brothers, the 1905-established Albion club nicknamed the Butcher Bhoys, the Brethren and the Filth, has been in premier grade since its first season. Eastern Districts, formed in 1947 at Coorparoo, became the Tigers or Easts and waited until 1950 for admission. GPS Old Boys, the 1887 Ashgrove outfit known as Jeeps, joined premier grade in 1931. North Brisbane, created in 1931 at Wooloowin, competed as Eagle Junction in 1933, Teachers in 1961 and Teachers-Norths in 1974 before the Eagles or Norths finally secured premier status in 1991. University of Queensland, the 1911 St Lucia club called the Red Heavies, stepped up in 1912. Southern Districts, the 1948 Annerley Magpies or Souths, entered premier grade the same year they were founded. Sunnybank, born in 1969, became the Dragons and won admission in 1989. West Brisbane, the 1951 Toowong Bulldogs or Wests, joined premier grade in 1955.
