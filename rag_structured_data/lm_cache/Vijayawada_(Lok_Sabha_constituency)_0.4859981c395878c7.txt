Harindranath Chattopadhyaya won the inaugural 1952 Vijayawada seat as an Independent. Dr. Komarraju Atchamamba took it for Indian National Congress in 1957, after which K. L. Rao secured three straight victories for the same party in 1962, 1967 and 1971. Godey Murahari kept the seat with Indian National Congress in 1977, followed by Chennupati Vidya under the Indian National Congress (I) banner in 1980. Vadde Sobhanadreeswara Rao switched it to Telugu Desam Party in 1984, lost it back to Chennupati Vidya of Indian National Congress in 1989, then regained it for Telugu Desam Party in 1991. Parvathaneni Upendra gave Indian National Congress two consecutive wins in 1996 and 1998, before Gadde Ramamohan returned the seat to Telugu Desam Party in 1999. Rajagopal Lagadapati restored Indian National Congress fortunes in 2004 and repeated the feat in 2009. Finally, Kesineni Srinivas captured the constituency for Telugu Desam Party in both 2014 and 2019.
