Vladimir Mukhanov led Aktobe from Central Stadium, which holds 13,200, while Bernd Storck coached Almaty in the same 23,804-seat Central Stadium shared by Vakhid Masudov’s Kairat and Vladimir Gulyamkhaidarov’s Megasport. Vyacheslav Ledovskih directed Astana at the 12,350-capacity Kazhymukan Munaitpasov Stadium, Sergey Andreyev handled Atyrau at 9,500-seat Munaishy Stadium, and Vladimir Linchevskiy guided Energetic-2 from Ekibastuz’s 6,300-seat Shakhter. Evgeny Yarovenko’s Esil Bogatyr played for Petropavl at 11,000-seat Karasai Stadium, Oirat Saduov’s Irtysh represented Pavlodar in the 15,000-seat Central Stadium, and Vladimir Nikitenko’s Kaisar performed in Kyzylorda’s 7,000-seat Gani Muratbayev Stadium. Viktor Rymar’s Okzhetpes used Kokshetau’s 4,158-seat Okzhetpes Stadium, Jovica Nikolić’s Ordabasy filled Shymkent’s 20,000-seat Kazhimukan Munaitpasov Stadium, Ivan Azovskiy’s Shakhter Karagandy occupied Karagandy’s 20,000-seat Shakhter Stadium, Dmitriy Ogai’s Tobol staged matches in Kostanay’s 8,323-seat Central Stadium, Oleksandr Holokolosov’s Vostok welcomed fans to Oskemen’s 8,500-seat Vostok Stadium, and Ilie Karp’s Zhetysu competed in Taldykorgan’s 4,000-seat Zhetysu Stadium.
