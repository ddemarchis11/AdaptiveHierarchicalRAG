Finnish long-distance runner Kaarlo Maaninka admitted in 1980 to using blood transfusions, a practice not yet banned, and received no sanction. Czechoslovakian shot-putter Remigius Machura first tested positive for stanozolol in 1985, confessed to earlier anabolic-steroid use dating back to 1981, and had a life ban cut to two years; in 2010, competing for the Czech Republic, he drew another two-year ban after a HGH violation. British javelin thrower Colin Mackenzie served a three-month ban in 1994, while Canadian counterpart Mike Mahovlich’s 1986 case is listed without detail. South African sprinter Simon Magakwe received two years in 2014 for refusing doping control, and compatriot long-jumper Luvo Manyonga got 18 months in 2012 for methamphetamine. Brazilian long-jumper Maurren Maggi tested positive for clostebol in 2003 and sat out two years. Portuguese pole-vaulter Edi Maia drew only a public warning and event disqualification in 2008 for cannabis. Swiss sprinter Pascal Mancini was publicly warned in 2011 for undisclosed norandrosterone and banned for two years in 2012. Russian middle-distance runner Irina Maracheva earned a two-year sanction in 2012 via biological-passport anomalies. French hurdler Pascal Maran received a public warning and disqualification in 1997 for ephedrine, while Romanian middle-distance runner Natalia Mărășescu served eight months in 1979 for anabolic steroids. Dutch sprinter Brian Mariano drew four years in 2016 for stanozolol, Croatian discus thrower Martin Marić two years in 2014 for the SARM ostarine, and Israeli high-jumper Itay Margalit was sanctioned in 1999 for nandrolone. Moldovan hammer throwers Marina and Zalina Marghieva tested positive for stanozolol in 2012 and for dehydrochloromethyltestosterone plus stanozolol in 2009 (retested 2013), each incurring two-year bans. Azerbaijani hammer thrower Dzmitry Marshin received four years in 2015 for dehydrochloromethyltestosterone, and American hurdler LaVonna Martin-Floreal had her 1991 furosemide sanction reduced from two years to 14 months.
