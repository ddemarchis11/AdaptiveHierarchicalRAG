Aston Medical School, opened in 2015, confers the MBChB, while Anglia Ruskin School of Medicine welcomed its first 100-student cohort in 2018 for the same degree. Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry, tracing teaching to 1123 and formally established in 1785, awards the MBBS after merging the historic Medical College of St Bartholomew’s and London Hospital Medical College under Queen Mary. Birmingham Medical School, dating from 1825, and Bristol Medical School, from 1833, both grant MBChB after their respective mergers with Mason Science College and University College, Bristol. Brighton and Sussex Medical School, created jointly by the universities of Brighton and Sussex in 2002, offers the BMBS. Cambridge’s School of Clinical Medicine, re-established in 1976 after centuries of intermittent teaching since 1540, awards the MB BChir. UCLan School of Medicine in Preston, recruiting international students since 2015 and UK-funded students from 2018, grants the MBBS. Exeter Medical School, formed in 2013 from the split of Peninsula College, awards the BMBS, as does the 2020-founded Kent and Medway Medical School, a collaboration between Kent and Canterbury Christchurch supervised by Brighton and Sussex. Hull York Medical School, affiliated with both Hull and York since 2003, confers the MBBS. Imperial College School of Medicine, established in 1997 from multiple mergers including Charing Cross Hospital (1818), awards the MBBS. Keele, beginning undergraduate clinical teaching in 2003 and awarding Manchester’s MBChB until 2012, now grants its own MBChB. King’s College London School of Medicine and Dentistry, merging in 1998 with roots at St Thomas’s from 1550, awards the MBBS. Lancaster Medical School, initially awarding Liverpool’s MBChB via a consortium, has granted Lancaster’s own MBChB since 2013. Leeds (1831), Leicester (1975), Liverpool (1834) and Manchester (1824, with teaching from 1752) all confer the MBChB, while Lincoln Medical School, anticipated in 2019, will award the MBBS.
