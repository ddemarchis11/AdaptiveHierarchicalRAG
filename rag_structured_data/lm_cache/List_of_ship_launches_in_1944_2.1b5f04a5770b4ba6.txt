On 1 March 1944 Permanente Metals’ #1 Yard at Richmond, California launched the Liberty ship John Roach, and the next day Todd Houston Shipbuilding Corporation in Houston, Texas added Anna H. Branch while Permanente Metals’ #2 Yard sent Henry M. Stephens down the ways. 3 March was busier: Walsh-Kaiser Company at Providence, Rhode Island launched Jesse H. Metcalf, Southeastern Shipbuilding Corporation at Savannah, Georgia launched John E. Sweet, Bethlehem-Fairfield Shipyards at Baltimore launched Sameden, and Kaiser Shipyards at Vancouver, Washington slid the Casablanca-class escort carrier Cape Esperance into the water. 4 March saw eight launches—Frederick C. Hicks by California Shipbuilding Corporation in Los Angeles, George Steers again by Todd Houston in Houston, Harry Toulmin by Delta Shipbuilding Company in New Orleans, the Great Lakes icebreaker Mackinaw by American Ship Building Company in Toledo, Ohio, Rebecca Lukens by J.A. Jones Construction in Panama City, Florida, Samcolne by Bethlehem-Fairfield in Baltimore, Stanton H. King by New England Shipbuilding in South Portland, Maine, and Willet M. Hays by Permanente Metals’ #2 Yard in Richmond. On 6 March Delta Shipbuilding in New Orleans launched Cecil N. Bean, Permanente Metals’ #2 Yard launched Edward E. Hale, J.A. Jones Construction in Brunswick, Georgia launched Isaac Shelby, Oregon Shipbuilding in Portland launched Netherlands Victory, and California Shipbuilding in Los Angeles launched United States Victory, the last two being Victory ships.
