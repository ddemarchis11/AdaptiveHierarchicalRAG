Barking and Dagenham, Havering and Redbridge share the North East sub-region and the City and East or Havering and Redbridge Assembly seats, while Barnet and Camden jointly represent the North, Barnet partnering Camden in the Barnet and Camden constituency. Bexley and Bromley co-represent the South East, mirrored by the Bexley and Bromley Assembly seat, whereas Croydon and Sutton share both the South West sub-region and the Croydon and Sutton seat. Brent and Harrow cover the North West and West, served by the Brent and Harrow seat, and Ealing and Hillingdon pair in the West under the Ealing and Hillingdon constituency. Enfield and Haringey span the North, united in the Enfield and Haringey seat, and Hounslow and Kingston upon Thames join the South West, Hounslow falling within the South West Assembly area. Inner London’s Camden, Hackney, Islington, Hammersmith and Fulham, Kensington and Chelsea, and Greenwich each align with their sub-regions—North, West, South East—and corresponding Assembly seats: Barnet and Camden, North East, West Central, or Greenwich and Lewisham.
