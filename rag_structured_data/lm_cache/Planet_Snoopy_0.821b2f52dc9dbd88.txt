Cedar Point welcomed Planet Snoopy on 12 May 2008, replacing Peanuts Playground with eight kid-sized rides. California’s Great America followed on 28 March 2010, swapping both Nickelodeon Central and, five years later, KidZville for a fifteen-ride Peanuts zone. Canada’s Wonderland opened its fourteen-ride version on 1 May 2010, simultaneously retiring Nickelodeon Central and The Happy Land of Hanna-Barbera. Kings Dominion launched twenty attractions on 2 April 2010 after phasing out Nickelodeon Central that year and KidZville in 2013, while Kings Island debuted nineteen rides on 17 April 2010 in place of Nickelodeon Universe. Dorney Park & Wildwater Kingdom unveiled sixteen attractions on 4 May 2011 on the former Camp Snoopy site, Valleyfair introduced seventeen rides on 14 May 2011 after removing KidWorks, and Worlds of Fun presented seventeen attractions on 16 April 2011, likewise replacing Camp Snoopy.
