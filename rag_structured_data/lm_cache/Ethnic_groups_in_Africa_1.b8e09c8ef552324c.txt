The Akan of Ghana and Ivory Coast speak a Niger-Congo Kwa language and number 20 million. In the Horn of Africa, Abyssinians span Ethiopia and Eritrea with Afro-Asiatic Semitic speech, while their Amhara kin within Ethiopia alone total 22 million (2007). Central Africa’s Chewa, Malawi and Zambia’s 12-million-strong Niger-Congo Bantu speakers, neighbour the 15-million Hutu of Rwanda, Burundi and DR Congo. West Africa’s Fulani, a 20-million-strong Niger-Congo Senegambian people, range from Mauritania to Sierra Leone, whereas the Hausa, 43 million Afro-Asiatic Chadic speakers, dominate Nigeria and Niger and spill into seven further states. Nigeria’s Igbo, 34 million (2017) Niger-Congo Volta-Niger speakers, share the region with the Yoruba, whose 40 million members extend from Nigeria across Benin, Togo, Ghana, Ivory Coast and Sierra Leone. Kanuri, Nilo-Saharan Saharan speakers, count 10 million in Nigeria, Niger, Chad and Cameroon. Central Africa’s Kongo, Luba and Mongo each claim 10 or 15 million Niger-Congo Bantu speakers in DR Congo and Angola. North Africa’s Maghrebis, 100 million Afro-Asiatic Semites, blanket Mauritania to Libya. Nilotes, 22 million (2007) Nilo-Saharan Nilotic speakers, stretch from South Sudan to Ethiopia. Oromo, 35 million (2016) Afro-Asiatic Cushites, and Somali, 20 million (2009) Cushites, share the Horn with Kenya. Shona, 15 million (2000) Niger-Congo Bantoid people, straddle Zimbabwe and Mozambique, while 12 million (2016) Zulu complete the arc in South Africa.
