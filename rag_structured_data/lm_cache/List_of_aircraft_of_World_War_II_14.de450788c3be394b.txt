The US fielded the Aeronca L-3 in 1941, while its earlier Barkley-Grow T8P-1 dated from 1937 and the twin-beech Beechcraft 17 Staggerwing, also known as the UC-43 Traveler, had been flying since 1933; the larger Beechcraft Model 18 family—C-45, JRB, SNB, AT-7 and AT-11 Expeditor—followed in 1937, and the Cessna 165 appeared in 1935. Britain’s Airspeed Envoy entered service in 1934 and the Avro 652 in 1935. Italy’s wartime inventory included the AVIA FL.3 (1939), the Breda Ba.44 (1934) and the Caproni Ca.164 (1938). France relied on a string of Caudron types: the C.272 from 1931, the C.280/C.400/C.410 series from 1932, the C.440 Goéland from 1934, and the C.480 Frégate, C.600 Aiglon and C.635 Simoun all from 1935, plus the Bloch MB.81 ambulance aircraft and MB.120 mailplane, likewise dating from 1935. Czechoslovakia contributed the Beneš-Mráz Be-555 Super Bibi in 1936, and Brazil the CAP CAP-4 Paulista in 1935.
