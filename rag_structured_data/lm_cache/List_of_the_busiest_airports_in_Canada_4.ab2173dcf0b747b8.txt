Toronto Pearson International Airport leads Canada with 44,335,198 passengers, up 8.0%, serving Greater Toronto. Vancouver International follows with 22,288,926 passengers, a 9.7% rise, for Metro Vancouver. Montréal-Trudeau ranks third, carrying 16,589,067 travelers, 6.9% more than the previous year, for Greater Montreal. Calgary International handled 15,680,616 passengers, growing 1.3%, for the Calgary Region. Edmonton International welcomed 7,523,864 passengers, up 5.7%, to the Edmonton Metropolitan Region. Ottawa Macdonald-Cartier served 4,743,091, a 1.9% increase, for the National Capital Region. Winnipeg Richardson saw 4,015,200 passengers, 6.3% higher, for the Winnipeg Capital Region. Halifax Stanfield accommodated 3,908,799, up 5.6%, for Halifax. Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport, on the downtown island, carried 2,700,000 passengers, surging 9.0%. Victoria International reached 1,856,421 travelers, 8.5% more, for Greater Victoria. Kelowna International climbed to 1,732,113 passengers, 8.7% growth, for Kelowna and the Okanagan Valley. Québec City Jean Lesage handled 1,615,750, up 2.0%. St. John’s International served 1,600,000, rising 4.3%. Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker carried 1,450,000, edging up 0.5%. Regina International welcomed 1,262,899, up 0.6%. Thunder Bay International reached 807,041 passengers, 4.5% higher. Fort McMurray International soared 32.3% to 744,798. Greater Moncton Roméo LeBlanc handled 657,272, up 2.0%. Yellowknife Airport served 575,000, climbing 7.0%. Abbotsford International closed the top twenty with 530,643 passengers, up 8.8%, for Abbotsford and the Fraser Valley.
