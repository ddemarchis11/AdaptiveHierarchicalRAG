Addaura cave rises 70 m above sea level in Palermo, Sicily, while the Ear of Dionysius, also in Syracuse, Sicily, reaches 400 m and extends only 65 m. Along the Ligurian coast, Arene Candide sits at 90 m in Finale Ligure and Borgio Verezzi cave at 200 m in its namesake village. Tuscany hosts Antro del Corchia at 600 m in Stazzema, Giusti cave 200 m long at 570 m in Monsummano Terme, and Maona, likewise 200 m, at 255 m in Montecatini Terme. Trentino-Alto Adide’s Grotto Calgeron lies at 400 m in Grigno, the Blue Grotto opens at sea level off Anacapri in Campania, and Castelcivita cave stretches 3 km beneath 94 m in Campania’s Castelcivte, mirrored by Castellana’s 3 km at 290 m in Apulia. Sardinia offers Bue Marino and Is Zuddas at sea level in Dorgali and Santadi, Ispinigoli perched at 1,300 m in Dorgali, and Neptune cave at 5 m in Alghero. Abruzzo’s Cavallone cave runs 1 km at 1,300 m between Lama dei Peligni and Taranta Peligna, Sicily’s Gelo cave crowns 2,043 m in Randazzo, Lauro cave sits at 1,068 m in Alcara li Fusi, while Marche’s Frasassi system winds 5 km at 300 m in Genga and Friuli’s Giant cave measures 280 m at 265 m in Sgonico.
