Ian Paisley led the Protestant Unionist Party from 1966 to 1971 and then the Democratic Unionist Party until 2008, representing Bannside and North Antrim for 42 years. Ruairí Ó Brádaigh headed Sinn Féin from 1970 to 1983 and later Republican Sinn Féin from 1986 to 2009, totaling 36 years of leadership. Gerry Adams succeeded him at the helm of Sinn Féin from 1983 to 2018, representing West Belfast and later Louth for 35 years. William Norton led the Irish Labour Party from 1932 to 1960, a span of 28 years. Tomás Mac Giolla successively chaired Sinn Féin from 1962 to 1970, Official Sinn Féin until 1977, Sinn Féin The Workers Party until 1982, and finally the Workers' Party until 1988, amounting to 26 years. Hugh Smyth headed the Progressive Unionist Party from 1979 to 2002, a 23-year tenure. John Hume led the Social Democratic and Labour Party from 1979 to 2001, representing Foyle for 22 years. Albert McElroy chaired the Ulster Liberal Party from 1956 to about 1975, roughly 19 years. Lord Craigavon led the Ulster Unionist Party from 1921 to 1940, representing Down and North Down for 19 years. John Redmond headed the Irish Parliamentary Party from 1900 to 1918, representing Waterford City for 18 years. Lord Brookeborough led the Ulster Unionist Party from 1946 to 1963, representing Lisnaskea for 17 years. James Molyneaux chaired the same party from 1979 to 1995, representing South Antrim and Lagan Valley for 16 years. Joseph Devlin led the Nationalist Party from 1918 to 1934, representing Belfast Falls, Fermanagh and Tyrone, and Belfast Central for 16 years. Gerry Fitt first led the Republican Labour Party from 1964 to 1970 and then the Social Democratic and Labour Party until 1979, representing Belfast West and Belfast Dock for 15 years. James Kilfedder headed the Ulster Popular Unionist Party from 1980 to 1995, representing North Down for 15 years. Margaret Buckley led Sinn Féin from 1937 to 1950, a 13-year period. Robert McCartney chaired the UK Unionist Party from 1995 to 2008, representing North Down for 13 years. James McSparran led the Nationalist Party from 1945 to 1958, representing Mourne for 13 years. Oliver Napier headed the Alliance Party from 1972 to 1984, a 12-year span. Thomas Joseph Campbell led the Nationalist Party from 1934 to 1945, representing Belfast Central for 11 years.
