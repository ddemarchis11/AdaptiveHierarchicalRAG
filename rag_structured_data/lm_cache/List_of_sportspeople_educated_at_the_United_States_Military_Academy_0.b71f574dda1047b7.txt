Abner Doubleday, class of 1842, became a Civil War major general and the apocryphal inventor of baseball. Guy Henry, 1898, won equestrian bronze at the 1912 Olympics while earning two Army Distinguished Service Medals and the Silver Star across four wars. Paul Bunker, 1903, earned All-America football honors in 1901-02, was retroactively awarded the 1902 Heisman by Sports Illustrated, and entered the College Football Hall of Fame. George S. Patton, 1909, placed fifth in the 1912 Olympic modern pentathlon before commanding armies from North Africa to the Rhine. Elmer Oliphant, 1918, set a world record in the 220-yard low hurdles and starred as one of college football’s greatest players. P.C. Hains, 1924, competed in the 1928 Olympic modern pentathlon after graduating. John Roosma, 1926, became a Basketball Hall of Famer and namesake of West Point’s MVP award. Robin Olds, 1943, flew in World War II and Vietnam, earned the Air Force Cross and four Silver Stars, and joined the College Football Hall of Fame. Doc Blanchard, 1947, won the 1945 Heisman, Maxwell and Sullivan awards as “Mr. Inside.” Glenn Davis, 1947, captured the 1946 Heisman as “Mr. Outside” after the 1944 Maxwell. James Hartinger, 1949, flew in Korea and Vietnam, entered the National Lacrosse Hall of Fame, and inspired the Hartinger Medal for space contributions. Dan Foldberg, 1951, spurned the Detroit Lions to become an infantry colonel after earning All-America honors in football and lacrosse. Bill Carpenter, 1959, earned the Distinguished Service Cross in Vietnam and football immortality as the “Lonesome End.” Pete Dawkins, 1959, uniquely combined Brigade Commander, class president, football captain, top-five academic standing, 1958 Heisman and Maxwell awards, Rhodes Scholarship, Princeton Ph.D., and two Bronze Stars in Vietnam. Ronald Zinn, 1962, placed sixth in the 1964 Olympic racewalk before dying in Vietnam in 1965. Mike Silliman, 1966, captained the 1968 Olympic gold-medal basketball team. Napoleon McCallum, 1985, became a Raiders draft pick. Michael Thornberry, 1994, competed in handball at the 1996 Olympics. Dan Browne, 1997, won the 2002 U.S. Marathon title and raced 10 km and marathon at the 2004 Olympics. Ronnie McAda, 1997, became “Mr. Irrelevant” as the final 1997 NFL draft pick by Green Bay.
