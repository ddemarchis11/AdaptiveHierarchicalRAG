S. P. B. Charan produced Samuthirakani’s 2003 drama Unnai Charanadainthen, casting himself alongside Venkat Prabhu. Two years later he backed S. Rajkumar’s romantic Mazhai starring Jayam Ravi and Shriya Saran. In 2007 Charan’s Capital Film Works released Venkat Prabhu’s cricket-comedy Chennai 600028, assembling Jai, Nithin Sathya, Shiva, Premji Amaren and Vijayalakshmi. Rajamohan’s 2009 debut Kunguma Poovum Konjum Puravum followed, featuring newcomers Ramakrishnan and Tharshana. Charan then produced Shakthi’s 2010 heist thriller Naanayam with Prasanna and Sibiraj, and Thiagarajan Kumararaja’s National-Award-winning neo-noir Aaranya Kaandam in 2011, headlined by Jackie Shroff, Ravi Krishna and Sampath Raj. Caarthick Raju’s 2014 cop-comedy Thirudan Police brought Attakathi Dinesh and Iyshwarya Rajesh together under the banner. In 2015 Charan reunited his father S. P. Balasubrahmanyam with Lakshmi, K. Bhagyaraj and fresh faces Arjun Chidambaram, Venkatesh Harinathan and Aditi Chengappa for Madhumitha’s Moone Moonu Varthai. Finally, 2016 saw the reunion of the original Chennai 600028 ensemble—Jai, Nithin Sathya, Shiva, Premji Amaren and Vijayalakshmi—under Venkat Prabhu once more for the sequel Chennai 600028 II.
