Nintendo launched the Wii U Virtual Console in North America on April 26, 2013 with its own Nintendo EAD’s Super Mario World and F-Zero, followed on May 15 by Nintendo R&D1’s Super Metroid. Capcom entered the lineup on May 16 with Capcom-developed Super Ghouls ’n Ghosts, while HAL Laboratory delivered three Kirby titles—Kirby Super Star, Kirby’s Dream Course and Kirby’s Dream Land 3—on May 23. Capcom returned May 30 with Mega Man X, and HAL’s Vegas Stakes arrived June 27. Nintendo EAD’s Pilotwings cleared the runway July 4, and the HAL/Ape collaboration EarthBound landed July 18 with a Teen rating. Natsume published Amccus’s Harvest Moon on August 1, Koei Tecmo issued Koei’s Romance of the Three Kingdoms IV: Wall of Fire on August 8, and Capcom released three Teen-rated Street Fighter II variants—The World Warrior, Turbo: Hyper Fighting and Super: The New Challengers—on August 22. Capcom’s Breath of Fire II followed September 5, and the company capped the list October 3 with three Final Fight entries: the Everyone-rated Final Fight, the Everyone 10+ Final Fight 2, and the Teen-rated Final Fight 3.
