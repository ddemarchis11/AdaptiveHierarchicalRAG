Davenport House at 157 Davenport Road entered the National Register on 30 April 1980, followed on 7 September 1979 by the paired landmark of First Presbyterian Church and Lewis Pintard House fronting Pintard Avenue. The Knickerbocker Press Building, 50-52 Webster Avenue, gained recognition 11 May 2000, while Leland Castle, 29 Castle Place, had already been listed 27 August 1976. Nearby, the Lispenard-Rodman-Davenport House, 180 Davenport Avenue, was added 22 September 1986. New Rochelle Railroad Station, standing between North Avenue and Memorial Highway, joined the register 14 October 2009, and the 20 Sicard Avenue home of Thomas Paine Cottage had been honored since 28 November 1972. Pioneer Building, 14 Lawton Street, was designated 29 December 1983, and the leafy streets of Rochelle Park-Rochelle Heights Historic District—The Circle, The Boulevard, The Serpentine, Hamilton Avenue—were collectively listed 6 July 2005. Trinity-St. Paul’s Episcopal Church, 311 Huguenot Street, followed a year later on 12 July 2006. The city’s main post office at 255 North Avenue was entered 11 May 1989, and the cliff-side mansion Wildcliff, 42 Wildcliff Road, rounded out the roster on 31 December 2002.
