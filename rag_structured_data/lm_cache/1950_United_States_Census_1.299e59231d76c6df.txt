New York City led the 1950 United States Census with 7,891,957 residents, followed by Chicago’s 3,620,962 in Illinois and Philadelphia’s 2,071,605 in Pennsylvania, while Los Angeles claimed 1,970,358 to rank fourth in California and Detroit counted 1,849,568 to rank fifth in Michigan. Baltimore, Maryland, held 949,708 people, Cleveland, Ohio, 914,808, and St. Louis, Missouri, 856,796, placing them sixth through eighth. Washington, District of Columbia, reached 802,178, barely edging Boston, Massachusetts, at 801,444 for ninth and tenth. San Francisco’s 775,357 ranked it eleventh in California, Pittsburgh twelfth with 676,806 in Pennsylvania, and Milwaukee thirteenth with 637,392 in Wisconsin. Houston, Texas, grew to 596,163, Buffalo, New York, held 580,132, and New Orleans, Louisiana, 570,445, occupying ranks fourteen through sixteen. Minneapolis, Minnesota, counted 521,718, Cincinnati, Ohio, 503,998, Seattle, Washington, 467,591, and Kansas City, Missouri, 456,622, closing the top twenty.
