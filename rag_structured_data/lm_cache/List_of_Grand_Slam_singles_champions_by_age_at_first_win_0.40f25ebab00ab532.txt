Ken Rosewall, already a champion before the Open Era, captured his first Open Era Slam at 33 years 220 days on 9 June 1968 at Roland-Garros, the first of four he would claim. Rod Laver, likewise marked with pre-Open credentials, began his five-Slam run at 29 years 332 days by winning Wimbledon on 6 July 1968. Arthur Ashe, born 10 July 1943, opened his three-title collection at 25 years 60 days during the 1968 US Open. Jan Kodeš, born 1 March 1946, earned the first of three Slams at 24 years 98 days in the 1970 French Open, while John Newcombe, another pre-Open star, started his five-title haul at 26 years 42 days on 4 July 1970 at Wimbledon. Stan Smith, born 14 December 1946, took the first of two Slams at 24 years 241 days in the 1971 US Open. Andrés Gimeno, born 3 August 1937, became the oldest first-time Open Era champion at 34 years 306 days on 4 June 1972 in Paris. Ilie Năstase, born 19 July 1946, secured the first of two titles at 26 years 53 days in the 1972 US Open. Jimmy Connors, born 2 September 1952, launched his eight-Slam streak at 21 years 121 days in the 1974 Australian Open, and Björn Borg, born 6 June 1956, announced his eleven-title reign at barely 18 years 10 days on 16 June 1974 at Roland-Garros. Manuel Orantes, born 5 February 1949, won his lone Slam at 26 years 214 days in the 1975 US Open. Mark Edmondson, born 24 June 1954, surprised the draw at 21 years 195 days in the 1976 Australian Open. Adriano Panatta, born 9 July 1950, claimed his only Slam at 25 years 340 days in the 1976 French Open. Roscoe Tanner, born 15 October 1951, struck in the January 1977 Australian Open at 25 years 87 days. Guillermo Vilas, born 17 August 1952, began his four-title run at 24 years 292 days in the 1977 French Open. Vitas Gerulaitis, born 26 July 1954, captured his sole Slam at 23 years 159 days in the December 1977 Australian Open. John McEnroe, born 16 February 1959, opened his seven-title account at 20 years 205 days in the 1979 US Open. Brian Teacher, born 23 December 1954, won his only Slam at 26 years 13 days in the 1980 Australian Open. Johan Kriek, born 5 April 1958, started his two-title tally at 23 years 273 days in the 1981 Australian Open. Finally, Mats Wilander, born 22 August 1964, became the youngest first-time champion at 17 years 293 days on 11 June 1982 at Roland-Garros, the first of seven he would collect.
