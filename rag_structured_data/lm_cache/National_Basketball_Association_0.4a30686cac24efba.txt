The Boston Celtics have captured 17 championships—1957, 1959-66, 1968-69, 1974, 1976, 1981, 1984, 1986 and 2008—while finishing second in 1958, 1985, 1987 and 2010, giving them 21 total Finals appearances and 4 losses. The Minneapolis/Los Angeles Lakers have gone 16-15 in 31 trips, winning in 1949-50, 1952-54, 1972, 1980, 1982, 1985, 1987-88, 2000-02 and 2009-10, and losing in 1959, 1962-63, 1965-66, 1968-70, 1973, 1983-84, 1989, 1991, 2004 and 2008. The Philadelphia/San Francisco/Golden State Warriors own 6 titles—1947, 1956, 1975, 2015, 2017-18—and 5 runner-up finishes in 1948, 1964, 1967, 2016 and 2019. Chicago’s perfect 6-0 record came in 1991-93 and 1996-98. San Antonio went 5-1 with crowns in 1999, 2003, 2005, 2007 and 2014, dropping only the 2013 Finals. Syracuse/Philadelphia 76ers won in 1955, 1967 and 1983 and lost in 1950, 1954, 1977, 1980, 1982 and 2001. Fort Wayne/Detroit Pistons triumphed in 1989-90 and 2004, falling in 1955-56, 1988 and 2005. Miami Heat earned 3 titles in 2006, 2012-13 and were runners-up in 2011 and 2014. New York Knicks won in 1970 and 1973, losing in 1951-53, 1972, 1994 and 1999. Houston Rockets repeated in 1994-95 after defeats in 1981 and 1986. Cleveland finally broke through in 2016 after losses in 2007, 2015, 2017 and 2018. St. Louis/Atlanta Hawks won their lone crown in 1958, losing in 1957, 1960 and 1961. Baltimore/Washington Bullets captured 1978, falling in 1971, 1975 and 1979. Seattle SuperSonics/Oklahoma City Thunder won 1979 and lost in 1978, 1996 and 2012. Portland Trail Blazers won 1977, lost 1990 and 1992. Milwaukee Bucks triumphed in 1971, fell in 1974. Dallas Mavericks reversed 2006 heartbreak with 2011 glory. Folded Baltimore Bullets, Rochester Royals and Toronto Raptors each own one perfect Finals appearance—1948, 1951 and 2019 respectively.
