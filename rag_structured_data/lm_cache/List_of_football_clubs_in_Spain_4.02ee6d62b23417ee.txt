Andorra, based in Encamp and playing at Prada de Molès, returned to the regional divisions in 2019-20 after first entering the national pyramid in 1980-81. Atlético Levante of Valencia, who host at Buñol, placed 11th last season and have been in their present division since 2018-19, having originally joined in 2004-05. Badalona, finishing 7th, have remained uninterrupted in the category since their 2004-05 debut at the Municipal de Badalona. Barcelona B, operating from the Johan Cruyff stadium, ended 8th and have occupied this level since 2018-19, though the reserve side first played nationally in 1977-78. Castellón, 15th last season, returned to this tier in 2018-19 after earlier campaigns dating back to 1994-95 at Nou Castàlia. Cornellà and Ebro, 4th and 9th respectively, have stayed since their 2014-15 promotions to the Nou Camp and La Almozara. Ejea, 14th, is a newcomer, entering in 2018-19 at the Municipal de Ejea. Espanyol B, 5th, rejoined this division in 2018-19 after first featuring in 1995-96 at the Dani Jarque. Gimnàstic, relegated 20th from Segunda División, began their current spell in 2019-20, having first played nationally in 1978-79 at Tarragona’s Nou Estadi. Hércules of Alicante, runners-up, have stayed since 2014-15 at the José Rico Pérez, having originally arrived in 1988-89. La Nucía, promoted as Tercera División group 6 champions, stepped up in 2019-20 to the Camilo Cano. Llagostera, topping Tercera group 5, returned in 2019-20 to the Municipal de Llagostera after first reaching this level in 2011-12. Lleida Esportiu, 6th, have been ever-present since 2011-12 at Camp d'Esports. Olot, 10th, came back in 2017-18 to the Municipal d’Olot, having debuted nationally in 2013-14. Orihuela, Tercera group 6 winners, re-entered in 2019-20 at Los Arcos after earlier stints since 2002-03. Prat, 4th in Tercera group 5, returned in 2019-20 to the Sagnier, having first played nationally in 2012-13. Sabadell, 12th, have remained since 2015-16 at the Nova Creu Alta, following campaigns since 1983-84. Valencia Mestalla, 13th, have stayed since 2011-12 at Antonio Puchades, having first featured in 1987-88. Villarreal B, 3rd, have been in place since 2012-13 at the Mini Estadi, after originally joining in 2007-08.
