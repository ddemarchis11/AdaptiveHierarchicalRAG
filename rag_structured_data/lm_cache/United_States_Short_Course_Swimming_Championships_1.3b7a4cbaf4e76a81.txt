Abbey Weitzeil swept the sprint freestyles for Canyon, clocking 21.12 in the 50-yard and 46.47 in the 100-yard on 5–6 March 2016 in Austin. Mallory Comerford of Louisville Cardinals took the 200-yard free in 1:41.17 on 1 December 2017 in Columbus. Katie Ledecky dominated distance, posting 4:29.54 for the 500-yard and 15:13.30 for the 1650-yard free on 4 and 6 December 2014 in Greensboro while representing Nation’s Capital. Natalie Coughlin opened the meet’s modern era with a 50.64 100-yard backstroke on 30 November 2007 in Atlanta for Cal Aquatics. Missy Franklin claimed the 200-yard back in 1:49.18 on 1 December 2012 in Austin for Colorado Stars. Alia Atkinson touched in 57.62 for the 100-yard breast on 6 December 2013 in Knoxville with South Florida, and Laura Sogar followed in the 200-yard breast at 2:05.04 on 1 December 2012 in Austin for the University of Texas. Kelsi Worrell flew to a 49.87 100-yard fly on 1 December 2017 in Columbus for Louisville Cardinals. Katinka Hosszú of USC Trojans doubled, winning the 200-yard fly in 1:51.02 on 4 December 2010 and the 400-yard IM in 4:00.03 on 3 December 2010, both in Columbus. Melanie Margalis captured the 200-yard IM in 1:52.63 on 30 November 2017 in Columbus for Florida Swimming. Relay highlights include SwimMAC Carolina’s 1:27.19 4×50-yard free on 5 December 2013 in Knoxville anchored by Arianna Vanderpool-Wallace, Louisville Cardinals’ 3:12.15 4×100-yard free on 2 December 2017 in Columbus led by Mallory Comerford’s 46.50 split, California Golden Bears’ 6:59.11 4×200-yard free on 6 December 2013 in Knoxville with Missy Franklin’s 1:42.93 lead-off, Arizona Wildcats’ 1:36.46 4×50-yard medley on 30 November 2012 in Austin capped by Margo Geer’s 21.43 anchor, and California Golden Bears’ 3:29.91 4×100-yard medley on 5 December 2013 in Knoxville featuring Elizabeth Pelton’s 51.27 backstroke lead and Missy Franklin’s 47.51 freestyle anchor.
