Fernando Alonso planted his Renault on pole with a 1:11.988, just 0.157 s clear of Juan Pablo Montoya’s McLaren-Mercedes. Giancarlo Fisichella made it two Renaults in the top three, 0.570 s adrift, while Jenson Button’s BAR-Honda and Kimi Räikkönen’s silver arrow filled the next spots at +0.708 s and +0.793 s. Christian Klien led the Red Bull charge in sixth, 0.901 s off the benchmark, followed by Michael Schumacher’s Ferrari, Jarno Trulli’s Toyota and the Sauber-Petronas pair of Felipe Massa and Rubens Barrichello, the latter conceding 1.195 s to Alonso. Ralf Schumacher’s Toyota, Jacques Villeneuve’s Sauber and Tiago Monteiro’s Jordan-Toyota slotted 11th to 13th, gaps creeping past 1.3 s. Mark Webber and Antônio Pizzonia shared row eight for Williams-BMW at +1.550 s and +1.593 s, while David Coulthard’s Red Bull trailed by 1.856 s. Narain Karthikeyan and Christijan Albers brought up the rear among those who set times, 2.532 s and 2.775 s behind. Takuma Sato and Robert Doornbos failed to log laps, the Minardi driver spinning.
