Emmanuel Mbata powered Kaya to a 7-1 demolition of Team Socceroo on 23 January 2014. Jeffrey Christiaens starred as Global crushed Philippine Army 9-1 on 1 February 2014, and Christian Pasilan did likewise for Green Archers United in another 7-1 rout of Team Socceroo three days later. Mark Hartmann twice tormented Team Socceroo, first in a 9-0 Global avalanche on 11 February and again in an identical 9-0 repeat on 20 March. OJ Porteria added to Philippine Army’s misery by headlineing Kaya’s 9-1 triumph on 13 February. Diego Barrera provided Team Socceroo’s lone bright spot, edging Stallion 4-3 on 13 March. Milad Behgandom orchestrated Global’s 7-0 blanking of Pasargad on 29 March, while Freddy Gonzalez drove Loyola past Philippine Army 4-1 on 5 April. Jeong Woo Lee sparked Team Socceroo’s 6-2 revenge over Pasargad on 11 May. Izzeldin El Habbib closed the campaign in style, propelling Global to a 7-0 humbling of Team Socceroo on 14 June and capping it with a 9-0 obliteration of Stallion on 26 June.
