The European Parliament convenes 785 MPs in Brussels and Strasbourg under Martin Schulz, while the European Council gathers the 28 Heads of State or Government plus the Commission President in Brussels, chaired by Donald Tusk. The Council of the European Union, also in Brussels, comprises 28 ministers led by a biannual rotating chair. Jean-Claude Juncker presides over the 28-member European Commission in Brussels. Luxembourg hosts the European Court of Justice with 27 judges and 8 Advocate-Generals under Koen Lenaerts, and the Court of Auditors with 24 members and a president, Vítor Manuel da Silva Caldeira, split between Brussels and Luxembourg. Frankfurt am Main is the seat of the European Central Bank, chaired by Mario Draghi. The European Investment Bank, owned by the EU member states and chaired by Werner Hoyer, is based in Luxembourg. Brussels also houses the 222-member Economic and Social Committee chaired by Georges Dassis and the 344-member European Committee of the Regions led by Markku Markkula. Emily O’Reilly serves as the European Ombudsman in Strasbourg alongside a Secretary.
