Ludovic Roudy and Bruno Sautter’s party game Just One earned the 2019 Spiel des Jahres for Repos Production, while Michael Kiesling’s Azul took the 2018 prize under Next Move/Plan B Games. Bruno Cathala’s Kingdomino won for Pegasus Spiele in 2017, the same publisher that released Donald X. Vaccarino’s Kingdom Builder, the 2012 laureate. Vlaada Chvátil’s word-association hit Codenames brought 2016 glory to Czech Games Edition, preceded by Christophe Raimbault’s train-robbing Colt Express for Ludonaute in 2015 and Steffen Bogen’s racing camels in Camel Up via Eggertspiele the year before. Antoine Bauza’s cooperative fireworks puzzle Hanabi won for Abacusspiele in 2013, and Susan McKinley Ross’s tile-laying Qwirkle did so for Mindware Spiele in 2011, one year after Jean-Louis Roubira’s dreamy storytelling game Dixit claimed the title for Libellud. Donald X. Vaccarino also designed 2009 winner Dominion, published by Rio Grande Games, while Reiner Knizia’s Keltis earned Kosmos the 2008 award. Michael Schacht’s zoo-building Zooloretto won for Abacus Spiele in 2007, Andreas and Karen Seyfarth’s postal-themed Thurn and Taxis did so for Hans im Glück in 2006, and Thomas Liesching’s rapid Niagara earned Zoch Verlag the 2005 prize. Alan R. Moon’s cross-country Ticket to Ride won for Days of Wonder in 2004, Dirk Henn’s palace-building Alhambra gave Queen Games the 2003 honor, Bill Payne’s balancing Villa Paletti earned Zoch Verlag the 2002 nod, Klaus-Jürgen Wrede’s medieval Carcassonne won for Hans im Glück in 2001, and the architectural strategy game Torres by Wolfgang Kramer and Michael Kiesling closed the millennium with the 2000 award for Ravensburger.
