Ichikawa Jūkai III, born in 1886, became a Living National Treasure in 1960 for his tachiyaku roles in Kabuki, and Ichikawa Dannosuke VI, born 1876, received the same honor that year as a wakiyaku performer. Ichikawa Sadanji III, born 1898, was designated in 1964, followed by Nakamura Ganjirō II, born 1902, in 1967. Onoe Baikō VII and Nakamura Utaemon VI, both onnagata specialists born in 1915 and 1917 respectively, were jointly honored in 1968, the same year Onoe Taganojō III, born 1887, received recognition for his fukeonnayaku performances. Onoe Shōroku II, born 1913, and Kataoka Nizaemon XIII, born 1903, both became Living National Treasures in 1972. Bandō Mitsugorō VIII, born 1906, and Matsumoto Hakuō, born 1910, were designated in 1973 and 1975 respectively, alongside Nakamura Kanzaburō XVII, born 1909. Takemoto Hinatayū V, born 1898, and Kineya Eizaemon, born 1894, received their honors in 1978 for takemoto and nagauta contributions. Yoshimura Gorōji II, born 1901, followed in 1981. Ichimura Uzaemon XVII, born 1916, was designated in 1990, Nakamura Jakuemon IV, born 1920, in 1991, and Nakamura Tomijūrō V, born 1929, in 1994. Nakamura Matagorō II, born 1914, received recognition in 1997, and Matsushima Jusaburō V, born 1920, in 1998.
