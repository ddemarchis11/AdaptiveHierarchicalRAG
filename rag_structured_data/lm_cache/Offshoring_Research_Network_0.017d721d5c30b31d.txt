Duke University’s Fuqua School of Business launched the Offshoring Research Network in 2004, with Prof. Arie Y. Lewin leading a sequence of colleagues: Dr. Carine Peeters through 2006, Dr. Stephan Manning until 2009, and Dr. Nidthida Perm-Ajchariyawong from 2008 onward. The United Kingdom joined in 2005 when Prof. Silvia Massini brought the University of Manchester’s Manchester Business School into the consortium. A four-school European wave followed in 2006: Copenhagen Business School’s Center for Strategic Management and Globalization added Profs. Torben Pedersen and Bent Petersen; Rotterdam School of Management at Erasmus University enlisted Prof. Henk Volberda; Solvay Brussels School (ULB) welcomed back Prof. Carine Peeters; IESE Business School at the University of Navarra placed Prof. Joan E. Ricart on the team; and Germany’s WHU – Otto Beisheim School of Management installed Prof. Thomas Hutzschenreuter. Australia entered in 2008 through a joint venture between the University of Newcastle’s Business School and the University of Western Sydney’s Centre for Industry and Innovation Studies, represented by Profs. Stephen Chen and Oscar Hauptman. France’s EMLYON Business School, South Korea’s Kyung Hee University, and the University of Tokyo all completed the roster in 2010, respectively under Profs. Christiane Prange, Geon-Cheol Shin, and the duo of Takahiro Fujimoto and Youngwon Park.
