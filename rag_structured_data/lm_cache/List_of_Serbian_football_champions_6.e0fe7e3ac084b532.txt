In the inaugural 1945 season SR Serbia took the title ahead of JNA and SR Croatia, with JNA’s Stjepan Bobek scoring 8 goals. Partizan claimed the 1946-47 championship, edging Dinamo Zagreb and Crvena Zvezda while Dinamo’s Franjo Wölfl hit 28 goals. Wölfl repeated as top scorer with 22 goals the next year as Dinamo Zagreb won, followed by Hajduk Split and Partizan. Partizan regained the crown in 1948-49, with Hajduk’s Frane Matošić topping the charts on 17 goals. Hajduk Split won 1950, pushing Crvena Zvezda and Partizan down to second and third, while Partizan’s Marko Valok led scorers with 17. Crvena Zvezda took 1951 ahead of Dinamo Zagreb and Hajduk, their own Kosta Tomašević scoring 16. Hajduk won again in 1952, BSK Belgrade’s Stanoje Jocić leading scorers with 13. Crvena Zvezda returned to the summit in 1952-53, Todor Živanović hitting 17. Dinamo Zagreb won 1953-54, Partizan’s Stjepan Bobek scoring 21. Hajduk Split secured 1954-55, with Predrag Marković, Kosta Tomašević and Bernard Vukas sharing the 20-goal top-scorer honour. Crvena Zvezda won 1955-56, 1956-57, 1958-59 and 1959-60, Todor Veselinović claiming the scoring title in 1956-57 with 28 and in 1957-58 with 19, while Bora Kostić struck 25 and 19 in the latter two seasons. Dinamo Zagreb interrupted in 1957-58. Partizan then won 1960-61, 1961-62, 1962-63 and 1964-65, Crvena Zvezda taking 1963-64 in between, with Zlatko Dračić topping scorers in the last of those seasons with 23 goals.
