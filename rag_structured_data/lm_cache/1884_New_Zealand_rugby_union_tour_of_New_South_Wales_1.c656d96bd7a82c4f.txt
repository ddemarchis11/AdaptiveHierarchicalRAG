On 28 May 1884 the New Zealand tourists opened their New South Wales campaign by overwhelming Cumberland County 33-0 at Parramatta Ground, Sydney. Three days later they defeated the NSW Waratahs 11-0 on the Agricultural Society Ground, and on 3 June they beat Combined Suburbs 23-5 at the Cricket Ground. After travelling to Newcastle on 5 June they routed Northern Districts 29-0 at the Sports Ground, then returned to Sydney to overcome the Waratahs again, this time 21-2 on 7 June at the Agricultural Society Ground. A country fixture followed on 11 June in Bathurst where Western Districts were beaten 11-0 at the Bathurst Ground, and the next day back in Sydney the combined Wallaroo & University NSW side were defeated 23-10 at the Cricket Ground. The tour concluded on 14 June with a third victory over the NSW Waratahs, 16-0, again on the Agricultural Society Ground, sealing eight wins from eight matches played.
