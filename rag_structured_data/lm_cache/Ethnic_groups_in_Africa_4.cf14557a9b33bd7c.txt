The Aka and Efé, both Nilo-Saharan Pygmy peoples, speak their eponymous languages in the Central/Northern region, the Aka inhabiting the western Central African Republic and northwest Congo while the Efé live in the Ituri Rainforest of Congo, and are joined there by the Sua who also speak a Nilo-Saharan Pygmy language. The Baka Pygmy, speaking Baka, number 0.25 million in Western Equatoria of South Sudan. Banda and Gbaya, each 1.5 million strong and Niger-Congo Ubangian speakers, occupy the Chad Basin within the Central African Republic. The 9 million Chewa, Chichewa-speaking Niger-Congo Bantu, span Botswana, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, Tanzania, Zambia and Zimbabwe, while the 1.1 million Chokwe, speaking Chokwe, live in Angola, Congo (Kinshasa) and Zambia. The 10 million Kongo, Kongo-speaking Niger-Congo Bantu, reside in Democratic Republic of the Congo, Angola and Republic of the Congo, matched in number by the Kanuri, whose Western Saharan Nilo-Saharan language is heard in Nigeria, Niger, Cameroon and Chad. Lingala, a Niger-Congo Bantu tongue, is used in Democratic Republic of the Congo, Republic of the Congo, Angola and Central African Republic. Luba, numbering 13 million, speak Luba-Kasai and Luba-Katanga in Democratic Republic of the Congo. The Afro-Asiatic Chadic Kotoko, fewer than 1 million, speak Lagwan in Cameroon and Chad. Mbunda, 0.5 million, speak Mbúùnda, Chimbúùnda and Mpuono in Angola and Zambia, while the 2.4 million Mbundu speak Kimbundu in Angola and the 4.6 million Ovimbundu speak Umbundu there. Moghamo and Ngamambo, Southern Bantoid Niger-Congo peoples of Cameroon, are 18,000 and 11,000 respectively. The 12 million Mongo, Mongo-speaking Niger-Congo Bantu, live in Democratic Republic of the Congo. The 3.5 million Sara, Central Sudanic Nilo-Saharan speakers, inhabit Chad, Cameroon and Central African Republic.
