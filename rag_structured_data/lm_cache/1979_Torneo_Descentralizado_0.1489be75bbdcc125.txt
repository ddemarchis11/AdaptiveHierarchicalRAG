Alfonso Ugarte of Puno plays at the 20,000-seat Enrique Torres Belón on grass, while Alianza Lima uses Lima’s 35,000-capacity Alejandro Villanueva, also on grass. Atlético Chalaco and Sport Boys share Callao’s 15,000-seat Miguel Grau grass pitch, and Atlético Torino’s home is Talara’s 8,000-capacity Campeonísimo grass ground. CNI draws 24,000 fans to Iquitos’ Max Augustín on grass, Coronel Bolognesi fills Tacna’s 19,850-seat Jorge Basadre, and Deportivo Junín hosts matches in Huancayo’s 20,000-capacity Huancayo stadium, all on grass. Deportivo Municipal, Sporting Cristal and Universitario all play on Lima’s Nacional, a 45,750-seat grass arena. Juan Aurich entertains 24,500 at Chiclayo’s Elías Aguirre, Juventud La Palma 12,000 at Huacho’s Segundo Aranda Torres, and León de Huánuco 15,000 at Huánuco’s Heraclio Tapia, each on grass. Melgar’s 20,000-seat Mariano Melgar in Arequipa and Unión Huaral’s 10,000-seat Julio Lores Colan in Huaral complete the sixteen-team slate, every surface grass.
