Allyson Felix of North Hills, California, claimed the 2003 honor for track & field before heading to USC, while Candace Parker took the 2004 basketball award from Naperville, Illinois, and enrolled at Tennessee. Stanford-bound Cynthia Barboza earned the 2005 volleyball prize from Long Beach, California, and Connecticut-bound post players dominated the next three seasons: Tina Charles from Middle Village, New York, in 2006, Maya Moore from Lawrenceville, Georgia, in 2007, and Notre Dame-bound Skylar Diggins from South Bend, Indiana, in 2009, with Stanford’s Chiney Ogwumike of Cypress, Texas, winning in 2010. Breanna Stewart continued the UConn lineage in 2012 out of North Syracuse, New York. Soccer’s 2011 winner, Morgan Brian, came from St. Simons Island, Georgia, and chose Virginia, while 2013 recipient Morgan Andrews from Milford, New Hampshire, picked Notre Dame. Brianna Turner kept Notre Dame on the women’s basketball map in 2014, arriving from Manvel, Texas. Track standouts included Tennessee-bound Chanelle Price from Easton, Pennsylvania, in 2008, Candace Hill of Conyers, Georgia, in 2015 forgoing college, and Kentucky-bound Sydney McLaughlin repeating from Scotch Plains, New Jersey, in 2016 and 2017. Katelyn Tuohy of Thiels, New York, swept cross country and track & field in 2018, and Kelley Lynch closed the span as 2019 softball winner from Sharpsburg, Georgia, bound for Washington.
