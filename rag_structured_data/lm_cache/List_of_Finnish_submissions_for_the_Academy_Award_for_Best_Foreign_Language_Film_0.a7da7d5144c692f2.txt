Rauni Mollberg’s Maa on syntinen laulu represented Finland at the 46th Academy Awards in 1973 but was not nominated, and he returned thirteen years later with Tuntematon sotilas for the 59th ceremony with the same result. Pirjo Honkasalo and Pekka Lehto’s Tulipää went forward in 1980, followed by Jaakko Pakkasvirta’s Pedon merkki in 1981, Heikki Partanen’s Pessi ja Illusia in 1984, Päivi Hartzell’s Lumikuningatar in 1987, Pekka Parikka’s Talvisota in 1990, Christian Lindblad’s Ripa ruostuu in 1994 and Markku Pölönen’s Kivenpyörittäjän kylä in 1995, all unsuccessfully. Aki Kaurismäki’s Kauas pilvet karkaavat was disqualified in 1996 after the director himself withdrew it. Auli Mantila’s Neitoperho took the slot in 1997, Markku Pölönen returned with Kuningasjätkä in 1998, Aleksi Mäkelä entered Häjyt in 1999, Anastasia Lapsui and Markku Lehmuskallio submitted Seitsemän laulua tundralta in 2000, and Jarmo Lampela offered Joki in 2001; none advanced. Kaurismäki finally secured Finland’s only nomination with Mies vailla menneisyyttä at the 75th Oscars. Klaus Härö then shouldered the country’s hopes three times in four years: Neitoperho in 2003, Äideistä parhain in 2005, and in 2006 Kaurismäki again withdrew his own Laitakaupungin valot.
