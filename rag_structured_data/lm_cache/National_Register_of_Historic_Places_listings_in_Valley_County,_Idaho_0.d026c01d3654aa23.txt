Big Creek Commissary, listed on April 21, 2000, stands in Yellow Pine within Payette National Forest. Off a pack trail near Suicide Rock, the Braddock Gold Mining and Milling Company Log Building and Forge Ruins were entered on September 12, 1985, in Thunder City. Cabin Creek Ranch, added June 27, 1990, sits where Cabin Creek meets Big Creek in Payette National Forest outside Black Butte. Elo School, recorded July 26, 1982, lies southeast of State Highway 55 on Farm-to-Market Road in McCall. Also in McCall, the Matt N. Hill Homestead Barn and the Charles Koski Homestead were listed November 17, 1982, while the Johnson Flying Service Hangar at 103 S. 3rd St. joined the Register on November 20, 2019, and the McCall District Administrative Site at the junction of West Lake and Mission streets was added December 30, 1991. Near Lake Fork, the Thomas Jarvi, John G. Johnson (Rintakangas), John S. Johnson (Sampila), Gust Laituri, and Herman Ojala homesteads—all along Pearson Road—were listed November 17, 1982, as was the Long Valley Finnish Church southeast of town. Donnelly claims the John Korvola, Jacob and Herman Mahala, and Jacob Maki homesteads, all listed November 17, 1982. The Krassel Ranger Station, along the South Fork Salmon River west of Yellow Pine, was entered November 19, 1992. The North Fork Payette River Bridge, roughly 2.5 miles north of Smiths Ferry on State Highway 55, was listed April 2, 1999, and the Payette Lakes Club at 1858 Warren Wagon Road in McCall was added April 24, 2017.
