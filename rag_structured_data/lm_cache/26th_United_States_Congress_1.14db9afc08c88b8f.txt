Tilghman Howard, a Democrat, took the vacant Indiana 7th seat on August 5, 1839, the same day credentials were presented for the originally elected Howard. Albert G. Harrison’s death on September 7, 1839, opened Missouri’s at-large seat for fellow Democrat John Jameson, installed December 12. Massachusetts 6th Whig James C. Alvord died September 27, 1839, and Osmyn Baker, also a Whig, succeeded him on January 14, 1840. William W. Potter’s October 28, 1839, death in Pennsylvania’s 14th led to Democrat George McCulloch being seated November 20. Charles F. Mercer resigned Virginia’s 14th on December 26, 1839, and Whig William M. McCarty replaced him January 25, 1840. Thomas Corwin quit Ohio’s 4th on May 30, 1840, to run for governor, and Whig Jeremiah Morrow took over October 13. William L. Storrs left Connecticut’s 2nd in June 1840, with Whig William W. Boardman filling the seat December 7. Anson Brown’s June 14, 1840, death in New York’s 11th brought Whig Nicholas B. Doe in on December 7. Tilghman Howard resigned Indiana’s 7th on July 1, 1840, flipping the seat to Whig Henry S. Lane, seated August 3. Walter T. Colquitt left Georgia’s at-large seat July 21, 1840, and Whig Hines Holt succeeded him February 1, 1841. Rice Garland resigned Louisiana’s 3rd on July 21, 1840, for a judgeship, with Whig John Moore installed December 17. Richard Biddle quit Pennsylvania’s 22nd on July 21, 1840, and Whig Henry M. Brackenridge took over October 13. Simeon H. Anderson’s August 11, 1840, death in Kentucky’s 5th led to Whig John B. Thompson’s December 7 seating. Abbott Lawrence resigned Massachusetts 1st on September 18, 1840, and Whig Robert C. Winthrop replaced him November 9. William S. Ramsey died October 17, 1840, in Pennsylvania’s 13th, and Democrat Charles McClure succeeded him December 7. William W. Chapman’s term as Iowa Territory’s delegate expired by law October 27, 1840, and Democrat Augustus C. Dodge began his term the next day. Joel Holleman resigned Virginia’s 1st in December 1840, and Whig Francis Mallory was seated December 28. George Evans vacated Maine’s 4th on March 3, 1841, upon election to the Senate, leaving the seat unfilled for the remainder of the term.
