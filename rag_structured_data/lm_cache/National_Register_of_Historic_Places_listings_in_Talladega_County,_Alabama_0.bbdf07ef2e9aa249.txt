The Benjamin H. Averiett House and the nearby William Averiett House, both off State Route 8 in Sylacauga, entered the National Register on August 28, 1986. Boxwood at 406 E. North St. in Talladega was listed June 9, 1983, while the Charles Butler House stands at the junction of 1st St. and 10th Ave. in Childersburg, added February 26, 1996. Sylacauga’s B.B. Comer Memorial Library, 711 N. Broadway Ave., joined the register September 6, 2005, the same town where the Goodwin-Hamilton House on Marble Valley Rd. and the Hightower Brothers Livery Stable at 413 Norton Ave. were listed August 28, 1986, and July 3, 1997, respectively. The J.L.M. Curry House, 3 miles northeast of Talladega on State Route 21, dates to October 15, 1966, and the Elston House, 10 miles north on Turner’s Mill Rd., to October 8, 1976. Talladega’s First Presbyterian Church at 130 North St. was listed November 17, 1983, Idlewild on State Route 5 north of Route 21 on October 15, 1993, and the Silk Stocking District roughly bounded by Coffee, 2nd, McMillan, and Court Sts. on December 13, 1979. The Jemison House Complex south of the Chocolocco and Cheaha Creeks confluence in Eastaboga was added October 1, 1990. Kymulga Mill and Covered Bridge, 4.5 miles northeast of Childersburg on State Route 46, entered October 29, 1976, and the Lawler-Whiting House along State Route 21 south of Talladega on May 22, 1986. Oxford’s Dudley Snow House on Peek Dr. was listed February 4, 1982. Swayne Hall on the Talladega College campus and the adjacent Talladega College Historic District at Battle St. and Martin Luther King Dr. were registered December 2, 1974, and August 23, 1990. The Sylacauga Historic Commercial District, roughly bounded by Broadway Ave., W. 1st, Anniston Ave., and W. 4th St., joined June 2, 2004, and the Talladega Courthouse Square Historic District, centered on Courthouse Sq., was entered October 18, 1972.
