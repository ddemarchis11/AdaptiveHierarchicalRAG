The AME School operated first in Campbell from 1972 to 1976, then reopened in Weston Creek’s Weston suburb, continuing as an independent institution until 1996. In North Canberra, Our Lady of Mercy Primary School served Catholic families in Braddon between 1935 and 1973, while St Brigid’s Primary School in Dickson educated students from 1960 to 1986, and St Michael’s Primary School in Lyneham functioned from 1961 to 1973. South Canberra’s Forrest hosted St Christopher’s School from 1928 to 1971, and Yarralumla’s St Peter Chanel’s Primary School operated from 1956 to 1989. In Tuggeranong, Padua Catholic High School in Wanniassa opened in 1978 and closed in 1997, the same year St Peter’s Catholic College in Isabella Plains ceased instruction after only eight years, having opened in 1989.
