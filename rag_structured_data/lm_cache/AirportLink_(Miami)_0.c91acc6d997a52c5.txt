Palmetto, opened 30 May 2003, lies 31 minutes from downtown and links only to Metrobus 87, drawing 1,500 weekday riders. Okeechobee, dating to 19 May 1985, trims the ride to 26 minutes via buses 73 and 267 and counts 1,156 passengers. Hialeah, also from 1985, reaches downtown in 23 minutes on buses 29, 37, 54, 112, 135 and serves 1,408 riders. Tri-Rail station, launched 5 June 1989, offers 21-minute access plus Tri-Rail and buses 42, 112 for 1,284 riders. Northside, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Plaza, and Brownsville, all opened 18–19 May 1985, sit 19, 16, and 14 minutes out, respectively, and carry 1,496, 1,216, and 899 passengers with their varied bus connections. Miami International Airport station, newest at 28 July 2012, is 16 minutes from downtown, unites Tri-Rail, MIA Mover, and nine bus routes, and leads the northern tier with 1,776 riders. Earlington Heights and Allapattah, December 1984 veterans, are 11 and 9 minutes out, handling 1,686 and 1,930 riders. Santa Clara, Civic Center, Culmer, and Historic Overtown/Lyric Theatre, all opened December 1984 or May 1984, range 2–7 minutes from downtown; Civic Center peaks at 5,679 riders. Government Center, the system hub since 20 May 1984, records 10,818 riders through Metromover, Metrobus, Broward 95X, and Virgin Trains. Brickell, also 2 minutes out, counts 6,319 riders via Brickell Loop Metromover and multiple buses. Vizcaya, Coconut Grove, Douglas Road, and University, 1984 stations 5–12 minutes south, draw 1,265, 1,748, 3,623, and 2,041 riders, with Douglas Road adding Coral Gables Trolley.
