Dorsum Buckland stretches 380 km across the Moon, memorializing the English geologist William Buckland (1784-1856), while the 268 km Dorsum Oppel honors German paleontologist Albert Oppel (1831-1865). Dorsum Von Cotta, 199 km long, bears the name of Carl Bernard von Cotta (1808-1879). Dorsum Heim, 148 km across, commemorates Swiss geologist Albert Heim (1849-1937), and the 121 km Dorsum Grabau remembers American geologist Amadeus William Grabau (1870-1946). Dorsum Scilla, 108 km long, is named for Italian scientist Agostino Scilla (1639-1700), while Dorsum Arduino, 107 km, honors Italian geologist Giovanni Arduino (1714-1795). Dorsum Bucher and Dorsum Termier, each 90 km, honor Walter Hermann Bucher (1889-1965) and Pierre-Marie Termier (1859-1930) respectively. Dorsum Cayeux, 84 km, remembers French geologist Lucien Cayeux (1864-1944), while Dorsum Cushman, 80 km, is named for American geologist Joseph Augustine Cushman (1881-1949). Dorsum Gast, 60 km, commemorates Paul Werner Gast (1930-1973), as does Dorsum Higazy for Egyptian geologist Riad Higazy (1919-1967). Dorsum Nicol and Dorsum Niggli, each 50 km, honor William Nicol (1768-1851) and Paul Niggli (1888-1953). Dorsum Guettard, 40 km, remembers Jean-Etienne Guettard (1715-1786), while Dorsum Owen, 50 km, is named for George Owen (1552-1613). Dorsum Thera, only 7 km, uniquely takes its name from a Greek feminine name.
