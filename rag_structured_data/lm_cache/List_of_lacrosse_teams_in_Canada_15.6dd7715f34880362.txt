Akwesasne’s Jr. Indians, founded in 2007, sat out 2012 and have yet to claim any league or Minto Cup crowns. Brampton’s 1883-born Excelsiors—once the ABC’s and Bramalea Excelsiors—have pocketed three league titles since 1961 and one Minto Cup. Barrie’s 2000-born Tornado, scoreless in both championship columns, moved to OLA Jr A in 2009 and captured the Founders Cup in 2003. Burlington Chiefs, tracing back through the Hamilton and Bay Area Bengals since 1976, remain without titles. Kitchener-Waterloo Braves, established 1976 and formerly the Kitchener Braves, earned one Junior B league banner and two Founders Cups before joining the top tier. Mississauga Tomahawks, cycling through Athletics, Sullivan Homes, Merchants and Arrowheads since 1973, collected two Junior B league titles and one Founders Cup. Orangeville Northmen, born 1975 as the Stingers, own four league titles and three Minto Cups, adding one Junior C, two Junior B league banners and two Founders Cups along the way. Orillia Rama Kings—once Lions, 501 Kings and Rogers Kings—since 1973 have three Junior B league titles and two Founders Cups. Ottawa Titans, launched 2005, are still awaiting their first trophy. Peterborough Lakers, whose lineage winds through Legionnaires, Petes, Tee-Pees, Gray-Munros, Century 21, James Gang, Maulers, Traders, Javelins and P.C.O.s, lead with 13 league titles and 12 Minto Cups. St. Catharines Athletics, formerly Supertests, Lakesides and Legionaires, count five league and three Minto Cup victories. Six Nations Arrows, begun 1974 as the Braves, hold six league titles and two Minto Cups after climbing through Junior C and B. Toronto Beaches, founded 1976, emerged from Junior B without silverware. Whitby Warriors, once Consolidated Builders, have seven league titles and five Minto Cups.
