Chivas USA opened the 2004 MLS Expansion Draft by selecting midfielder Arturo Torres from the Los Angeles Galaxy, and Real Salt Lake answered by claiming Chicago Fire midfielder Andy Williams. The Fire were hit again when Chivas USA took defender Orlando Perez third overall, and Chicago lost a third player when Real Salt Lake chose goalkeeper D.J. Countess at pick four. Chivas USA continued with defender Ezra Hendrickson from D.C. United, while Real Salt Lake selected MetroStars midfielder Pablo Brenes. Francisco Gomez moved from Kansas City Wizards to Chivas USA, Brian Kamler left New England Revolution for Real Salt Lake, Antonio de la Torre departed Colorado Rapids for Chivas USA, and Nelson Akwari switched from Columbus Crew to Real Salt Lake to close the first ten selections. In the second half of the draft, Chivas USA added forward Matt Taylor from Kansas City Wizards, Real Salt Lake claimed midfielder Chris Brown from San Jose Earthquakes, and Chivas USA selected defender Craig Ziadie from MetroStars. Real Salt Lake then picked defender Matt Behncke from F.C. Dallas, Chivas USA took midfielder Jamil Walker from San Jose Earthquakes, and Real Salt Lake chose defender Rusty Pierce from New England Revolution. The final three selections saw Thiago Martins move from D.C. United to Chivas USA, Kevin Ara depart D.C. United for Real Salt Lake, Jeff Stewart leave Colorado Rapids for Chivas USA, and Erick Scott transfer from Columbus Crew to Real Salt Lake to complete the twenty-player draft.
