In 1975 Armando Crispino’s Autopsy terrified Italian screens with Barry Primus, Mimsy Farmer and Ray Lovelock, while Hong Kong offered Lo Wei’s The Bedevilled starring O Chun Hung, Reiko Ike and James Tien, and Ho Meng Hua’s Black Magic headlined by Ti Lung and Lo Lieh. Mexico shivered to Carlos Enrique Taboada’s Blacker Than the Night with Claudia Islas, Helena Rojo, Susana Dosamantes and Lucía Méndez. The United States saw Nick Millard’s Criminally Insane fronted by Priscilla Alden, Curtis Harrington’s The Dead Don’t Die featuring George Hamilton, Linda Cristal and Joan Blondell, Peter Wolf’s Deafula with Wolf and Lee Darel, Robert Fuest’s occult The Devil’s Rain boasting Ernest Borgnine, Eddie Albert and Ida Lupino, Harry Kerwin’s God’s Bloody Acre led by Wayne Crawford, and Chris Munger’s Kiss of the Tarantula pairing Eric Mason, Suzanna Ling and Beverly Eddins. Britain contributed Freddie Francis’s The Ghoul with Peter Cushing, John Hurt and Alexandra Bastedo, Pete Walker’s House of Mortal Sin starring Anthony Sharp, Peter Sasdy’s I Don’t Want to Be Born with Joan Collins, Ralph Bates and Eileen Atkins, plus Francis again directing Peter Cushing in Legend of the Werewolf. Australia’s Terry Bourke filmed Inn of the Damned with Judith Anderson, Alex Cord and Michael Craig; Japan’s Yoichi Takabayashi offered Death at an Old Mansion with Akira Nakao and Takahiro Tamura; Italy added Massimo Dallamano’s The Cursed Medallion starring Richard Johnson, Joanna Cassidy and Ida Galli, Luigi Cozzi’s The Killer Must Kill Again with Cristina Galbó, George Hilton and Femi Benussi, and Aldo Lado’s Last Stop on the Night Train featuring Marina Berti, Franco Fabrizi and Irene Miracle. Steven Spielberg’s Jaws, starring Roy Scheider and Richard Dreyfuss, also premiered that year.
