William Clarke, MLC, of Melbourne served as the first president from 1877 to 1881, followed by Cr James Garton, who held office from 1882 to 1886. Frank Grey Smith, also from Melbourne, then led from 1887 to 1896. Theodore Fink, MLA, of Richmond presided from 1897 to 1900, succeeded by James Hall of Williamstown for 1901–1902. Cr John George Aikman of Essendon (A.) held the longest tenure, 1903–1928, after which Cr John James Liston of Williamstown served from 1929 to 1944. Henry Zwar, MLA, of Preston led 1944–1946, followed by Squire Reid, MLA, of Oakleigh, 1947–1949, and Dr Frank Hartnett of Camberwell, 1949–1950. Lewis Page of Brighton presided 1951–1953, then Cr Alex Gillon of Brunswick from 1954 to 1980. Alan Wickes of Frankston served 1981–1984, Brook Andersen of Brunswick 1985–1989, John Grieve of Williamstown 1989–1992, and Tony Hannebery of Williamstown closed the sequence from 1993 to 1994.
