Brazil entered Glauber Rocha’s Portuguese-language epic *The Black God and the White Devil* under its original title *Deus e o Diabo na Terra do Sol*, but it was not nominated. Czechoslovakia, competing for the first time, sent Oldřich Lipský’s Czech western satire *Lemonade Joe* (*Limonádový Joe*) without success, while Denmark offered Annelise Hovmand’s Danish chamber piece *Sextet* (*Sekstet*) and Egypt submitted Atef Salem’s Arabic domestic comedy *Mother of the Bride* (*أم العروسة*), both likewise passed over. France secured a nomination with Jacques Demy’s sung French romance *The Umbrellas of Cherbourg* (*Les parapluies de Cherbourg*), and Israel, also a first-time participant, saw Ephraim Kishon’s Hebrew social satire *Sallah Shabati* advance to the final five. Italy triumphed with Vittorio De Sica’s Italian triptych *Yesterday, Today and Tomorrow* (*Ieri, oggi, domani*), which went on to win the Oscar, while Japan’s entry, Hiroshi Teshigahara’s Japanese existential drama *Woman in the Dunes* (*砂の女*), and Sweden’s contender, Bo Widerberg’s Swedish coming-of-age tale *Raven’s End* (*Kvarteret Korpen*), completed the roster of nominees. Among the remaining hopefuls, South Korea entered Shin Sang-ok’s Korean tragedy *The Deaf Samyong* (*벙어리 삼용*), the Netherlands offered Bert Haanstra’s Dutch documentary *The Human Dutch* (*Alleman*), Poland submitted Andrzej Munk and Witold Lesiewicz’s Polish Holocaust drama *Passenger* (*Pasażerka*), Spain sent Manuel Summers’s Spanish adolescent comedy *The Girl in Mourning* (*La Niña de luto*), Taiwan presented Lei Pan’s Mandarin romance *Lovers’ Rock* (*情人石*), Turkey, another newcomer, entered Metin Erksan and David E. Durston’s Turkish rural drama *Dry Summer* (*Susuz Yaz*), and Yugoslavia closed the list with Veljko Bulajić’s Macedonian documentary *Skoplje ’63* (*Скопје 63*), none receiving nominations.
