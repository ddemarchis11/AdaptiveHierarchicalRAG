In the first round the Vikings doubled up, nabbing North Carolina State cornerback Dewayne Washington 18th overall and California offensive tackle Todd Steussie one slot later at 19. They returned in the second round for two more impact players, choosing Alabama wide receiver David Palmer 40th overall and Jackson State defensive end Fernando Smith at 55. After a quiet third round, Iowa defensive tackle Mike Wells came off the board to Minnesota at 125, followed by Penn State defensive back Shelly Hammonds 134th overall in the fifth. Western Carolina tight end Andrew Jordan was the 179th selection in the sixth round, and the Vikings closed the draft by bringing hometown linebacker Pete Bercich from Notre Dame aboard at 211.
