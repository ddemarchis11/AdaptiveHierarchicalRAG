The Armourers and Brasiers’ Company Prize, created in 1984, is awarded every two years for excellence in materials science and technology, carries a £2,000 gift, and was most recently given to Steven Armes in 2018. The GlaxoSmithKline Prize and Lecture, established in 1976, is a biennial £2,500 award for original contributions to medical and veterinary sciences published within the previous decade; originally sponsored by the Wellcome Trust, it was renamed in 2002 and last went to Andrew Hattersley in 2016. Since 1986 the annual Michael Faraday Prize has honoured excellence in communicating science to UK audiences, providing £2,500 and a lecture, with Martyn Poliakoff receiving it in 2019. The Milner Award, launched in 2012 and supported by Microsoft Research, is an annual honour for outstanding European achievement in computer science named after Robin Milner; Cordelia Schmid won in 2020. The Mullard Award, dating from 1967, targets early-career UK scientists or engineers whose work promises national prosperity, adds £2,000, and was presented to Hagan Bayley in 2019. Created in 2016, the Royal Society Africa Prize gives an £11,000 research grant, a bronze medal and £1,000 to an Africa-based biologist advancing capacity building; Henry Mwandumba took the 2019 award. Also founded in 2016, the biennial Royal Society Athena Prize rewards UK individuals or teams advancing STEM diversity with a medal and £5,000, most recently to Emma Chapman in 2018. The Royal Society Insight Investment Science Book Prize, begun in 1988, annually crowns the year’s best general-science book for lay readers with £25,000; Andrea Wulf won in 2016. The Rosalind Franklin Award, instituted in 2003, annually grants £30,000 for outstanding STEM contributions by a woman, and Nguyễn Thị Kim Thanh claimed it in 2019.
