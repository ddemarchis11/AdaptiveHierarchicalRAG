New York City, top-ranked, has no active major tracks but once hosted Meadowlands, Bridgehampton and Trenton. Los Angeles keeps two major tracks including Fontana while Ontario and Riverside are gone. Chicago’s lone current oval is Chicagoland after Cicero closed. Baltimore/Washington, now trackless, lost Baltimore, Marlboro and RFK Stadium. San Jose/San Francisco still runs two majors and once had San Jose and Altamont. Boston’s New Hampshire supplies its single active venue. Philadelphia relies on Pocono after Langhorne, Flemington and Nazareth shut. Dallas/Fort Worth races at Texas with Dallas defunct. Miami uses Homestead, having shed two Miami venues plus West Palm Beach. Houston has none left after Houston and Texas closed. Atlanta and Detroit each count two majors, Atlanta and Michigan respectively. Seattle retains one, Phoenix keeps its oval while an earlier Phoenix faded, Minneapolis/St. Paul, Cleveland and Denver lost their namesakes, San Diego’s Del Mar is gone, Portland never had one, and Orlando’s Daytona endures after Walt Disney World disappeared.
