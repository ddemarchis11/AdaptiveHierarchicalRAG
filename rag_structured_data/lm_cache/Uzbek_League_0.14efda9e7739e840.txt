Mirjalol Qosimov’s AGMK of Olmaliq plays at the 12,000-seat AGMK Stadium wearing Joma kits, while Ildar Sakaev’s Andijan side fills Soglom Avlod’s 18,360 places in Adidas. Vadim Abramov’s Bunyodkor, the capital’s Nike-clad giant, hosts 34,000 at Bunyodkor Stadium, dwarfing Andrey Fyodorov’s Lokomotiv who occupy only 8,000 at Lokomotiv Stadium across town. Bakhtiyar Ashurmatov’s Bukhoro packs 22,700 into Bukhara Arena in Joma, and Numon Hasanov’s Kokand 1912 outfits 10,500 Adidas fans at Kokand Stadium. In Bekabad, Andrey Shipilov’s Metallurg commands 15,000 at Metallurg Stadium in Adidas, while Ruziqul Berdiyev’s Nasaf fills Qarshi Markazi’s 16,000 in the same brand. Andrei Kanchelskis guides Navbahor from Namangan’s 22,000-seat Namangan Markazi Stadium in Adidas, and Shota Arveladze’s record-holding Pakhtakor fills the league’s largest ground, 35,000-seat Pakhtakor Stadium, also in Adidas. Sergey Lushan’s Sogdiana of Jizzakh wears Nike while hosting 11,650 at Soghdiana Stadium; Hamidjon Aktamov’s Qizilqum outfits 12,500 Adidas supporters at Zarafshan’s Progress Stadium. Davron Fayziev’s Dinamo Samarqand plays in Joma before 13,800 at Dinamo Stadium, and Andrei Miklyaev’s Surkhon, the league’s smallest, welcomes 6,000 to Termez’s Alpamish stadium in Adidas.
