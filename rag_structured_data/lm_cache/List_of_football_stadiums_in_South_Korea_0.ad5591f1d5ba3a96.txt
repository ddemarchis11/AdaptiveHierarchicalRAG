Seoul’s Olympic Stadium, seating 69,950 since 1984, hosts Seoul E-Land FC, while across the capital the 66,704-seat Seoul World Cup Stadium, opened in 2001, is home to FC Seoul. Daegu Stadium, also from 2001, holds 66,422 spectators, and Busan Asiad Main Stadium, built the same year, accommodates 53,769 fans for Busan IPark. Incheon Munhak Stadium, seating 52,179 since 2001, stands without a listed tenant, as does the 44,102-seat Ulsan Munsu Football Stadium, yet Ulsan Hyundai FC plays there. Suwon Samsung Bluewings occupy Suwon World Cup Stadium, built in 2001 for 43,959, while Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors FC fill Jeonju World Cup Stadium, opened the same year with room for 42,477. Goyang Stadium, completed in 2003, seats 41,311, and Daejeon Hana Citizen FC play in the 40,535-seat Daejeon World Cup Stadium, both from 2001. Gwangju FC call the 40,245-seat Gwangju World Cup Stadium home, Jeju United FC play in the 35,657-seat Jeju World Cup Stadium in Seogwipo, and Hwaseong FC occupy the 35,265-seat Hwaseong Stadium, built in 2011. Ansan Greeners FC use the 35,000-seat Ansan Wa~ Stadium from 2007, matching the capacity of 1984’s Gumi Civic Stadium and 2002’s Uijeongbu Stadium, neither listing a tenant. Bucheon FC 1995 play in the 34,456-seat Bucheon Stadium, also from 2001, while Duryu Park Stadium, dating to 1977, holds 32,500 in Daegu, Gimcheon Stadium seats 32,000 since 2000, and Incheon Asiad Main Stadium, opened in 2014, provides 30,000 places.
