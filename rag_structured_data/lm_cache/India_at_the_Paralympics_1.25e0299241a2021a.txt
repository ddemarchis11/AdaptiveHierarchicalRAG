Murlikant Petkar captured India’s first Paralympic gold in 1972 Heidelberg by winning the men’s 50 m freestyle 3 in swimming. Twelve years later, at the 1984 Stoke Mandeville/New York Games, Bhimrao Kesarkar took silver in the men’s javelin L6, while Joginder Singh Bedi claimed silver in the men’s shot put L6 and added bronze in both the men’s javelin L6 and discus throw L6. Devendra Jhajharia became India’s second gold medallist by triumphing in the men’s javelin F44/46 at the 2004 Athens Games, the same year Rajinder Singh Rahelu earned bronze in the men’s 56 kg powerlifting. In 2012 London, Girisha Nagarajegowda leapt to silver in the men’s high jump F42. At the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Games, Mariyappan Thangavelu soared to gold in the men’s high jump F42, Devendra Jhajharia repeated his Athens feat by again striking gold in the men’s javelin F46, Deepa Malik secured silver in the women’s shot put F53, and Varun Singh Bhati rounded out the podium with bronze in the men’s high jump F42.
