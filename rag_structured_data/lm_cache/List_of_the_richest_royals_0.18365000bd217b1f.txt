Thailand’s late King Bhumibol Adulyadej headed the 2011 Forbes roll with $43.8 billion drawn from Crown Property Bureau investments, while Dubai Sheikh Ahmed bin Saeed Al Maktoum followed at $31.7 billion through chairmanship of Emirates Group, Dubai World, Noor Bank and associated insurers. Brunei’s Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah claimed third place with $20 billion in oil-and-gas proceeds, and Saudi Arabia’s King Abdullah bin Abdul Aziz Al Saud, who died in 2015, held $18 billion from petroleum. Abu Dhabi’s President and Emir Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan reached $15 billion via the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority, Morocco’s King Mohammed VI held $5.7 billion in SNI and Siger Holdings, and Dubai ruler Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum counted $4.5 billion from Dubai Holding and the same sovereign fund. Liechtenstein’s Prince Hans Adam II commanded $3.5 billion through his family foundation, Qatar’s Emir Hamad bin Khalifa Al Thani—who abdicated in 2013—possessed $2.4 billion from assorted enterprises, and Monaco’s Prince Albert II rounded out the billion-dollar tier with $1 billion rooted in casinos and the Société des bains de mer. France-based Aga Khan IV, Karim al-Hussayni, held $800 million via the Aga Khan Development Network, Oman’s late Sultan Qaboos bin Said al Said had $700 million, Britain’s Queen Elizabeth II $450 million in property and jewels, Kuwait’s Emir Sabah Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah $350 million, the Netherlands’ Queen Beatrix—who abdicated in 2013—$200 million in Royal Dutch Shell stock, and Eswatini’s King Mswati III $100 million.
