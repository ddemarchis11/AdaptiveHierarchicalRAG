Juan Pablo Montoya planted his Williams-BMW on pole with a 1:38.117 lap, only 0.019 s ahead of team-mate Ralf Schumacher. Mika Häkkinen’s McLaren-Mercedes slotted third at 1:38.811, followed by Michael Schumacher’s Ferrari a further 0.13 s back. David Coulthard made it two silver arrows in the top five, 1.457 s off the pace, while Rubens Barrichello’s second Ferrari was sixth. Sauber’s Nick Heidfeld and rookie Kimi Räikkönen lined up seventh and eighth, Pedro de la Rosa’s Jaguar and Jarno Trulli’s Jordan completing the top ten. Eddie Irvine edged Jacques Villeneuve’s BAR by 0.066 s for 11th, with Olivier Panis a further 0.173 s behind in the sister BAR. Jean Alesi’s Prost, Ricardo Zonta’s Jordan, Luciano Burti’s Prost and the Benetton pair of Giancarlo Fisichella and Jenson Button filled rows 14-18, all within 0.7 s. Arrows teammates Enrique Bernoldi and Jos Verstappen rounded out the grid, Bernoldi 0.202 s ahead of Verstappen but 3.551 s adrift of Montoya’s benchmark.
