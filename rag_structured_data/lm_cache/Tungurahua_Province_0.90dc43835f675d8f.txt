Ambato, the provincial capital, dominates Tungurahua with 387,282 inhabitants spread across 1,009 km². Baños de Agua Santa, better known simply as Baños, holds 19,112 residents within 1,065 km². Cevallos, the smallest canton at only 19 km², shelters 8,873 people, while neighboring Mocha covers 86 km² for its 8,371 inhabitants. Patate’s 16,771 residents occupy 315 km², and Pelileo (San Pedro de Pelileo) rises to 58,988 people across 202 km². Píllaro (Santiago de Píllaro) contains 44,925 inhabitants within 443 km², Quero (Santiago de Quero) has 23,187 residents in 173 km², and Tisaleo’s 14,525 citizens live on 59 km².
