Alabaster’s US Gypsum quarry at the corner of Benson and Turtle roads became a Michigan State Historic Site on 8 August 1963, while the Alabaster Historic District—bounded by Lake Huron, Gypsum, Keystone, and Rempert roads—earned both state recognition and National Register status on 16 December 1977. Oscoda claims the Edward A. Brackenridge House at 218 West Dwight Street, listed 26 November 1985; the Cooke Hydroelectric Plant at 1201 Cooke Dam Road, listed 2 August 1996 and also on the National Register; the Dock Reserve informational site on the beach at the foot of Michigan, River Road, Dwight and Park streets, listed 21 July 1988; and the Green Pack House at 5014 N US-23, listed 27 January 1983. Au Sable’s Louis Chevalier Claim Informational Site stands at 295 Harbor Street, listed 29 July 1980. Glennie’s Five Channels Dam Archeological District, 6051 State Route 65, joined the state register and the National Register on 13 March 2002. East Tawas hosts the Ladies Literary Club Building at 316 Newman Street, listed 5 April 1974, and the Lumbering on the Huron Shore informational designation within Tawas Point State Park, dating to 19 July 1956. Oscoda Township’s Lumberman’s Memorial at 5401 Monument Road was listed 19 February 1958, while Grant Township’s Pioneer Township Cemetery at 4252 Old State Road followed on 1 September 1963. Tawas Point Light Station on Tawas Point Road, near East Tawas, gained dual state and National Register recognition on 23 April 1971.
