George Thomson won Nanaimo on 3 January 1887 after W. Raybould died 3 December 1886. Henry Fry took Cowichan on 5 May 1887 following William Smithe’s death 28 March 1887. Thomas Basil Humphreys was elected in Comox on 30 December 1887 after A. M. Stenhouse resigned to join the LDS Church. Simeon Duck entered for Victoria City on 25 January 1888 when E. G. Prior stepped down to contest a federal by-election. James Tolmie secured Victoria on 30 June 1888 after R. F. John became warden of the provincial gaol. Ithiel Blake Nason won Cariboo on 26 November 1888 when R. McLeese left to pursue a federal by-election. Andrew Haslam took Nanaimo on 14 June 1889 after Robert Dunsmuir died 12 April 1889. Alfred Wellington Smith was chosen in Lillooet on 21 September 1889 following premier Alexander Edmund Batson Davie’s death 1 August 1889. Thomas Cunningham entered for New Westminster City on 25 November 1889 after H. N. Bole resigned to join the County Court of British Columbia.
