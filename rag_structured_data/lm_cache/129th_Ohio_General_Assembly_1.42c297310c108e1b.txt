Craig Newbold, a Republican from Columbiana elected in 2010, represented District 1 until term-limited in 2018, while fellow 2010 GOP newcomers Andrew Brenner of Powell and Ron Amstutz of Wooster, the latter first elected in 2008, served Districts 2 and 3 with term limits of 2018 and 2016 respectively. Matt Huffman of Lima and Gerald Stebelton of Lancaster, both Republicans first elected in 2006, faced term limits in 2014 for Districts 4 and 5, as did Randy Gardner, the Bowling Green Republican appointed in 2008 for District 6. Democrats Kenny Yuko of Richmond Heights, first elected in 2004, and Timothy J. DeGeeter of Parma, appointed in 2003, were term-limited in 2012 for Districts 7 and 15, while Armond Budish of Beachwood, Barbara Boyd of Cleveland Heights, Sandra Williams of Cleveland, and Michael Foley of Cleveland, all Democrats first elected or appointed in 2006, faced 2014 term limits for Districts 8, 9, 11, and 14. Bill Patmon of Cleveland, John E. Barnes Jr. of Cleveland, and Nickie Antonio of Lakewood, all Democrats elected in 2010, were set for 2018 term limits in Districts 10, 12, and 13. Republicans Nan Baker of Westlake, elected in 2008, and Marlene Anielski of Walton Hills, Mike Dovilla of Berea, and Anne Gonzales of Westerville, all elected in 2010, represented Districts 16, 17, 18, and 19 with term limits of 2016 or 2018, while Democrat Nancy Garland of New Albany, elected in 2008, served District 20 until term-limited in 2016.
