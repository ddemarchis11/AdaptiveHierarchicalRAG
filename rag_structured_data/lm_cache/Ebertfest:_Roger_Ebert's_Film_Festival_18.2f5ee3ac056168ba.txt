Rolf de Heer’s 1998 drama Dance Me to My Song, starring Heather Rose, John Brumpton and Joey Kennedy, screened with Rose herself answering questions afterward. David D. Williams brought his 1997 film Thirteen, featuring Hermine Douglas, Brandon Flynn Wells and Alan Douglas, and was set to discuss it live. Nancy Savoca’s 1993 Household Saints, with Tracey Ullman, Vincent D’Onofrio and Lili Taylor, was accompanied by Savoca and producer-co-writer Richard Guay. Sergei Eisenstein’s 1925 Battleship Potemkin, headlined by Aleksandr Antonov, Vladimir Barsky and Grigori Aleksandrov, received a new Concrete Orchestra score performed live. Hirokazu Koreeda’s 1995 Maborosi showcased Makiko Esumi, Takashi Naitō and Tadanobu Asano. Kevin DiNovis introduced his 1998 Surrender Dorothy, in which he also appears alongside Peter Pryor and Jason Centeno. Dale Rosenbloom’s 1996 family tale Shiloh, starring Michael Moriarty, Scott Wilson and Blake Heron, brought Rosenbloom, Wilson and producer Carl Borack to the post-screening discussion. Jan Troell’s 1996 Hamsun presented Max von Sydow and Ghita Nørby. Éric Rohmer’s 1998 Autumn Tale united Béatrice Romand, Marie Rivière and Alain Libolt. Finally, Steven Lisberger’s 1982 Tron, with Jeff Bridges, Bruce Boxleitner and David Warner, closed the lineup as Lisberger and Warner faced the audience for a Q&A.
