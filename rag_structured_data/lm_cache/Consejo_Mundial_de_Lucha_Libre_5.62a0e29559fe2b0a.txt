Ricky Marvin was the final CMLL Japan Super Lightweight champion, holding the belt from its 27 February 1999 creation until the title vanished on 6 August 2000, while the CMLL Japan Tag Team straps lasted barely five months, ending 7 July 1999 with Masato Yakushiji and Naohiro Hoshikawa as last champions, and the CMLL Japan Women’s crown disappeared on 13 February 2000 with Chikako Shiratori as its last holder. Místico carried the LLA Azteca Championship from its birth 19 December 2009 until it was abandoned 12 May 2013. Blue Panther was the last Mexican National Middleweight champion, reigning from 1933 until the belt was dropped in 1992, and Los Destructores—Tony Arce and Vulano—were the final 1957-created Mexican National Tag Team champions before that title ended in 1993, whereas Mascarita Dorada held the Mexican National Mini-Estrella title from its 8 January 1993 debut until it was retired 5 November 2004. El Faraón’s NWA Intercontinental Heavyweight reign lasted only one month, October to November 1990. El Texano Jr, Averno, and Mephisto were the last holders of the 1952-born NWA World Light Heavyweight, 1939-born NWA World Middleweight, and 1946-born NWA World Welterweight championships respectively, all three belts ceasing on 12 August 2012. Pluma Negra was the final Occidente Lightweight champion, the 1985-created title ending in 2006, and La Parka held the IWC World Heavyweight title from its September 1995 creation until it was gone in July 1996.
