Ammadelle, the 1859 Italianate villa Calvert Vaux designed in Oxford, Lafayette County, gained National Historic Landmark status on 30 May 1974. On 14 September 1993 the Anna Site, a Plaquemine culture archaeological location in Natchez, Adams County, was likewise designated. Arlington and Auburn, two early historic Natchez homes, joined the register on 30 May 1974, as did the unusual dual-function Commercial Bank and Banker’s House and the House on Ellicott’s Hill. Beauvoir, Jefferson Davis’s post-war Biloxi home and library in Harrison County, became an NHL on 7 November 1993, survived Hurricane Katrina, and was restored by 2008. The Champion Hill Battlefield near Bolton, Hinds County, designated 5 May 1977, marks the Civil War turning point, while the Siege and Battle of Corinth Sites, honored 6 May 1991, straddle Alcorn County, Mississippi, and Hardeman County, Tennessee. Dancing Rabbit Creek Treaty Site in Noxubee County, where Choctaw gathered before their 1830 removal, was listed 19 June 1996. Dunleith, an 1855 Natchez mansion, Emerald Mound Site in Stanton, Grand Village of the Natchez, and Holly Bluff Site in Yazoo County all commemorate Plaquemine culture. Medgar Evers’s Jackson home, designated 16 February 2017, William Faulkner’s Oxford mansion, honored 23 May 1968, Fort St. Pierre Site near Vicksburg, the 9000–7000 BC Hester Site in Monroe County, Highland Park Dentzel Carousel in Meridian, and Jaketown Site near Belzoni complete Mississippi’s NHL roster.
