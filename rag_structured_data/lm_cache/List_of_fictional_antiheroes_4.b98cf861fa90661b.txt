Batman, created by Bill Finger and Bob Kane, debuted in Detective Comics #27 in May 1939 as a DC Comics antihero, followed by fellow Gothamite Catwoman in Batman #1 during 1940. Marvel’s irreverent Deadpool first cracked wise in The New Mutants #98 in February 1991, while the Hulk smashed onto newsstands in May 1962 in The Incredible Hulk #1, and Iron Man rocketed from Tales of Suspense #39 in March 1963, both from Stan Lee’s stable. Indian vigilante Doga appeared in 1993’s Curfew, crafted by Sanjay Gupta, Tarun Kumar Wahi and artist Manu, whereas Robert Crumb’s counter-culture feline Fritz the Cat dates back to 1959. Jason Todd, resurrected as the lethal Red Hood, returned in Batman #635 in 2005, and Jhonen Vasquez’s homicidal Johnny C. began his spree in Johnny the Homicidal Maniac #1 in August 1995, both under DC’s banner. Alan Moore, Stephen R. Bissette and John Totleben introduced chain-smoking mystic John Constantine in The Saga of the Swamp Thing #37 in June 1985. Garth Ennis and Steve Dillon launched preacher Jesse Custer in Preacher #1 in April 1995. Magneto, created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, first menaced the X-Men in The X-Men #1 in September 1963. Frank Miller’s Marv arrived in The Hard Goodbye in April 1991, with Dwight following in A Dame to Kill For in November 1993. Gerry Conway, John Romita Sr. and Ross Andru debuted the Punisher in The Amazing Spider-Man #129 in February 1974, while Todd McFarlane’s Spawn #1 appeared in May 1992. Bill Everett’s Sub-Mariner predates them all, surfacing in Motion Picture Funnies Weekly in April 1939. Alan Moore and David Lloyd’s masked anarchist V struck in Warrior #1 in March 1982, and Roy Thomas, Len Wein and John Romita Sr. unveiled Wolverine in The Incredible Hulk #180 in October 1974.
