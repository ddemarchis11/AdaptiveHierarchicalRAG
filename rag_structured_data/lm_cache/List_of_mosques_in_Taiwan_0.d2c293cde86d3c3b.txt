Taipei Grand Mosque, founded in 1947 and rebuilt in 1960 in Da’an, Taipei, is Taiwan’s oldest and largest mosque. Kaohsiung Mosque followed, begun in 1949, re-erected in 1951, and replaced again in 1992 in Lingya, Kaohsiung. Taipei Cultural Mosque started in 1950 and gained its present building in Zhongzheng, Taipei, in 1982. Taichung Mosque originated in 1951 and moved into its current Nantun, Taichung, structure in 1990. Longgang Mosque, launched in Zhongli, Taoyuan, in 1967, was reconstructed on the same site in 1989. Tainan Mosque opened in September 1996, also in Nantun, Taichung. At-Taqwa Mosque, known locally as Dayuan Mosque, was completed in Dayuan, Taoyuan, on 9 June 2013. An-Nur Tongkang Mosque began serving Donggang, Pingtung, on 18 February 2018, and Hualien Al-Falah Mosque welcomed worshippers in Hualien City starting 18 March 2018.
