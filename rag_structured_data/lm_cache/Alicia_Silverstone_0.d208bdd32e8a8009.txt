Alicia Silverstone first appeared onscreen in 1993 as Darian/Adrian Forrester in The Crush. During 1995 she juggled four roles: Trudy Wadd in Le Nouveau monde, Regina Harrison in Hideaway, the iconic Cher Horowitz in Clueless, and Jennifer in The Babysitter. She followed with Mary Giordano in 1996’s True Crime, then tackled Barbara Wilson/Batgirl in 1997’s Batman & Robin and Emily Hope in Excess Baggage the same year. After playing Eve Rustikoff in 1999’s Blast from the Past, she portrayed The Princess of France in 2000’s Love’s Labour’s Lost. The 2000s brought Natalie Nat Bevin in Global Heresy (2002), Sheila Rio in Scorched (2003), Heather Jasper Howe in Scooby-Doo 2: Monsters Unleashed (2004), Lynn in Beauty Shop (2005), Violet in Silence Becomes You (2005), Jack Starbright in Stormbreaker (2006), and a cameo as herself in 2008’s Tropic Thunder. She rounded out the period as Ms. Herman in The Art of Getting By (2011), Jill Emmet in Butter (2011), and Goody in 2012’s Vamps.
