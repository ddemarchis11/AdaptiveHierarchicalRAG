Olof Thörnell became the first Supreme Commander on 8 December 1939 and led the Army for 4 years and 114 days until 31 March 1944, when Helge Jung took over, extending Army leadership through 1 April 1944 to 31 March 1951, a tenure of 6 years and 364 days. Nils Swedlund succeeded him on 1 April 1951 and remained until 30 September 1961, serving 10 years and 182 days in the Army. Torsten Rapp then became the first Air Force officer to hold the post, serving from 1 October 1961 to 30 September 1970, nearly nine years. Stig Synnergren returned leadership to the Army from 1 October 1970 to 30 September 1978, followed by Lennart Ljung who held the same Army role from 1 October 1978 to 30 September 1986. Bengt Gustafsson continued the Army streak from 1 October 1986 until 30 June 1994, totaling 7 years and 272 days. Owe Wiktorin, another Air Force officer, served from 1 July 1994 to 30 June 2000. Johan Hederstedt led the Army from 1 July 2000 to 31 December 2003. Håkan Syrén, from the Navy’s Amphibious Corps, held office from 1 January 2004 to 24 March 2009. Sverker Göranson returned command to the Army from 25 March 2009 to 30 September 2015, when Micael Bydén, Air Force, took over and remains incumbent.
