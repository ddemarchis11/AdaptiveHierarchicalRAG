All Saints Episcopal Church, a National Register property, stands at 252 Grand Street on Saugatuck’s southwest corner of Hoffman Street and was listed as a Michigan State Historic Site on April 24, 1981. Allegan County’s informational marker at 125 Chestnut Street in Allegan dates to November 14, 1961, while the Allegan Road Commemorative Designation in Saugatuck’s River Bluff Park was recognized on February 17, 1994. Castle Park Lodge, 6700 Bryant Avenue within Castle Park in Holland, joined the rolls on September 10, 1979, the same year the Church of the Good Shepherd at 101 Walnut Street in Allegan had already been listed since July 26, 1973. The Clipson Brewery Ice House, also known as Twin Gables Hotel, 900 Lake Street in Saugatuck, was designated on April 28, 1987, followed by the John Crispe House at 404 East Bridge Street in Plainwell on April 25, 1988, and the Delano House at 302 Cutler Street in Allegan on July 26, 1973. Douglas Union School, another National Register entry at 130 Center Street in Douglas, was listed on December 20, 1990, while Dutcher Lodge at 86 Center Street in Douglas was recognized on January 13, 1985. Ebenezer Reformed Church’s informational marker at 5166 Ottogan Street in Holland dates to February 27, 1970, and the Dorr E. Felt Mansion, 6597 138th Avenue in Holland, entered both the state and national registers on January 17, 2006. The Fifty-Seventh Street Bridge over the Kalamazoo River at 57th Street in New Richmond was listed on April 4, 1978, and the First Congregational Church at 296 Hoffman in Saugatuck on March 21, 1991. The Francis Metallic Surfboat at 130 W. Center Street in Douglas was designated on January 17, 2016, the Ganges Fractional District No. 1 School House at 6292 124th Avenue in Fennville on July 26, 1978, and the Dr. Asa Goodrich House at 112 Center Street in Douglas on December 5, 1986. Graafschap Christian Reformed Church, located on A-621 (48th Avenue) between Graafschap Road and Washington Avenue in Holland, was listed on December 14, 1976, the Hacklander Site (20AE78) at 130th Avenue and 63rd Street in Saugatuck Township on April 14, 1972, and the Henika Ladies’ Library at 149 South Main Street in Wayland on November 26, 1985.
