Ed O’Neill debuted onscreen in 1980 as Detective Schreiber in Cruising and as Terry in The Dogs of War, then spent 1989 as Sergeant Brannigan in K-9 and George Denver in Disorganized Crime. 1990 found him playing Lieutenant Amos in The Adventures of Ford Fairlane and Wilbur Meany in Sibling Rivalry, followed by Dutch Dooley in 1991’s Dutch. He returned as Glen in both 1992’s Wayne’s World and 1993’s Wayne’s World 2, then portrayed Ed in 1994’s Blue Chips and Kevin O’Shea in Little Giants that same year. After embodying Bill Dellinger in 1997’s Prefontaine and an FBI Team Leader in The Spanish Prisoner, he took on Detective Paulie Sellitto in 1999’s The Bone Collector. The new millennium saw him as Dick Simmons in Lucky Numbers, Norman Pinkney in Nobody’s Baby, Burch in Spartan, Congressman Cardone in Steel Valley, a Hollywood Producer in Redbelt, and Chief Justice Renato Corona in Lost Masterpieces of Pornography.
