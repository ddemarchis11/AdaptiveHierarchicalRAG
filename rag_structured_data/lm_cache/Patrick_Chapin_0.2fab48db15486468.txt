Patrick Chapin placed eighth at the 1996–97 Pro Tour in New York, playing Booster Draft from 30 May to 1 June 1997, and two seasons later took sixth at the Los Angeles Pro Tour that ran 26–28 February 1999 under Rochester Draft rules. In May 2002 he finished second at the Standard-format Grand Prix Milwaukee, and in December 2007 repeated that runner-up finish at the Special-format World Championship back in New York. Returning to the Pro Tour, he earned sixth place at the Standard event in Paris held 10–13 February 2011, then secured third at Grand Prix Pittsburgh on 27–28 August 2011 and second at Grand Prix Orlando 14–15 January 2012. Chapin reached the pinnacle of his career by winning the Block Constructed Pro Tour Atlanta 16–18 May 2014, and subsequently placed second at the Special-format World Championship in Nice, 2–7 December 2014. He closed this competitive stretch with sixth place at Grand Prix Indianapolis, 31 October–1 November 2015, again in Standard.
