Chi Cheng opened the 1970s by clocking 12.93 in Munich, then Karin Balzer sliced the mark to 12.60 in East Berlin the next year. Anneliese Ehrhardt dominated the next three seasons, improving to 12.59 in Munich 1972, defending with 12.68 in Dresden 1973 and 12.66 in Rome 1974. Bożena Świerczyńska took 1975 with 12.91 in Zielona Góra before Grazyna Rabsztyn began her Polish reign: 12.69 in Bydgoszcz 1976, a breakthrough 12.48 in Fürth 1978, and an equal 12.48 in Warsaw 1979. Rabsztyn lowered the bar to 12.36 in Warsaw 1980, but Tatyana Anisimova claimed 1981 with 12.68 in Tbilisi. Yordanka Donkova then seized control, first with 12.44 in Sofia 1982, then the landmark 12.26 in Ljubljana 1986 and finally 12.21 in Stara Zagora 1988; sandwiched between her feats, Bettine Jahn managed 12.42 in Berlin 1983, Lucyna Kalek 12.43 in Hannover 1984, and Ginka Zagorcheva 12.42 in Sofia 1985 and 12.25 in Dráma 1987. Cornelia Oschkenat closed the decade at 12.60 in Barcelona 1989.
