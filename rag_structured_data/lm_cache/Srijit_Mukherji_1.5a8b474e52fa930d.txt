In 2009 Srijit Mukherji wrote lyrics for Abhijeet Guha and Sudeshna Roy’s Cross Connection, scored by Neel Dutt and starring Abir Chatterjee, Payel Sarkar and Rimjhim Mitra, and again for Anjan Dutt’s Madly Bangalee with the same composer alongside Biswajit Chakraborty, Chandan Sen and Supriya Choudhury. The next year he provided lyrics for Ravi Kinagi’s Josh, composed by Jeet Ganguly and starring Jeet and Srabanti, while Prasenjit Mukherjee and Priyo Chattapadhyay joined him on lyrics for Raj Chakraborty’s Le Chakka, composed by Indradeep Dasgupta and featuring Dev and Payel Sarkar. In 2011 he contributed lyrics to Aparna Sen’s Iti Mrinalini alongside Rabindranath Tagore and Sunil Gangopadhyay, with Debojyoti Mishra composing for Aparna Sen, Konkona Sen Sharma and Rajat Kapoor, and also wrote lyrics for Ghente Ghaw, acted in Flop-e, and saw Srijato’s lyrics set to Indradeep Dasgupta’s score in Anindo Banerjee’s Chaplin with Rudranil Ghosh, Soham Maitra and Mir Afsar Ali. 2012 brought lyrics for Anjan Dutt’s Dutta Vs Dutta, again composed by Neel Dutt and starring Anjan Dutt, Ronodeep Bose, Arpita Chatterjee and Roopa Ganguly, and for Abhijeet Guha and Sudeshna Roy’s Bapi Bari Jaa with Jeet Ganguly and featuring Arjun and Mimi Chakraborty, while Kaushik Ganguly’s Shabdo featured Ritwik Chakraborty and Raima Sen. Rituparno Ghosh co-wrote lyrics with him for Mainak Bhaumik’s 2013 Maach Mishti & More, composed by Neel Dutt and starring Parambrata Chatterjee, Raima Sen, Parno Mittra and Swastika Mukherjee, and in 2018 he wrote lyrics for Pratim D Gupta’s Ahare Mon, scored by Neel Dutt and featuring Paoli Dam, Ritwick Chakraborty, Parno Mittra and Adil Hussain.
