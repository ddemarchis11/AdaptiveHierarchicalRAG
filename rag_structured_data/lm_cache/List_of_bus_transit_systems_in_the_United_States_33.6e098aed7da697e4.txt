ACTA links Ashe County’s seat, Jefferson, while neighboring Watauga County relies on AppalCART for Boone. Buncombe County’s Asheville Transit System serves Asheville itself, and Durham County is covered twice: the Bull City Connector and Duke Buses both operate within Durham, joined by the Durham Area Transit Authority (DATA). Wake County fields two systems—Capital Area Transit (CAT) for Raleigh and Cary Transit (C-Tran) for Cary—while Chapel Hill Transit unites Chapel Hill and Carrboro. Mecklenburg County’s Charlotte Area Transit System (CATS) centers on Charlotte; Chatham Transit Network radiates from Siler City in Chatham County, and Cleveland County Transit works county-wide without naming a single hub. Craven Area Rural Transit System (CARTS) connects New Bern, Cumberland County’s FAST links Fayetteville with Spring Lake, and Gastonia Transit stays within Gaston County’s Gastonia. GATEWAY serves Wayne County from Goldsboro, GTA covers Guilford County’s Greensboro, and Hi tran does the same for High Point. Greenway Public Transportation spans Alexander, Burke, Caldwell, and Catawba counties, while ICATS focuses on Statesville in Iredell County.
