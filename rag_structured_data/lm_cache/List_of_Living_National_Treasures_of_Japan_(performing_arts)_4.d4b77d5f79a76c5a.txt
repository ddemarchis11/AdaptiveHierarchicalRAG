Reibo Aoki, born 1935, became a Living National Treasure in 1999 for shakuhachi, the same subcategory in which Hōzan Yamamoto II, two years his junior, was honored in 2002. Shōin Yamase, born 1932, and Fumiko Yonekawa II, born 1926, were recognized in 2001 and 2008 respectively for koto. Kisaburō Kineya XV, born 1923, and Tetsuo Miyada, born 1934, received their honors in 1997 and 1998 for nagauta vocals, while Masatarō Imafuji, born 1935, was designated in 2013 for nagauta shamisen and Kisaku Katada III, also born 1935, in 1999 for nagauta narimono. Komanosuke Takemoto, born 1935, was honored in 1989 for gidayūbushi vocals, and Tomoji Tsuruzawa, born 1913, in 1998 for gidayūbushi shamisen. Shibun Uji VII, born 1933, and Bunchō Uji, born 1935, received recognition in 1999 and 2001 for icchūbushi vocals and shamisen respectively. Setsuko Yamabiko, born 1920, was designated in 1994 for katōbushi vocals, and Senko Yamabiko, born 1946, in 2009 for katōbushi shamisen. Senroku Miyazono II, born 1944, became a Living National Treasure in 2007 for miyazonobushi vocals. Wakasanojō Tsuruga 11, born 1938, and Nakasaburō Shinnai, born 1940, were both honored in 2001 for shinnaibushi vocals and shamisen. Ichihadayū Tokiwazu, born 1930, received the designation in 1995 for tokiwazubushi vocals, and Eiju Tokiwazu, born 1929, in 1992 for tokiwazubushi shamisen. Eijudayū Kiyomoto, born 1935, was recognized in 2003 for kiyomotobushi vocals.
