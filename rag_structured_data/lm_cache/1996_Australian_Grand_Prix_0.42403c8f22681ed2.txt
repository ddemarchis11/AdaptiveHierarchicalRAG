Jacques Villeneuve planted his Williams-Renault on pole with a 1:32.371 lap, edging teammate Damon Hill by 0.138 s. Eddie Irvine’s Ferrari slotted into third, a further 0.380 s adrift, while Michael Schumacher’s sister Ferrari lined up fourth, 0.754 s off the pace. Mika Häkkinen’s McLaren-Mercedes headed the next pack in fifth, followed by the Benetton-Renault duo of Jean Alesi and Gerhard Berger in sixth and seventh. Rubens Barrichello’s Jordan-Peugeot, Heinz-Harald Frentzen’s Sauber-Ford and Mika Salo’s Tyrrell-Yamaha completed the top ten, each separated by fractions. Olivier Panis (Ligier-Mugen-Honda), Jos Verstappen (Footwork-Hart) and David Coulthard (McLaren-Mercedes) filled rows eleven to thirteen within 0.022 s. Johnny Herbert’s Sauber-Ford, Ukyo Katayama’s Tyrrell-Yamaha and the Minardi-Ford pair of Giancarlo Fisichella and Pedro Lamy followed, trailed by Ricardo Rosset’s Footwork and Martin Brundle’s Jordan. Pedro Diniz brought up the rear of the 20-car Melbourne grid, 3.927 s behind Villeneuve’s benchmark.
