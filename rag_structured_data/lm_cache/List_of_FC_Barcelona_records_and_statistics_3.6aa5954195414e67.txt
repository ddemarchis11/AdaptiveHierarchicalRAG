Lionel Messi, the Argentine phenomenon who arrived in 2004, towers over every scorer in Barça’s history with 433 goals. Spain’s César netted 190 between 1942 and 1955, while today’s Uruguayan striker Luis Suárez has already struck 142 since 2014. Hungarian magician László Kubala amassed 131 from 1950 to 1961, and Cameroonian Samuel Eto’o hit 108 during his 2004-2009 reign. Spaniards Mariano Martín and Josep Escolà registered 97 and 93 respectively across the 1940s, Dutchman Patrick Kluivert claimed 90 between 1998 and 2004, fellow Spaniard Estanislao Basora 89 from 1946 to 1958, and Brazilian Rivaldo 86 from 1997 to 2002.
