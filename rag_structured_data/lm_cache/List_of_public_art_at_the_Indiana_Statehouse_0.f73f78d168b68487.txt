Enrico Vittori’s 1920 bronze Columbus rises 39 by 23 by 29 inches on granite, while Rudolph Schwarz and Marie Stewart’s 1906 bronze Lincoln relief stretches 65 by 35 inches. Donald De Lue’s 1959 Washington stands nine feet four inches tall and weighs 900 pounds. Eric Ernstberger’s 1991 Tulip to Life blooms in stainless steel, copper, and limestone, spanning fifteen by eighteen feet. The 2001 Indiana Law Enforcement and Firefighters Memorial spreads multiple bronze, granite, and limestone pieces across the plaza. Schwarz returned in 1907 to cast the ten-foot Morton monument in bronze and granite, and Frances Goodwin’s 1911 Robert Dale Owen Memorial measures 45.5 by 42.5 by 70 inches in bronze. Richard Henry Park’s 1890 Hendricks Monument rises 132 inches high on red granite, and John J. Szaton’s 1966 Coal Miner, eighty-five inches tall, stands on black granite. Herman Carl Mueller’s 1878 limestone Westward Journey presents four groupings and single figures twelve feet high. Daniel Edwards’s 1995 Workers’ Memorial rises in bronze on limestone, and David K. Rubins’s 1962 Young Abe Lincoln reaches 108 inches on granite. Thomas Dow Jones’s 1862 plaster Lincoln bust measures 33 by 25 by 18 inches, Henry Dexter’s 1860 Willard plaster bust is 9 by 11.5 by 23.5 inches, and Richard Peglow’s Benjamin Harrison bust lacks date and dimensions. An unknown sculptor carved the 47.5-inch marble Calvin Fletcher, Belle Kinney Scholz’s 1913 bronze Colonel Richard Owen stands 70 by 40 by 21 inches, James Paxton Voorhees modeled the 31-inch plaster Daniel W. Voorhees, David McLary cast the 1987 plaster George Rogers Clark, and Jean-Antoine Houdon’s 1932 plaster Washington bust, owned by the Indiana Historical Bureau, measures 25 by 18 by 18 inches.
