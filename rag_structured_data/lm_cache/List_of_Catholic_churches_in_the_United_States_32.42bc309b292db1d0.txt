The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, a Gothic Revival landmark at 125 Eagle Street in Albany, New York, was built in 1902 and listed on the NRHP in 1976. St. Stanislaus Church, a Late Gothic Revival structure at 50 Cornell Street in Amsterdam, New York, dates to 1897 and gained NRHP status in 1999. In Binghamton, the Late 19th & 20th Century Revival Saints Cyril and Methodius School stands at 144-146 Clinton Street, constructed in 1920 and NRHP-listed in 2007. Buffalo hosts several notable churches: the Lombard Romanesque Blessed Trinity Church at 317 LeRoy Ave, built in 1906 and NRHP-listed in 1979; the Gothic Revival St. Joseph Cathedral at 50 Franklin Street, dating to 1862; the Modern Movement St. Rose of Lima Church at 500 Parker Ave, NRHP-listed in 2017; the Gothic Revival St. Theresa's Church at 1970 Seneca St., also NRHP-listed in 2016; and the Romanesque Revival St. Thomas Aquinas Church at 432 Abbott Rd., NRHP-listed in 2017. Our Mother of Sorrows Church in Greece, New York, a Romanesque building from 1859, was NRHP-listed in 1989. The National shrine Our Lady of Victory Basilica in Lackawanna, New York, built in 1926 at 767 Ridge Road, serves as a popular pilgrimage site in the Diocese of Buffalo.
