Antonio R. Barceló, a Unionist, served as the first President of the Puerto Rico Senate from 1917 to 1929, working alongside pro-tempore presiders Eduardo Georgetti (1917-1921), Juan Hernández López (1921-1924), and Luis Sánchez Morales (1926-1929). Luis Sánchez Morales, a Republican, succeeded him from 1929 to 1933 with Celestino Iriarte Miró as pro-tempore. Rafael Martínez Nadal, also Republican, held the presidency from 1933 to 1941, first with Bolívar Pagán (1933-1940) and later Luis Padrón Rivera (1940-1941) as pro-tempore. Luis Muñoz Marín of the PPD served from 1941 to 1949, with Francisco M. Susoni Abreu (1941-1944) and Samuel R. Quiñones (1945-1949) as pro-tempore. Samuel R. Quiñones continued as president from 1949 to 1969 with Luis Negrón López as pro-tempore. Rafael Hernández Colón (1969-1973) and Juan J. Cancel Ríos (1973-1977), both PPD members, served consecutively with Juan J. Cancel Ríos and Miguel Hernández Agosto as their respective pro-tempores. Luis A. Ferré Aguayo of the PNP served from 1977 to 1981 with Manuel Ramos Barroso as pro-tempore, followed by Miguel Hernández Agosto (PPD) from 1981 to 1993 with Sergio Peña Clos (1981-1988) and Miguel Deynes Soto (1989-1993). Roberto Rexach Benítez (PNP) served from 1993 to 1997 with Nicolás Nogueras Cartagena (1993-1995) and Luisa Lebrón de Rivera (1995-1997). Charles A. Rodríguez Colón (PNP) held office from 1997 to 2001 with Aníbal Marrero Pérez (1997-2000) and Luz Z. Arce Ferrer (2000-2001). Antonio Fas Alzamora (PPD) served from 2001 to 2005 with Velda González de Modestti as pro-tempore. Kenneth D. McClintock Hernández (PNP) served from 2005 to 2009 with Orlando Parga Figueroa. Thomas Rivera Schatz first served from 2009 to 2013 with Margarita Nolasco Santiago, then returned in 2017 to serve until the present with Larry Seilhamer Rodríguez as pro-tempore. Eduardo Bhatia (PPD) served from 2013 to 2017 with José Luis Dalmau as pro-tempore.
