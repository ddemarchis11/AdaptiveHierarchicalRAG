The Caledonia Bridge, listed on 27 February 1997, carries an unnamed county road across the Goose River one mile west of the Minnesota line in Caledonia. Mayville’s Delchar Theater, added 14 November 1985, stands at 20 W. Main St., while the First National Bank next door at 22 W. Main joined the Register five days later; the Goose River Bank at 45 Main St. E. and the Lura Building at 29 W. Main were listed the same day as the theater. The Grinager Mercantile at 37 Main St. E. and the Mayville Historic District—roughly bounded by 3rd St. NE, 5th Ave. NE, Main St. E., and 2nd–3rd Ave. NE—both entered on 19–20 November 1985, as did the Grandins’ Mayville Farm District on 2 Brunsdale W. and the Great Northern Railway Depot on Front St., listed 5 October 1977. Hatton claims the Carl Ben Eielson House at 405 8th St., listed 11 April 1977, and the Andres O. Ness House at Oak Ave. and 6th St., added 15 July 1977. The First State Bank of Buxton, 423 Broadway St., was listed 14 February 1978. Out in the countryside, the Ellingson Farm District sits one mile north and 2.5 miles west of Hillsboro, while Lucken Farm lies north of Portland. The Norway Bridge, like the Caledonia Bridge, spans the Goose River six miles east and three miles south of Mayville, both bridges listed 27 February 1997. Hillsboro’s Amos and Lillie Plummer House at 306 W. Caledonia Ave. was added 4 January 1996, and the Traill County Courthouse at 114 W. Caledonia Ave. on 25 November 1980.
