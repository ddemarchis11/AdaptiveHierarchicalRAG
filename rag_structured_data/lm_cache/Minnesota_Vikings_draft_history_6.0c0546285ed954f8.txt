Clinton Jones, a Michigan State running back, opened Minnesota’s 1967 draft haul as the second overall pick, followed at eighth overall by fellow Spartan Gene Washington, the first of three wide receivers selected. Notre Dame defensive tackle Alan Page arrived at fifteenth overall, while Oregon State’s Bob Grim came off the board twenty-eighth. Missouri wideout Earl Denny was the sixty-first choice, Minnesota’s own Ken Last the 115th, and South Carolina cornerback Bobby Bryant the 167th. California tight end John Beasley (197), Holy Cross guard Bill Morris (219), Nebraska running back Pete Tatman (244), Washington State defensive tackle Bob Trygstad (271), and Saint John’s guard Fred Cremer (296) rounded out the middle rounds. Tulsa defensive back Charles Hardt was taken 323rd, Howard Payne linebacker Jimmy Hargrove 348th, Eastern New Mexico defensive back Jimmy Shea 375th, Virginia Union’s Gene Beard 400th, and North Dakota State’s Todd Vandal 427th, closing the seventeen-round class.
