Felix Loch captured luge men’s singles gold on 14 February, while David Möller took silver in the same event. Magdalena Neuner earned biathlon women’s pursuit gold on 16 February and added women’s mass-start gold on 21 February, having already claimed women’s sprint silver on 13 February. Tatjana Hüfner topped the women’s singles luge on 16 February. Maria Riesch dominated Alpine skiing, winning women’s super-combined gold on 18 February and slalom gold on 26 February. André Lange and Kevin Kuske piloted the two-man bobsleigh to victory on 21 February, just ahead of teammates Thomas Florschütz and Richard Adjei who secured silver. Evi Sachenbacher-Stehle and Claudia Nystad raced to cross-country women’s team-sprint gold on 22 February, the same day Tim Tscharnke and Axel Teichmann claimed men’s team-sprint silver. Viktoria Rebensburg struck giant-slalom gold on 25 February. Stephanie Beckert collected speed-skating silver in the women’s 3000 m on 14 February and again in the 5000 m on 24 February, then joined Daniela Anschütz-Thoms, Anni Friesinger-Postma and Katrin Mattscherodt to capture women’s team-pursuit gold on 27 February. Jenny Wolf sped to 500 m silver on 16 February. Kerstin Szymkowiak slid to skeleton women’s silver on 19 February. Tobias Angerer earned 30 km pursuit cross-country silver on 20 February. Michael Neumayer, Andreas Wank, Martin Schmitt and Michael Uhrmann soared to large-hill team ski-jumping silver on 22 February.
