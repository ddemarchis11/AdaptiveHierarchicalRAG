Kings of Leon’s Only by the Night opened 2009 at number one, holding the top spot through 13 January, 19 January, 26 January, 2 February and 9 February, then returned for 23 March, 30 March, 6 April and 13 April after Lily Allen’s It’s Not Me, It’s You interrupted for 16 and 23 February and Natalie Bassingthwaighte’s 1000 Stars led on 2 March, while U2’s No Line on the Horizon ruled 9 and 16 March. Allen reclaimed the summit on 20 April. Ronan Keating’s Songs for My Mother then dominated 27 April, 4 May, 11 May and 18 May, followed by Eminem’s Relapse on 25 May and 1 June. Eskimo Joe’s Inshalla topped 8 June, The Black Eyed Peas’ The E.N.D. 15 June, Hilltop Hoods’ State of the Art 22 and 29 June. Michael Jackson’s posthumous The Essential Michael Jackson reigned for seven straight weeks from 6 July to 17 August. Short Stack’s Stack Is the New Black peaked 24 August, The E.N.D. returned 30 August, Jimmy Barnes’ The Rhythm and the Blues led 7 and 14 September, Muse’s The Resistance 21 September, Pearl Jam’s Backspacer 28 September, Paramore’s Brand New Eyes 5 October, The E.N.D. again 12 October, and Michael Bublé’s Crazy Love closed the survey period atop the chart on 19, 26 October and 2 November.
