Yuriy Sedykh’s 86.74-metre heave in Stuttgart on 30 August 1986 still crowns the all-time list, while fellow Soviet Sergey Litvinov trails at 86.04 m from Dresden two months earlier. Vadim Devyatovskiy reached 84.90 m in Minsk on 21 July 2005, Koji Murofushi of Japan launched 84.86 m in Prague on 29 June 2003, and Belarusian Igor Astapkovich recorded 84.62 m in Seville on 6 June 1992. Ivan Tsikhan’s 84.51 m came in Grodno on 9 July 2008, Igor Nikulin’s 84.48 m in Lausanne on 12 July 1990, and Jüri Tamm’s 84.40 m in Banská Bystrica on 9 September 1984. Adrián Annus threw 84.19 m in Szombathely on 10 August 2003, Paweł Fajdek 83.93 m in Szczecin on 9 August 2015, and Tibor Gécsek 83.68 m in Zalaegerszeg on 19 September 1998. Andrey Abduvaliyev achieved 83.46 m in Sochi on 26 May 1990, Aleksey Zagornyi 83.43 m in Adler on 10 February 2002, Ralf Haber 83.40 m in Athens on 16 May 1988, and Szymon Ziółkowski 83.38 m in Edmonton on 5 August 2001. Olli-Pekka Karjalainen’s 83.30 m came in Lahti on 14 July 2004, Heinz Weis 83.04 m in Frankfurt on 29 June 1997, Balázs Kiss 83.00 m in Saint-Denis on 4 June 1998, Karsten Kobs 82.78 m in Dortmund on 26 June 1999, and Krisztián Pars 82.69 m in Zürich on 16 August 2014.
