Alavés play in Vitoria-Gasteiz at the 19,840-seat Mendizorrotza, while neighbours Athletic Bilbao occupy the 53,332-capacity San Mamés in Bilbao. Atlético Madrid have moved to the 68,000-seat Wanda Metropolitano in Madrid, where city rivals Real Madrid stage matches in the 81,044-seat Santiago Bernabéu. Barcelona’s vast 99,354-seat Camp Nou dominates the Catalan capital, mirrored across the region by Espanyol’s 40,000-seat RCDE Stadium in Cornellà de Llobregat. Celta Vigo host at the 29,000-seat Abanca-Balaídos in Vigo, and tiny Eibar’s Ipurua accommodates just 7,083 in the Basque hills. Getafe’s Coliseum Alfonso Pérez holds 17,000, Leganés pack 12,450 into Butarque, and Granada’s Nuevo Los Cármenes seats 19,336. Levante and Valencia share the city of Valencia: Levante at the 25,354-seat Ciutat de València, Valencia at the 50,000 Mestalla. Mallorca’s Son Moix in Palma provides 24,262 places, Osasuna’s El Sadar in Pamplona 17,286. Real Betis and Sevilla divide Seville between the 60,720-seat Benito Villamarín and the 43,883-seat Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán. Real Sociedad play in the 39,500-seat Anoeta in San Sebastián, Valladolid’s José Zorrilla offers 26,512, and Villarreal’s Estadio de la Cerámica welcomes 23,500.
