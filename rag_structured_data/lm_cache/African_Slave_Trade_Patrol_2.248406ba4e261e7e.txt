Between 1858 and 1860, U.S. Navy cruisers intercepted nine suspected slavers off Cuba. On 21 August 1858, Dolphin seized the Putnam in Cuban waters. Mohawk captured Cygnet on 18 November 1859 near Cuba, then Wildfire on 26 April 1860. Wyandotte took William on 9 May 1860 off Cuba, while Crusader captured Bogota on 23 May, W.R. Kibby on 23 July, and Joven Antonio on 14 August, all in Cuban waters. Closing the year, Mohawk detained Toccoa on 20 December and Mary J. Kimball on 21 December at Havana.
