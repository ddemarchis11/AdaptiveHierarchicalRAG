Robert Hall of Massachusetts won the 1975 Boston Marathon in 2:58:00 and repeated in 1977 with a 2:40:10 finish. After no official result in 1976, George Murray from Florida took 1978 in 2:26:57 and returned in 1985 to lower the clock to 1:45:34. Ken Archer of Ohio claimed 1979 in 2:38:59, while Curt Brinkman of Utah set 1:55:00 in 1980 and Jim Martinson of Washington 2:00:41 in 1981. California’s Jim Knaub began a streak, winning 1982 in 1:51:31, improving to 1:47:10 for his second title in 1983, adding a third in 1991 at 1:30:44, a fourth in 1992 at 1:26:28, and a fifth in 1993 at 1:22:17. Canada’s André Viger interrupted with victories in 1984 (2:05:20), 1986 (1:43:25), and 1987 (1:55:42). France’s Mustapha Badid triumphed in 1988 at 1:43:19 and again in 1990 at 1:29:53, while fellow Frenchman Philippe Couprie set 1:36:04 in 1989. Switzerland’s Heinz Frei closed the span in 1994 at 1:21:23.
