W. J. Adkins, who earned a 1938 M.A., founded and led Laredo Community College from 1947 to 1960. B W Aston collected three Texas Tech degrees—B.S. 1962, M.S. 1964, Ph.D. 1972—and became a noted historian, as did Paul H. Carlson after completing his 1973 Ph.D. and specializing in Texas and the American West. Jo Carr, a 1947 graduate, served as a United Methodist minister, while Margaret Formby, class of 1950, established the National Cowgirl Museum and Hall of Fame. Sandra S. Harper, a 1974 alumna, presides over Our Lady of the Lake College in Louisiana, and Michael Hinojosa, who finished in 1979, has twice led Dallas ISD (2005-2011 and since 2015) after a stint at Cobb County. Walter B. Huffman, stacking a 1967 B.A., 1968 M.Ed., and 1977 J.D., directed Texas Tech’s law school and served as a U.S. Army Judge Advocate General. Billy Mac Jones governed Southwest Texas State and Memphis State universities, William N. Ellis became the nineteenth president of Howard Payne, and Barry B. Thompson channeled his Texas Tech studies into the chancellorship of the Texas A&M System. David J. Schmidly, B.A. 1966 and M.S. 1968, later presided over Oklahoma State, Texas Tech, and the University of New Mexico. Historians Willard Hughes Rollings (Ph.D. 1983) and Ernest Wallace (1935) illuminate Native American and regional history, while Keith E. Whitfield oversees academic affairs at Wayne State.
